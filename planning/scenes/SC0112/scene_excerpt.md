INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — ANTECHAMBER CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS

She is standing.

The corridor is quiet in a way it has not been since she entered the building — the machine gone, the fight over, the ambient that remains is the building's own: climate control, structure, the unremarkable sounds of a compound that is large and continues to be large regardless of what has happened in one of its corridors.

Her left side is a categorical fact. She is not managing it — she is not not-managing it. It is simply the current condition of the left side of her body, and she is standing, and the building is quiet.

Roman is on the floor to her left. She does not look at him. She confirmed his breathing from standing before she moved. That confirmation has not changed. She does not need to update it.

She gives herself three seconds.

This is a deliberate allocation — not collapse, not recovery, not the functional count she ran before standing. She is not counting toward anything. She is standing in a corridor in the VELTAIN compound with a rib injury and an exertion debt she will be paying for the next three days, and she is giving herself three seconds in which she has already survived and has not yet begun the next thing. Three seconds in which both of those things are true at once.

She does not think about the machine.

She does not think about Roman.

She does not think about the credential reader still displaying ADMINISTRATOR AUTHORIZATION: ACTIVE on its small amber display three meters to her right, because the display no longer matters and her attention is no longer required there.

She thinks about Jin.

Not the plan — not the compound level, the route, the remaining minutes on the lockdown clock. Not the operational shape of the next forty minutes. She thinks about Jin the way she has been thinking about Jin since before any of this had a shape: the specific geography of him. The scar at the left ear. The way he counts things he is not supposed to be counting. The numbers that are his.

Three seconds.

At the end of the three seconds she does not mark the moment. There is nothing to mark. The three seconds were hers and they are complete and the next thing is to move.

She turns toward the antechamber outer door.

She does not look back at the corridor. She does not look back at Roman on the floor or the credential reader or the server core door with the card still in its lock. She is looking at the outer door and at what is past it.

She moves.


INT. JOINT LEGAL OPERATIONS CENTER — EXTERIOR JURISDICTION — DAY

The room is not dramatic. It is a room with six workstations, three monitors at the far wall showing simultaneous filing queues across three separate jurisdictions, and the specific ambient of a space where the work has been continuous for eighteen hours and the people doing it have not eaten recently.

Okafor is at the center station. She has been here for eleven of the eighteen hours — the other seven she was in transit from the prior jurisdiction, running the filing coordination from a secure line in a car. She does not appear to have noticed the difference.

Chen Mei crosses the room. She sets a sealed data envelope on the workstation — the encrypted transfer of Dimitri's financial ledger, the working copy Chen Mei has been carrying for six weeks since it came out of the intelligence chain. Okafor does not look up. She opens the transfer verification, confirms the hash against the registered source document from the original intelligence package, and enters the authorization.

The ledger is in the system.

MULTI-JURISDICTION SEIZURE ORDER: SUBMISSION CONFIRMED
FILING JURISDICTIONS: PRAXIS AUTHORITY / OUTER VALE COMPACT / TERTIAN REGULATORY BOARD
ASSET CATEGORIES: BROADCAST INFRASTRUCTURE / FINANCIAL INSTRUMENTS / OPERATIONAL REAL ESTATE
SUBMISSION TIMESTAMP: 14:07:33

Okafor types. The room's six workstations continue their individual tasks — the filing queue is not a single action, it is a cascade of ninety-four individual submissions that have to arrive within a forty-minute window to prevent sequential injection attacks by Roman's legal team.

They have been inside the window since 13:43.

Chen Mei takes a position to Okafor's left. She does not sit. She watches the filing queue move.

Okafor pulls the Sera Mast publication timestamp from the reference archive — the outer-tier broadcast, the 43-second window from the moderation gap in SC0096, the content that reached 16,000 subscribers before The Herald's auxiliary routing could touch it. The story ran. Outside jurisdictions received the material. The Outer Vale Compact's legal basis for acting was established before Roman's network had finished restoring its broadcast feed. The story is not injunctable — it has been in circulation for nineteen hours.

The cascade reaches seventy-eight submissions. Eighty-one. Eighty-seven.

A notification arrives from the court registry. It is Wren's motion — a multi-jurisdiction injunction filing, comprehensive, well-constructed, precisely targeted at the legal mechanisms Okafor has been deploying. It is the motion they expected. It would have worked two hours ago.

CASS WREN / VELTAIN LEGAL GROUP — INJUNCTION FILING
MOTION: STAY OF SEIZURE PROCEEDINGS, MULTI-JURISDICTION
FILING TIMESTAMP: 14:48:17

The cascade reaches ninety-four.

Okafor reads the first line of Wren's motion. She reads it the way she reads everything — fully, once, without annotation. She sets the document down.

"File the next tranche."

She is not speaking to Chen Mei. She is speaking to the room. One of the six workstations acknowledges and begins the second filing sequence.

Chen Mei does not say anything. She watches the confirmation count on the primary monitor. The first seizure order acknowledgments are already returning — not all of them, not yet, the processes in different jurisdictions operate at different speeds and some of what Okafor filed will not be processed until tomorrow. But enough are back to confirm that the threshold has been crossed: the filing has landed, it has been received by systems that Roman's infrastructure cannot reach, and Wren's injunction motion is forty-one minutes behind what it was trying to stop.

Criminal infrastructure. Legal collapse initiated.

The room continues to work.

What is not happening yet: any criminal charges. Any forfeitures. Any trials. The legal process that starts here will take years — assets frozen, not yet touched; participants named, not yet tried; documentation in three separate jurisdiction archives where it cannot be withdrawn or suppressed, but also cannot be acted on quickly. What Okafor filed today is the initiation. Nothing more and nothing less.

She is already in the next file.

Chen Mei watches the acknowledgment count reach the threshold. She does not mark it. She does not say anything. This is what the last eighteen months of work looks like when it resolves.

She pulls up a chair. She is going to be here for the next tranche.
