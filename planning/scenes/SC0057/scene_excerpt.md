INT. REGIONAL BUS — FORWARD SECTION — AFTERNOON

She has the forward section to herself. Two other passengers are in the rear. Outside, the corridor is flat and agricultural — harvested fields, low service structures, the occasional relay tower.

She has been on this bus for three hours and forty minutes.

The device alerts.

She looks at it. An intelligence packet from the relay chain — a field contact identifier she does not recognize by code but knows by the routing: Zara's network, operational tier, near-VELTAIN access. She opens it.

The message reads:

STATUS REPORT — FIELD CONTACT V7-ECHO
Confirmed observation window: thirty-six hours prior to transmission.
Location: VELTAIN-7, eastern residential block, unit interior.
Subject: infant male, approximately seven months. Dark hair.
Physical condition: stable. No visible distress.
Distinguishing detail: pale blue medical identification bracelet, right wrist. Hospital-registry format.
Transmission ends.

She reads it once.

Her fingers find her wrist pulse point — brief, involuntary, the inventory gesture — then stop.

She reads it again.

The bracelet. She chose it from a rack in the registration corridor. Pale blue, she remembers, because the green one had a fraying edge and she had asked for a replacement. The administrative clerk had handed her the blue one and she had not thought about it again. The band contains the passive ID chip from the original registration — hospital issue, low-power, short-range. She has not thought about this in months. She thinks about it now.

She does not have the equipment to read the chip. She files the detail.

She opens a second document and writes:

*PL09: Medical bracelet — right wrist, passive ID chip, hospital registry. Equipment needed: short-range scanner, passive-mode reader. Acquire before perimeter approach.*

She closes the second document.

She reads the confirmation a third time. Not to receive new information. To decide whether to believe it.

The field contact identified the bracelet. Manufactured intelligence usually does not include the kind of incidental physical detail that requires actual visual access to an infant in a room. It is not proof. It is a credibility marker. She notes it as such.

She has partial confirmation of Jin's location since Zara's transmission — a reference code and a supply routing number. This is different. This is someone who has been in the building.

Thirty-six hours old. Not live. Possibly compromised since transmission. She knows all of this.

She puts the device away.

The plan was written before this message arrived. She was going to VELTAIN regardless. What the message changes is not the destination — it is the reason she can sustain moving toward it.

She was going to VELTAIN despite not knowing.

She is going to VELTAIN because he is there.

The bus moves through the corridor. Outside, another relay tower. Another field. The light is going yellow at the edges.

She does not look at the device again.
