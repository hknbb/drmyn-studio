INT. OKAFOR'S WORKING OFFICE — CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL LIAISON UNIT — DAY

The office is on the fourth floor of an institutional building that does not advertise what it houses. Shared workspace — two desks, one meeting table, one secure terminal. The window faces the street. The blinds are open. Okafor does not close the blinds.

Riko is at the secondary desk. He has not sat down in forty minutes. He stands with one hand on the desk's edge and watches the confirmation queue.

Okafor is reading.

---

The first challenge arrived at 06:47. Four hours and eleven minutes after the original filing.

Cass Wren's counter: a jurisdictional objection to the filing authority of the first notice. Precisely constructed. It targeted the specific procedural gap in the first notice's jurisdiction — a gap that Okafor had documented herself, four months ago, in a risk assessment of the same jurisdiction's evidentiary standing in cross-border financial cases.

RIKO
They had this ready. This is not something you draft in four hours.

OKAFOR
No.

RIKO
So the question is whether they had it ready for this specific filing or whether they maintain standing challenges on every registered instrument in the network.

OKAFOR
The answer is both. They prepared this one when the network was first registered. They maintain updated versions. It is very good work.

Riko looks at her.

RIKO
You sound almost respectful.

OKAFOR
I am respectful. A jurisdictional challenge this precise takes real architecture. It also takes considerable institutional reach to maintain that infrastructure across seven jurisdictions simultaneously.

She sets the challenge document down.

OKAFOR (CONT'D)
Which is documented now.
