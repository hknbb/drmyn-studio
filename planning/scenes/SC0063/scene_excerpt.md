INT. 14 HAVEL COURT — THIRD FLOOR — PRE-DAWN

She has been calculating for forty minutes. Six minutes between trigger and physical arrival. Probably less.

She moves to Tobias's door. She knocks the pattern from SEQ03: two sharp, two soft.

He opens immediately. He is dressed. His kit is closed and on his shoulder. He has been awake.

She tells him in nine words:

NADIA
Fire escape. East window. Freight corridor east. Go now.

He picks up the kit. He says nothing.

They move into the corridor together, toward the east-facing room. Her in front, him behind. The building picks up the sound of two people moving — she can feel the monitoring the way you feel watched, not through evidence but through the geometry of the space.

Twelve steps to the east room. She counts them.

On the ninth step, the staircase below is active.

Not a single set of footsteps. Multiple. Coordinated ascent. She counts the weight distribution: four people, maybe five, moving fast.

She hears the south room door at the same moment.

The grey man. He has been in the building the entire time, stationary, waiting. He steps into the corridor from the south end, thirty feet behind them. Glasses in his hand. The reading glasses she noted in SC0059. He does not say anything either.

Two vectors. The east room is ahead. Three doors down.

NADIA
(low, moving)
Keep moving. Don't stop.

She lets Tobias pass her and falls back two steps, putting herself between him and the grey man. The grey man is not running. He is walking with the calibration of someone who knows the door ahead is not the exit.

The ascending hunters reach the third-floor landing.

She hears the staircase door.

The corridor is eight feet wide. The east room is one door ahead of Tobias now. She has approximately four seconds.

She hits the grey man at the shoulder, hard enough to send him into the corridor wall, and turns without waiting for the result. She is through the east room doorway behind Tobias.

East window. Tobias has it open already — he found it, assessed it, opened it in the time it took her to clear the corridor. His kit is already through. He is on the sill.

She hears the third-floor landing door open behind her.

NADIA
Go.

He goes.

She is through the window a breath after him, one hand on the sill, and she looks down once — the fire escape, Tobias below her, the alley below that — and then she moves.

The sound from below changes before she has both feet on the fire escape.

Not Tobias. Not the fire escape. Something in the alley: weight, contact, the specific compression of a space that was empty a moment ago and is not now.

The alley was covered.

She goes over the east railing of the fire escape rather than down it — not the alley, the adjacent loading dock, one floor further and offset by fifteen feet. She drops.

The landing is wrong. Left leg, too much lateral, something in the knee registers before she has stopped moving and she is already running when it does.

Behind her, above her, the alley.

She does not look back.

She is in the freight corridor east before she has processed that Tobias is not with her.

EXT. FREIGHT CORRIDOR — LINDEN JUNCTION — PRE-DAWN

Running. The corridor is service infrastructure — loading access, no civilian foot traffic, concrete margins, darkness.

She runs.

The guilt arrives the way a wound arrives: not at the moment of injury, but when the body has enough information to understand what happened.

She brought him here. The route was designed to collect nodes. He was a node because of her. She stayed because of the suture. She stayed while he was in a building that was designed to hold people off-broadcast and she cannot now say she did not know what she was inside.

She knew.

She runs.

She does not stop.
