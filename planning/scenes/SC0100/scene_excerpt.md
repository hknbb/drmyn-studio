INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — SERVICE CORRIDOR / EAST FACE — CONTINUOUS

A maintenance passage: concrete walls, overhead conduit, a line of work-light panels at half-power. The passage smells of hydraulic compound and recycled air. It is not a space that appears in the broadcast's visual palette — too utilitarian, too empty of the aesthetic register The Herald uses to frame the game.

NADIA is moving through it at the speed of someone who has calculated exactly how fast she can move without tripping the vibration sensors embedded in the floor joints. She knows the sensors' threshold because she spent four minutes watching Morrow's maintenance crew work this passage six days ago, before Roman's people moved her to the ZORAL CORRIDOR. She filed it.

She has filed everything.

Forty meters ahead, at the junction where the service corridor opens into the east equipment room, a HALO UNIT is positioned against the left wall. Its optical arrays are tracking her — have been tracking her since she entered the passage — with the smooth, unhurried precision of a system that does not need to hurry because it has never lost anyone.

THE HERALD
(voice from the HALO UNIT; warm, audience-directed; she is its subject and its product simultaneously)
— and we're watching something quite remarkable here, folks. She came through the north gate's shadow in under four minutes from the perimeter alert, which tells you what kind of patience we're dealing with. Now she's moving east — and I want to draw your attention to the betting line, because—

Nadia stops. Thirty-eight meters from the unit.

Not close enough to reach it. Close enough for the maintenance channel.

She produces the device she has been carrying since the day she watched Morrow's hands. Morrow typed a six-character alphanumeric in a specific sequence — she clocked it from eleven meters in poor light; she typed it back into her own memory three times that night to make sure the index was stable. She has not had occasion to use it until this moment. The override command requires proximity to the unit's maintenance receiver — within forty meters.

Thirty-eight.

She enters the sequence.

THE HERALD (CONT'D)
— if you look at the odds right now, you can see the sympathy coefficient has been climbing all—

The voice stops.

Not at the end of a sentence. Not at a natural pause. Mid-clause: the word all and then nothing.

The HALO UNIT's optical arrays do not dim gradually. They go from active to standby in a single step — the specific visual quality of a system that has been interrupted rather than shut down. Its broadcast hardware is offline. Its movement capability remains; it can still track her optically; it just cannot transmit.

The service corridor is very quiet.

ORACLE PRIME PLATFORM INTERFACE — FLASH:

[BROADCAST INTERRUPTED — TECHNICAL MAINTENANCE — RESUMING SHORTLY]

The betting line freezes at the moment of cut.

Nadia looks at the unit for one full beat — not with triumph; with the specific quality of attention she gives to any system she has just proved she understands better than its builders assumed. The silence has a texture she has not experienced since the moment before the game began.

She is not being watched.

She starts counting. Under her breath, barely audible, her lips forming numbers: one, two—

She moves.

Eight minutes before Roman's technical team restores the feed. She knows this because Morrow's maintenance logs, which she also memorized, noted a restoration test time of seven minutes forty seconds for a full override recovery. She has rounded up.

The HALO UNIT tracks her optical silhouette — silent, recording nothing, unable to do the one thing it was built to do — as she passes it without breaking stride and turns east toward the interior door.

Eight minutes.

She is counting.


INT. ORACLE PRIME PLATFORM — SUBSCRIBER FEED — CONTINUOUS

ORACLE PRIME PLATFORM INTERFACE:

[BROADCAST INTERRUPTED — TECHNICAL MAINTENANCE — RESUMING SHORTLY]

The betting line is frozen at the moment of Nadia's entry into the service corridor. The last reported odds hang on the interface like a sentence left unfinished. The Herald's feed — which has not been silent since the game opened — is a blank rectangle at the center of the platform's display architecture.

In the chat streams that border the main feed, the audience is producing language at the speed of people who have lost the thing that was organizing their attention.

Some of them are furious. They have money on the line and the line is frozen and the machine has never frozen before, not in any of the previous events, not once.

Some of them have gone quiet in a way that is different from the furious quiet. They are waiting to see what they feel.
