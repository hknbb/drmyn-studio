INT. HUNTER FIELD STAGING — ZORAL CORRIDOR APPROACH — LATE AFTERNOON

A transit depot office, commandeered. Three folding tables pushed together to form a single working surface. Maps — actual printed maps, not just screens — laid flat and weighted at the corners. A HALO UNIT data terminal feeds position updates from the Corridor's surveillance infrastructure. The light comes from two portable floods and one inadequate ceiling fixture. This is a field operation run by people who know how to make temporary spaces functional.

MINA DRASC is at the terminal. She has a notebook — slim, physical, always within reach — open to a current page of handwritten position assessments. She does not need to look at the notebook to know what is in it; she looks at it anyway.

VEKA DRASC is not sitting. She is at the map. Her finger traces the Corridor approach geometry — the channeled section between the processing sheds and the checkpoint margin, two hundred meters of reduced lateral options. She has run this trace three times in the last ten minutes.

BARRAK SOLV stands at the room's perimeter, slightly apart. He is watching both of them in the way that a senior contractor watches a field team: not supervising, assessing.

VEKA
(without looking up from the map)
She's in the approach lane. We have the corridor geometry. Two exits — secondary gate or back into the industrial layer. Both covered.

MINA
Covered within the current window. The window is fourteen minutes.

VEKA
Fourteen minutes is enough.

MINA
Fourteen minutes is enough if she stays inside the approach layer. She's been in four covered zones in the last eight hours. She leaves them.

VEKA
Then we move in the next six.

She looks at Barrak. Direct.

BARRAK
Here's what's true — we have a viable window.

He says it without inflection, as if reporting a weather condition.

BARRAK (CONT'D)
We also have a standing instruction.

VEKA
The instruction is to keep her alive. I'm not proposing we don't keep her alive. I'm proposing we use the window to close the distance.

MINA
Closing the distance forces her into the secondary gate. Secondary gate puts her into the Corridor. Once she's through the Corridor we're in Merin jurisdiction and our surveillance asset density drops by sixty percent.

A beat.

VEKA
So we close it before she reaches the gate.

BARRAK
Not yet.

The two words land the same way Barrak says everything — complete and without drama. He moves to the map. He does not need to trace the route; he looks at it as a whole.

BARRAK (CONT'D)
Here's what's true — extended duration is extended yield. Roman's instruction is not arbitrary sentiment. Every hour she's in the field is another cycle of audience engagement. Another betting window. Another round of commissions against the field rate.

VEKA
We're losing ground in this window.

BARRAK
We're accumulating contract value in this window.

He looks at Veka. She does not look away. She does not agree.

BARRAK (CONT'D)
Not yet.

A beat. Veka repositions — moves from the map to the terminal side of the table, two steps, no destination, a change of location rather than a response.

Mina notes something in her notebook. Her expression does not change.

MINA
The north camera array has a four-minute blind rotation. If she's in the approach with local cover, she's not in Drune's packet during the blind.

BARRAK
Then she has four minutes of unlogged movement.

MINA
Which she may or may not know she has.

BARRAK
She's been reading the grid since Rennmark. She knows.

He turns back to the position data on the terminal. The conversation is over because he has finished it.

VEKA
(flat, to Mina)
Next window.

The look that ends it is directed at Mina, not Barrak. Mina closes her notebook.
