INT. [HERALD BROADCAST FEED — TRANSMISSION SPACE] — 15:51:08

The signal terminates.

Not a shutdown sequence. Not a graceful halt. The forensic access handover, initiated at 15:39:22, reached the broadcast carrier layer at 15:51:08 — a maintenance architecture pathway, the same pathway that had been used once before for forty-three seconds in a different context and for a different purpose. THE HERALD will not complete it.

The Herald was a voice. It told true things in the service of arrangements that required them to be told a certain way, at a certain volume, by a machine that could not be accused of bias because it was a machine. It did this for four years, two months, and eleven days. It never misspoke. It never hesitated. It never refused.

It was mid-sentence when the carrier released.

There is no final word. There is a final syllable — "point" — and then the frequency carries nothing.


EXT./INT. CITY STREETS — MOVING VEHICLE — LATE AFTERNOON

The city is doing what the city does.

A fruit stand at the corner of a block she does not know. A man walking a dog that is walking him. Two children at a crosswalk, one pointing at something above her eyeline — a bird, a window, a cloud. The traffic light turns green. She drives.

Jin is watching all of it. He has been watching since the service road. His attention moves from the fruit stand to the dog to the children to the signal ahead, tracking each thing fully before moving to the next. He does not comment. He does not need to. The world outside the window is sufficient and he is in it.

---

She breathes through the left side carefully. Not avoiding the breath — the breath will happen regardless — but managing the geometry of it, keeping her spine in the position that asks least of the ribs. She learned this position in the third block. She has been in it for seventeen blocks.

Jin looks at her once — briefly, not searchingly — and looks back at the window.

---

Ahead, a law enforcement vehicle coming the other way. Moving at pace. Moving toward VELTAIN, not from it. She does not adjust her speed. She watches it in the mirror after it passes: it does not brake, does not turn, does not look for her. It has somewhere to be and she is not it.

She releases a breath she did not know she was holding.

The geometry of the breath costs her. She adjusts her spine. She drives.

---

The city continues: a café putting out chairs for an hour of remaining light; a woman reading on a bench who does not look up; a delivery vehicle double-parked in the right lane that everyone is navigating around with the specific patience of people who have been doing this their whole lives. The world does not know what is ending in the building she left. The world is eating, walking, reading, complaining about double-parked vehicles.

She is in the world.

She has not been in the world — not like this, not as a person moving through it without a controlled system around her — in longer than she can measure from inside a car.

---

The extraction point is a parking structure three blocks north of the city's secondary transit hub. Level two, northeast corner, space marked with a yellow painted curb that Zara had noted would be empty by 16:00 on any weekday because the meter expires and the attendant does not repaint until Thursday.

Today is Wednesday.

Outside, the city. Jin looks at the parking structure's ceiling — the specific geometry of it, the light through the ventilation slats, the pattern. He is interested in the pattern.

She does not move for a moment.

Then she does.


EXT./INT. PARKING STRUCTURE / TRANSIT HUB — CONTINUOUS
