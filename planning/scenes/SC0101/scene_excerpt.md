INT. KESTON LALE'S RESIDENCE — CONTINUOUS

A private viewing room: one wall given over to a subscription terminal, a leather chair positioned at the precise distance that the display renders best. The room is dark. It has been dark for hours.

KESTON LALE, 44, watches the blank feed.

He is not furious. He placed his first bet in the third hour of the first day — thirty credits on Nadia not making it out of the ZORAL CORRIDOR. She made it out. He placed seventeen more bets across twelve days; his net position as of the betting line freeze is forty-three credits ahead.

He is forty-three credits ahead and he is watching a blank screen and his face has the specific quality of a person who has been waiting for permission to stop.

The blank screen is not permission. It is something worse: it is an interruption, which means the machine can be interrupted, which means it was always a machine rather than a fact.

He has known this. He has known this for some time. He placed bets anyway.

On the outer tier of the platform — the subscriber feed that runs alongside the main broadcast for high-tier members — a notification propagates. Not from The Herald. From an anonymous document-distribution address that has been seeding the outer tier for six hours with small pieces of what looks, at first, like archive material.

It is not archive material.

Keston clicks the notification. He starts reading.

CUT TO:

INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — CONTROL ROOM — CONTINUOUS

ROMAN is standing at the monitoring station when the duty technician reports the feed failure. He listens to the technician's summary — forty-three seconds; override event on the maintenance channel; source of override still being traced — without any visible change in expression.

Then:

ROMAN
Get it back.

The technician moves. Roman watches the blank feed on the control room's wall display — the same blank rectangle the audience is watching, from the inside.

He is not thinking about who triggered the override, not yet. He is thinking about the audience. The audience is his architecture of consequence: as long as they are watching, the event's logic holds. As long as the event's logic holds, his liability framework holds. Without the broadcast, the event is not an event — it is a private act with private consequences, and private consequences are the kind that regulators can see.

He needs the feed restored in under eight minutes. He knows this from the override protocol documentation, which he wrote.

He does not think about what else the eight minutes might be doing.

CUT TO:

INT. SERA'S WORKSPACE — A RENTED OFFICE NEAR VELTAIN STATION — CONTINUOUS

SERA is watching three screens simultaneously. The left screen is a real-time propagation tracker she built in a hotel room in MERIN three weeks ago. The middle screen is the ORACLE PRIME outer-tier feed. The right screen is a draft document that has been open for eleven days and is forty pages long.

The propagation tracker shows a cascade.

SERA
(to herself; fast, under her breath; confirming)
It's moving — okay — it's moving—

She opens a notebook that has been sitting closed on the desk — something she put down when the broadcast first went dark — and starts writing in it. The notebook is already full of previous entries. She is starting a new page.

The automated release she built into her document-distribution address had one trigger condition: a blackout event on the ORACLE PRIME platform lasting more than thirty seconds. The condition has been met. The documents are moving through the outer tier — not all forty pages, not yet; the first release is a selection: the financial records, the platform's liability framework in Roman's own handwriting, the list of previous events with their dates and participant counts and outcomes.

Outcomes meaning: what happened to every person who was put into the game before Nadia.

The outer tier is receiving this. The outer tier has approximately sixteen thousand subscribers who are not the betting audience — they are the peripheral audience, the watchers, the people who pay for access to the feed but do not bet. They are less monitored. They are more likely to share.

Sera watches the propagation counter.

SERA (CONT'D)
(writing, not looking up)
Who else knows about this. Right.

She writes the question in the notebook and underlines it. Then she looks at the propagation counter and writes the number down.
