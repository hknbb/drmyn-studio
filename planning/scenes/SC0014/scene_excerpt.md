INT. JIN'S NURSERY — VALE RESIDENCE — EVENING

A small room. Made carefully — the kind of careful that takes time and means something. Soft light. A crib. A shelf of things that belong to an eight-month-old.

NADIA is sitting in the chair by the crib, holding JIN. He is awake. She is not doing anything particular — not rocking, not singing. Just holding him. The way you hold something you are about to lose and cannot say so.

The door opens.

DIMITRI enters. Two men behind him. They stand inside the door.

DIMITRI
Mrs. Vale.

Nadia does not look up.

DIMITRI (CONT'D)
It's time.

She looks up now.

She stands, still holding Jin. She takes one step back — not toward anything, just back. The instinct of something that knows it is cornered.

DIMITRI
Please don't make this harder.

She says nothing.

The first man moves toward her. She turns her body. She is not slow and she is not weak and it does not matter — he is practiced and larger and there is a second man at the door and Dimitri in the middle. She keeps her arms around Jin.

The man reaches for the child with both hands. Careful. Firm.

She holds.

He lifts.

Her arms resist — everything they have, which is everything a person can give — and it is not enough.

Jin is lifted free.

Her hand reaches after him. Her fingers find the bracelet — the pale blue band, the clasp, the small type of his name — for exactly one beat.

Then the man steps back and her hand is empty.

The second man holds her by both arms. Not roughly. Just completely.

She watches Jin carried to the door.

Jin makes a small sound — not fear, just sound; he is too young to know what is happening and that is its own unbearable thing.

The door closes.

---

The second man releases her. He steps back. He nods once — professional courtesy — and leaves.

NADIA stands in the room.

The crib is empty.

Her hand — the one that held the bracelet — is open at her side.

She does not move toward the door.

She is already somewhere else.
