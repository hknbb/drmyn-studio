INT. OUTER DISTRICTS — WAREHOUSE PASSAGE — UPPER FLOOR — MORNING

During the SC0017 sweep she walked this passage once. Rusted floor plate spanning the north section — load-bearing frame corroded, deflection wrong under her foot weight, the sound wrong. She noted it. She kept moving.

She is back now. She takes position at the south end of the passage and she waits.

---

She hears him before she sees him.

Not footfall — he is too trained for that. What she hears is the quality of the silence changing: the birds moving through the upper structure have gone still. A professional clears a space without noise, but the things that live in the space react.

She does not move.

He appears at the north end. Mid-forties. Heavy shoulders, economical build — conditioned for function. He scans the passage before entering. Lead hand: right. Balance weight forward — closing preference, not standoff. The knife is field-carry, not display. Someone who has done this before and knows that display is for people who haven't. His scan pattern is systematic: corner, shadow, the column gap at her left, the floor.

He looks at the floor.

He looks up and sees her.

---

He commits without pause.

She moves at the same moment — south, toward the compromised section, not away from it. She needs him to follow. He follows because she is moving and he is trained to close on movement.

She crosses the span at a run, weight distributed at the edges, short strides, foot-fall away from the center. She reaches the far side.

His leading foot hits the center of the span. The floor sags — a half-second of structural protest — and he has already read it wrong and shifted his weight forward to compensate. Wrong compensation.

The span buckles. He drops his lead leg through to arrest the fall and the forward momentum twists him ninety degrees. One leg through the floor, body weight going the wrong direction.

His knife arm extends on instinct.

The reach is longer than she calculated.

---

A line of heat across her left forearm, outside edge. She does not stop.

She is at the passage end, the stairs, down. Controlled speed — not a run, because debris stairs at a run is a broken ankle and then the wound is not the problem. She hits the ground floor.

Behind her: the sound of the floor giving further, then the deliberate sounds of someone extracting himself from a structural trap without panic.

Professional.

---

Two hundred meters away, inside a different structure, she checks the forearm.

The sleeve is dark from wrist to elbow. She unbuttons it.

A draw cut — full length, outside edge, wrist bone to lateral elbow. Clean edges. Bleeding hard.

She holds it closed with her right hand. Applies pressure. Times it.

Thirty seconds. Then she moves.

She cannot afford a trail. Treatment has to be done properly. During the northern sweep she found a covered courtyard two structures east — a standpipe mounted to the interior wall there, different from the drop-zone standpipe. Far enough from the passage that Solv's repositioning won't reach it before she is done.

She does not react to the pain. She categorizes it: non-dominant arm, no tendon involvement, grip functional, treatment required within forty minutes to avoid compromise.

Forty minutes.

She moves.
