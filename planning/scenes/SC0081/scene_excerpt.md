INT. ABANDONED RELAY STATION — OUTER TRANSIT DISTRICT — NIGHT

The problem is format.

The broadcast grid's proximity sensors transmit on a standardized frequency, but the data packet structure is proprietary — a specific encoding the ORACLE PRIME platform developed to distinguish its own sensors from ambient network noise. An external signal on the right frequency that doesn't match the encoding schema gets flagged as interference and rejected before it reaches The Herald's processing layer.

The transmitter from the cache uses a commercial protocol. Not the right one.

Nadia is rewriting the header.

She has enough of the encoding structure from the deployment documents — the cell's operational files included a maintenance handshake record, which contains the schema prefix. Not the full format. Enough of the format. She builds the packet around what she has and works backward from the structural logic.
