INT. RECOVERY ROOM — LATE AFTERNOON

A table. Two chairs. A window with institutional blinds. Tobias Orell sits in one chair with a tray of instruments beside him — gauze, a penlight, two sealed packets — arranged with excessive precision. He has been arranging them since they brought him in. He does not comment on this.

He does not look injured. He looks tired and irritated in the specific way of someone who has been tired and irritated for a long time and has learned to use it as a primary facial expression.

Okafor sits across from him with a notepad and does not open it.

---

OKAFOR
The team confirmed you were held for eleven days. Compound level B.

TOBIAS
I know how long I was held.

OKAFOR
Medical assessment—

TOBIAS
I did my own. I'm fine. Relatively. That's a qualified fine — not a blanket fine. The left shoulder is—

He stops. Moves a packet of gauze three millimeters to the left.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
I'll write it up.

---

A pause. Okafor does not push. Tobias looks at the window, not through it — at the angle of the blinds, the geometry of the slats.

OKAFOR
We'll need to discuss what you witnessed. During the eleven days. There's a legal proceeding—

TOBIAS
I know what's happening. I follow the news. I followed the news from a room with no windows, which is a specific kind of following, but the information reached me.

OKAFOR
Then you understand that your testimony—

TOBIAS
I won't testify.

He says it flat. Not angry. Flat. He adjusts the penlight so it is parallel to the gauze.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
I went underground because I didn't want to be in a room answering questions with my name attached to it. That's still true. The situation around it has changed. The desire hasn't.

---

Okafor writes nothing on the notepad. She waits.

The room is quiet enough that the arrangement of instruments is audible — the small sound of a sealed packet moved precisely against a tray.

Tobias looks at the instruments. Looks at Okafor. Back at the instruments.

TOBIAS
What I witnessed. In eleven days.

He doesn't complete the sentence. He is deciding in real time whether to complete it. Okafor has been in this room long enough to recognize the rhythm.

A beat. Two beats.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
I might.

He picks up the penlight. Clicks it twice. Sets it down.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
I said might. Don't— don't put that in whatever that is.

He gestures at the unopened notepad.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
I'll think about it. There's a cost to it. I'm calculating the cost.

OKAFOR
Take the time you need.

TOBIAS
That's not reassuring. That's just — that's just a thing people say when they want you to feel like you have time you don't have.

He pauses.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
But yes. I'll think about it.
