INT. EASTBOUND TRAIN — FORWARD-FACING SEAT — LATE MORNING

She is in the relay installation diagrams. Second hour on the train. The fields outside have given way to low scrubland and then to a stretch of industrial corridor — warehouses, access roads, pale concrete running parallel to the track.

The screen at the far end of the car activates.

It has been carrying the regional travel service feed — route updates, station stops, a weather graphic for the eastern corridor. Now the feed cuts and the ORACLE PRIME signal replaces it. White on black. A tone. Then:

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Good morning. I am speaking to you from the heart of ORACLE PRIME's broadcast infrastructure, and I have news that our most committed audience members have been anticipating.

She does not put down the device. She watches from where she is.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
At this stage of each ORACLE PRIME event, the rules of engagement may be augmented — at the discretion of event management, in response to audience participation metrics, and in service of the experience we have built together over seven extraordinary years. Today, we augment.

SP02. The audience's appetite shaped the machine's operational escalation. The announcement exists because the metrics demanded it.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
First augmentation: Transparency Protocol. Effective immediately, the subject's last confirmed location will be broadcast on all ORACLE PRIME channels at fixed six-hour intervals. Location data will be transmitted directly from our field monitoring network to the broadcast. Our first Transparency Protocol window opens in five hours and fifty-three minutes.

She sets the device face-down in her lap.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Second augmentation: Reserve Activation. If our first Transparency Protocol window confirms the subject is operating within the primary zone of engagement — the corridor between Merin and our eastern facility — our passive hunter reserve will be activated. Three additional participants will enter the field within four hours of confirmation.

The primary zone of engagement. She is in it. She has to be in it. UNIT 7-ECHO is in it. The trophy room is at its eastern end.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Third augmentation: Event Deadline. This event will resolve within seventy-two hours of this announcement. If no resolution has been achieved at the deadline, Event Expiry protocols will activate. Our audience has waited long enough for a conclusion. So have we.

A pause. The Herald's tone shifts — the administrative register giving way to something warmer, more intimate.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
We are grateful, as always, for an audience that cares. Your engagement is the reason this event continues to matter. A brief procedural note before we close: ORACLE PRIME's betting odds are calculated in real time from live field data. Location data is transmitted directly from our monitoring network to broadcast and betting systems. As always, we do not adjust, filter, or delay betting data. What our systems confirm, our audience sees.

She hears this sentence. She hears it as something different from what the audience hears.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Current field status. Subject location: confirmed Merin transit hub. Time of last confirmation: ninety-one minutes prior. Current odds —

She picks up the device.

She checks the time. She has been on this train for ninety-four minutes. The last fixed infrastructure node she passed through was Merin station — she boarded there. Since then: no fixed nodes. Transit corridor.

The system has her at Merin.

The system does not know she is on this train. It cannot know — there are no ORACLE PRIME monitoring nodes on a regional eastbound rail service. The system gathers location from fixed infrastructure: transit hubs, station sensors, registered access points. Between those points, the system is working from its last confirmed data.

She is ninety-four minutes ahead of where the system thinks she is.

The broadcast ends. The regional travel service feed resumes. The fields outside are the same fields; the train is the same train.

She looks at the betting data still visible in the corner of the screen before the regional feed fills it: the odds against her survival are high. The odds are calculated on a subject standing in Merin transit hub, not a subject on a train with three hours until arrival at her first objective.

Two things are true at the same time. The Special Rules are genuinely dangerous. And Roman's tracking system has a blind spot she did not know about until The Herald told her, in a routine disclosure, that the system cannot lie about what it knows.

She cannot use this yet. The Transparency Protocol window opens in under six hours. She needs to be at UNIT 7-ECHO or beyond before it does, in a location with no fixed monitoring infrastructure. She needs to know the monitoring node map for the eastern corridor. She needs to know where the blind spots are.

She opens the relay installation documentation.

The relay installations. The fixed-node infrastructure. Marcus documented the broadcast relay points — and broadcast relay infrastructure and location monitoring infrastructure often share the same physical footprint.

She starts reading with a different question than she had before.
