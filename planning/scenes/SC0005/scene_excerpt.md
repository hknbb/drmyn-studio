INT. THE VALE RESIDENCE — MAIN RECEPTION — EVENING

The room is expensive in a way that has long since ceased to announce itself. Forty guests. Low conversation, the particular frequency of people who are comfortable. Caterers move with practiced invisibility.

NADIA VALE moves through the room. She is the hostess, which means she is working — receiving, directing, sustaining warmth across multiple simultaneous surfaces. She is very good at this.

TWO GUESTS near the fireplace. We catch the edge of their conversation:

GUEST 1
— the Bratislava event. Clean outcome, as I understand.

GUEST 2
Better than clean. The Bratislava event closed at a twelve-percent premium over projections.

GUEST 1
Roman's modeling. You trust the number before the event opens.

This is not remarkable to them. They move on to wine.

Nadia passes. She did not react. She never reacts.

---

ROMAN holds court near the east window with three men whose names do not matter because their function does: money, access, adjacency to legitimacy. He is in his patrician mode — unhurried, slightly amused, fully in command of the room's temperature.

ROMAN
The Minsk collection took me four years to curate. You understand — I don't mean the objects. I mean the story each one carries. A trophy without provenance is just an object. With provenance it becomes evidence of judgment.

A guest laughs, appropriately. Roman smiles — not warmly, precisely.

ROMAN (CONT'D)
I've been expanding the room. New acquisitions. Events of a certain significance tend to produce items worth holding onto.

He lifts his glass.

ROMAN (CONT'D)
For the archive.

---

DIMITRI KOSS stands slightly apart, managing the room's logistics without appearing to. He directs a caterer with two fingers. He adjusts the placement of a conversation by crossing the room at exactly the right moment. He is efficient in the way of a precision instrument.

He is also watching.

Not the guests. The room's geometry.

---

Nadia accepts a glass she will not drink. She receives a compliment she does not need. She steers a guest toward another guest with a sentence so smooth it does not feel like steering.

She is across the room from Roman.

She does not look at him. She does not need to.

And then she does.

It is not intentional. It is the small reflex of a long marriage — two people in the same space developing a peripheral awareness of each other's position.

ROMAN IS LOOKING AT HER.

Not social surveillance — not the casual pan of a host confirming his event is functioning. This is a different quality. Still. Patient. The look of a man who has already priced something in and is now confirming the price.

Nadia meets it.

She does not look away. She does not perform warmth. She holds the gaze with the particular neutrality of someone whose composure has been assembled from scratch and is not a natural phenomenon.

One beat. Two.

Roman's attention moves — back to his guests, without hurry, as if the look had never happened.

Nadia turns to her nearest guest.

NADIA
(to guest)
You were in Lisbon last spring? I heard the symposium was remarkable.

The guest is pleased to be asked. The conversation resumes.

Somewhere below her sternum, a variable has shifted that she cannot name yet. She adds it to the account she keeps internally — the running tally of things that do not yet mean anything but may.

The party continues.

Outside the tall east windows, the city moves through its evening — indifferent, continuous, knowing nothing about what is being priced in this room.
