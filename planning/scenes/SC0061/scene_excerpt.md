INT. TRANSIT VEHICLE — OUTER SERVICE DISTRICT, LINDEN JUNCTION — NIGHT

The vehicle is parked in the loading margin off the service road. Engine off. Dark. The windows face north toward the service quarter three blocks away. Zara has been here for forty minutes.

The intelligence package arrived through her hunter-pool access forty minutes ago. She has been reading it since.

The address is real. She has run the building: 14 Havel Court, northern service quarter, operational since the district's last infrastructure cycle, no flag in the public records. The geography is correct. She has verified the Soral access route against her own maps: genuine, passable, the transit restriction timing is accurately stated. The supplies are consistent with a legitimate underground cache. The channel is a real channel — one she has used herself for forward intelligence.

Most of it reads clean.

She reaches the contact phrase.

THE LIGHT'S BEEN OUT SINCE TUESDAY

She stops.

Her hands go still. Her eyes move.

The phrase is documented. Not used — documented. Six months ago, a hunter-pool debrief on a MERIN district capture operation. She was brought in as a contractor; the debrief ran three hours; she sat through the section on channel integrity because it was relevant to her next assignment. The notation was brief: a specific set of phrases had been seeded into the corridor-operative relay network as trap-identifiers. Voluntary entry confirmation. Anyone who used the phrase at a controlled address had confirmed their own presence to the monitoring network. The notation listed three phrases. This was one of them.

She read it once. She retained it because she retains everything that has operational value.

She looks at the phrase in the package.

She looks at the address.

She closes the document.

The connection is complete.

The obstacle is not the geography or the resources or the channel — it is the phrase, and the phrase is readable only if you were in that room six months ago, reading a notation that was filed and never flagged again. Nadia was not in that room. Nadia cannot have spotted this.

She exhales once, through her nose.

Then she opens a new message.

WARNING — LINDEN JUNCTION

Contact phrase compromised: monitored Roman architecture. Address flagged in prior operation. Do not use the phrase. Exit if mobile.

Route via: north alley to Soral junction — genuine geography, but exit before Soral access road. Take the freight corridor east from the service quarter junction instead.

She reads it back. It is specific. It is enough. She sends it toward Nadia's last confirmed relay point.

She does not know that Nadia is already inside.

She does not know about Tobias.

She knows the phrase is compromised, the address is monitored, and the window for a clean exit closes in proportion to how long someone has already been inside.

She picks up the vehicle's secondary communications unit and begins composing a second message — to a different relay, a different destination, flagging the channel itself for her own records. If this channel is seeded, her position as a contractor has been running on compromised infrastructure for some portion of the last six months. She needs to know the scope.

Her hands have not moved from their position on the table. Still, not rigid. The rapid eye movement has slowed.

She has sent the warning. She is already on the next problem.

That is the cost of the decision she just made and the size of the person who made it: she does not sit with what it means to have sent a message that works against the pool she has been contracted to. She does the next thing.

Outside: the service quarter three blocks north, lit against the dark. Somewhere in there, the trap is either closing or it has already closed.

She sends the second message.
