```fountain
The ORACLE PRIME monitor on the secondary console — a network terminal the relay station had, still has, still connected on a read-only commercial data feed — updates.

The odds line moves.

Not instantly: it takes four seconds for the honesty protocol to process the new data and re-price the position estimate. Then the number shifts. The contestant's estimated position: eastern approach corridor, junction node 7. Confidence interval: high. The Herald's voice, wherever it is being broadcast right now, is saying this to three hundred thousand people.

It is saying it because it is true.

It cannot say otherwise. It has no mechanism for otherwise. It was designed to be impossible to lie to, and that design is, right now, lying on behalf of the person the design was built to hunt.

Nadia reads the number.

She closes the monitor feed.

She sets the transmitter to passive broadcast — it will continue pinging the false position on a twelve-minute cycle, keeping the odds stable, until the grid's own spatial sensors accumulate enough contradictory data to override it. Two hours, maybe three. The coverage cycle for the eastern corridor will produce contradictions before the northwest approach does. She is counting on this. The false signal will hold longest precisely where it matters least.

She does not linger.

One breath. The plan she mapped in a staff room nine days ago — watch face-up, the shape of what would be needed not yet the plan itself — is running. The structure she identified then is executing now. Not the version she imagined then. The version that the available materials permitted. That is the only version that exists.

She stands.

---

Zara is at the door. She sees Nadia stand. She does not ask.

ZARA
Two hours?

NADIA
Less. Move now.

They go. The transmitter pulses once behind them, sending its false coordinates into the dark, and The Herald, somewhere, tells its audience exactly where it has been told to look.


EXT. EASTERN APPROACH CORRIDOR — JUNCTION DISTRICT — NIGHT

She has been in the corridor for eleven minutes.

The corridor runs east-west through the junction district: transit shelters, freight handling bays, a run of service-entrance facades that back onto the commercial logistics grid. The grid cameras cover the approach at eighty-meter intervals. She has walked past four of them staying at the edge of their coverage cone — not outside it, at the edge. Present but not centered. A shape among other shapes.

The camera at junction node 7's access junction covers a thirty-meter stretch of open pavement. No shelter. No interference. A clean frame.

She needs to be in that frame for four seconds.

---

The hunter team is east of the junction — she identified them at a distance by their spacing, two on the street and one positioned high. Professional deployment. They are covering the approach she would use if the broadcast signal were accurate and she were actually here running.

She is here running. That is the thing she is doing.

She times herself against the team's pattern. Their sweep interval is ninety seconds. She has the frame window she needs between the high position's line of sight rotating east and the two-man sweep coming back around. Twenty-two seconds. The camera will get her in the first four.

She steps into the junction.

---

The pavement is lit. She does not run — not yet. Running in an empty street is its own signal. She walks with the specific quality of someone who has somewhere to be and is not certain she will get there, which is precisely true.

One second. Two.

The high position will rotate back in eighteen seconds.

Three. Four.

She turns east and runs.

---

Not a sprint — wrong signature. The run of someone who has just realized the risk she's taken being out in the open, not someone executing an operation. The distinction is in the shoulders: she drops them. She lets the posture communicate what the posture should communicate. She is running because she is frightened. She is frightened because she is here, and she is here because the plan required her to be.

Both of these things are true.

The high position rotates back. She hears the radio burst — short, compressed, the call that says: contact.

She runs east. Then north. Away from VELTAIN. The direction that makes sense for someone trying to lose a hunter team and failing to think about what comes after.

Behind her, two sets of footsteps. The sweep team. They are fast.
```
