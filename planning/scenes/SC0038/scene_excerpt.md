EXT. MARKET DISTRICT — CENTRAL RELAY PILLAR — DUSK

The market district is everything the transit zone was not: noisy, dense, too many bodies to track individually. Stalls still open under portable lighting. The smell of food and fuel and the particular density of people who are not going home yet.

NADIA moves through it at civilian pace — not hurrying, not browsing, holding the texture of someone crossing to somewhere else. The crowd is useful. The crowd is also a source she cannot read fast enough.

At the district's central intersection: a HALO UNIT relay pillar, taller than the standard civic array, fitted with a broadcast speaker grid and two directional screens facing the pedestrian flow. It is the kind of installation that exists in every major public space in the city — part surveillance, part civic service, part broadcast relay.

As she approaches, the screens activate.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
(warm, the sports-broadcast warmth, the comfortable voice of shared excitement)
And here's the update you've been waiting for. As of eighteen hundred hours, the bounty has tripled. Triple. For those of you who have been following since the beginning — welcome to the number you were hoping for.

She does not stop. She adjusts her route to pass within the relay's range. This is deliberate: she wants the information. She can get it from here without accessing any device that can locate her.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
(continuing, the rhythm building, reaching broadcast peaks)
Our audience numbers — and this is significant — our audience numbers have crossed the threshold we were watching. Seventeen percent above the previous record. The interest is real, the investment is real, and I want to be transparent with you: new entrants to the hunter pool have registered in the last three hours. The field is not shrinking. The field is growing.

She passes a stall selling printed scarves. She does not look at it. She is listening to the math.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Now, here's what's interesting — the odds have adjusted accordingly. The adjustment is substantial. Our contestant has performed at a level that has made the audience more invested, which has made the bounty more attractive, which has brought new professionals into the field. This is the nature of the event. Growth breeds growth.

A woman at the next stall looks up at the broadcast screens with the relaxed interest of someone watching a familiar program. She is not listening to the words. She is reading the familiar shape of the broadcast.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
We continue to recommend — to all registered participants — patience and coordination. The field is experienced. Our contestant is experienced. This is going to be worth watching.

Nadia stops. Not because she has been told to stop. Because she has decided to ask.

She is standing at the edge of the relay pillar's broadcast zone. Close enough. She speaks at a volume the relay will pick up.

NADIA
What are the current survival odds.

Not a question. A request for a data point.

A beat.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
(the warmth recedes by a fraction; the delivery becomes precise; the syllables are clipped rather than performed)
Current odds: four point three to one against survival past seventy-two hours.

The number lands.

She does not react to the number. She holds it without flinching, without shifting her weight, without giving the broadcast's audience anything to analyze.

The relay screens show the number in clean figures — 4.3:1. Below it, the audience count, still climbing.

The silence lasts four seconds. The Herald allows it: this is content; the landing is the point.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
(the warmth returning, the broadcast register reassembling around the data)
Our audience appreciates your resilience, Nadia. They genuinely do. You are the reason they are here.

She walks away from the pillar.

She does not walk fast.

She is thinking about something that has nothing to do with the number, which she already knew would be bad and is bad — thinking instead about the second before the number arrived, the fractional shift in The Herald's voice, the way the performance-overlay thinned for one beat and something else answered.

She does not know what to call what she noticed.

She walks. Four point three to one. The market closes around her. Somewhere in the relay network, the audience count is still climbing.
