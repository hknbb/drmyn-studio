INT. 14 HAVEL COURT — NORTHERN SERVICE QUARTER, LINDEN JUNCTION — THIRD FLOOR — DUSK

The staircase is empty. The building has the quality of a space that has been recently occupied and recently vacated — functional, institutional, no history on the walls.

Third floor. East-facing unit. The door handle is clean.

She says the contact phrase through the door.

A pause. Then the door opens. A man — fifty, grey, reading glasses in his hand — steps back without speaking. She reads him: low heart rate, no lateral eye movement, the faint calibration of someone who does this regularly. She cannot determine if he is genuine or Roman's. She moves past him.

One scan. West wall: exit, the way she came. East window: fire escape access, two floors down to the alley. North interior: bathroom, one window, opaque. South wall: the supply array.

She begins.

SUPPLY MANIFEST — CONFIRMED ON-SITE

She works against the list in her head. Medical first.

The suture kit is pre-threaded and pre-staged for a penetration wound. Not a general kit. Specific. Someone who knew the wound type built this.

She takes it.

Antibiotic: blister pack, broad-spectrum. She takes it.

Wound dressing: non-adhesive, the correct size for an entry wound rather than an abrasion or a burn. She takes it.

Communication hardware: a relay device, pre-keyed on the east-window side of the supply table. Positioned to transmit passively outward through the glass. She picks it up, turns it face-down, and places it in her bag. If it is transmitting, it is transmitting in a direction she controls now.

Clothing: her size, active rating. She notes the fit without remarking on it. She takes two items.

She checks the bathroom. Second supply set — duplicated. Medical. Same sizes. Same brands.

She stands in the doorway for two seconds.

No genuine rescue operation builds this redundancy. You do not double-stock if you are assembling at speed. You stock what you have. You stock what the subject is likely to need. You stock once. This is a space where the subject was expected to spend time and require a second round.

Three markers: suture kit (wound-specific), relay device (positioned for transmission), supply duplication (designed for extended stay, not rapid extraction).

She already knew. She is confirming.

Seven minutes in the space.

She is repacking the bag when she hears the contact phrase — spoken at the building entrance, one floor below, by a voice she knows before the sentence ends.

She is moving before she decides to move.

Third-floor landing. She positions herself at the top of the staircase. He is coming up.

TOBIAS ORELL appears around the turn of the second-floor stairs — medical bag on one shoulder, moving with the specific unhurried pace of someone who has learned to look unhurried — and stops when he sees her.

A beat.

TOBIAS
This the forward cache, or...

He trails off. He is reading her face. She does not have a face that is easy to read; she has learned that. He reads it anyway.

She says:

NADIA
This place is Roman's. Leave.

Tobias goes still.

He closes his eyes — brief, private, the reset — and opens them.

TOBIAS
What did you take.

NADIA
Medical. Hardware. Two clothing items. Nothing activated as far as I can determine.

TOBIAS
How long were you inside.

NADIA
Seven minutes.

TOBIAS
You think they logged me at the door.

She does not answer because the answer is yes and he knows the answer is yes.

He looks at the bag over her shoulder, then at the door to the unit behind her, then at the staircase he came up. His hands find his medical kit strap and adjust it with precise, unnecessary attention.

TOBIAS
I came in on the ground channel. Same relay you used.

NADIA
Different message.

TOBIAS
Supply cache for a VELTAIN contact. That's what it said. I thought you might—

He stops.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
I thought you might need it. Before the perimeter.

The building is quiet. Whatever is in the walls is quiet too.

She says:

NADIA
We need to move now. Together or separately — your choice. If separately, go north. Not the corridor route.

Tobias picks up his bag, adjusts the strap one more time, and nods.

They are both in the building. The building knows they are both in the building. The warning was real and it arrived thirty seconds after it would have helped.
