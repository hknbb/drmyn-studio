EXT. RENNMARK DISTRICT — INDUSTRIAL ZONE — CONTINUOUS

Four minutes since the broadcast. She has been moving since before the broadcast finished.

The district is a grid of functional ugly: refurbishment units, materials storage, ducting infrastructure, the kind of maintenance corridors that exist because the district needs to breathe, not because anyone planned them as pathways. She has been reading this grid since SC0022. Surveillance intervals, coverage gaps, which passages carry HALO UNIT rotation and which carry only behavioral monitoring — the assumption that no one goes there, which is different from the fact.

She has a map. The map is four days of accumulated observation. The sweep pattern she is moving through now was not part of the map when she made it.

It is part of the map now.

---

What she can read from the positioning:

The sweep is not a search. A search looks for presence. This sweep covers exit vectors — the hunters have assumed she is trying to leave the district, and they have positioned to intercept departure rather than discover location. That is a different intelligence model. It means the sweep was planned, not improvised. Someone looked at the district's exit geometry and covered it.

MINA DRASC is at the north end, near the transit junction. She has a notebook open. She is consulting something in it. The notebook's presence visible from sixty meters is itself information: she is checking a model against reality, updating as she moves.

VEKA DRASC is moving south along the main maintenance corridor — the obvious route. She moves into rooms as if already owning them, which makes her visible but also means she occupies space aggressively and leaves less of it for anyone trying to pass.

The obvious route is watched.

Nadia does not take the obvious route.

---

The first gap is the ducting maintenance access behind the three-unit block — forty centimeters wide, behavioral monitoring only, no HALO UNIT rotation in this corridor. She noted it in SC0022 during the nine-second recalibration. She presses flat and moves through it at the pace that doesn't register as urgency.

The second gap is the refurbishment unit rear access on the east side — she cuts through it using the same approach geometry she built for the Tobias unit. Pattern recognition in the district's architecture: all rear accesses have the same setback from the alley. She knows exactly where the door will be.

She is past both gaps before either hunter has closed the angle.

---

The third gap.

The narrow passage between the materials storage units on the north perimeter — the one that leads to the transit junction gap. Optimal route. Sixty-three seconds to the perimeter from here.

She reaches the entrance. Checks the angle.

She cannot take it.

The rib bruising on her left side — Tobias's words: it will limit your range of motion on the left side — means she cannot press the left wall of the passage at speed for the last twelve meters, where the passage narrows. The passage requires it. She has been managing the limitation across the last four minutes by choosing routes that don't require left-wall pressure at velocity. This one requires it.

She reroutes.

The reroute costs twelve seconds. She knows exactly how many seconds it costs because she mapped the alternative and counted.

---

The ventilation passage is not on her map.

She turns into it because it connects to the passage she needs and she has never seen anyone use it. Behavioral monitoring only. No HALO UNIT. A gap in the district's geometry that is functionally invisible because nothing in the district's design requires anyone to use it for anything.

VEKA DRASC is already in it.

She took a shortcut. Veka does this — moves into spaces as if already owning them, including spaces the sweep plan did not route her through. Mina would not have approved this deviation from the planned geometry.

They are face-to-face.

The distance between them is four meters.

---

One second.

Both register the other completely: height, clothing, face, gait signature from the approach. The recognition is mutual and instant and requires nothing from either of them beyond open eyes.

Nadia is already in motion before Veka's right hand reaches for comms.

She does not run. Running produces a specific acoustic and visual signature that the HALO UNIT network is calibrated for. She moves at the fastest pace that does not register as flight — the pace of someone with a destination, not the pace of someone being chased.

She exits the ventilation passage at the far end.

She is past the north perimeter in forty-one seconds.
