INT. KASPAR TERMINAL — STAFF ROOM — NIGHT

Nadia is standing at the break table. Her watch is face-up on the surface. She is not looking at it.

She is working.

The broadcast system has three functional components. She has been treating them as a single threat. That was a mistake — not a catastrophic one, she has been surviving correctly inside the error — but a mistake. She corrects it now.

One: The Herald. Visible-facing. Performance engine and delivery mechanism. Warmth register, live betting odds, location tracking, contestant status updates. Designed to be watched. Designed to feel like a narrator rather than a machine.

Two: The grid. The physical monitoring infrastructure — cameras, signal receivers, proximity sensors distributed through the broadcast zone. What feeds The Herald. She has spent nineteen hours learning where the grid cannot see. She knows four dead spots by location and two more by inference.

Three: The audience. Three hundred thousand active viewers as of the last odds cycle. They have been betting on her survival. The odds have been moving in her favor.

She stops.

She had not thought about the audience as a structural component before. She had thought about them the way she thought about the terminal hum — as context, not resource.

Three hundred thousand people watching whether she lives.

The odds moving in her favor means money flowing into Roman's system on the bet that she survives. He profits from her survival as long as the game continues. He profits more from her death at the correct moment, but sustained survival with growing audience investment is not nothing to him. She is not useless to him alive. She is useful to him in a specific, time-limited way.

This is leverage. She is carrying it. She has been carrying it without knowing she was.

She picks up her watch. She puts it back down.

The Herald cannot fabricate numerical data. She had noticed this weeks ago — a fractional shift in its delivery register, the broadcast performance-overlay thinning for one beat, the warmth dropping out and something more precise taking its place. She had filed the observation without a use for it.

She has a use for it now.

Three structural constraints. She enumerates them the way she would run a differential on a patient presenting something she has not seen before — systematically, without shortcuts, starting from what she knows is true:

One: The Herald cannot fabricate live betting odds. The numbers it reports are the numbers the grid produces. If the grid can be fed false position data that registers as genuine, The Herald will accurately broadcast that deception. Roman's network will respond to what The Herald tells them — because they have designed a system that cannot lie to them, which means they have designed a system they have to believe.

Two: The Herald's honesty protocol has a legible tell. The register shift is readable. She noticed it; she is not uniquely attentive. Anyone who has been watching this broadcast long enough with the right kind of attention — not as a bettor, but as someone trying to understand what the machine is doing — has noticed it too. The tell marks when The Herald is constrained by data versus when it is performing. Real-data moments are evidentiarily reliable. They can be cited.

Three: The audience record cannot be suppressed. Every bet placed on her survival is a timestamped transaction logged inside Roman's system. If any part of that record reaches outside the platform — if a single extract of the odds archive leaves the broadcast ecosystem — it documents the game's operation in Roman's own numbers. Not an allegation. A ledger.

The audience is not only Roman's resource. They are also her witness.

She is very still.

The plan is not complete. What she has is the shape of what the plan will need: false position signals fed into the grid; The Herald accurately reporting her deception to Roman's network; the odds record leaving the platform and reaching someone who can read it. She will need to be in the broadcast zone as an active participant to feed the grid. Not hiding from the system. Using it.

She will be visible. This is the cost of the plan.

She already started paying it when she said come inside and Chen Mei crossed the threshold and a camera above the north bay door recorded her arrival. The performance of invisibility ended then. She is already visible to anyone watching carefully enough. The cost has been running since the corridor.

She did not know that then. She knows it now.

She straightens. Not parade-ground — something else. The uprightness of function. No performance in it, no managed presentation. She is not maintaining anything for anyone's perception. She is simply standing the way you stand when you are about to work.

The mask is not gone because she decided to remove it. It is gone because she stopped needing it. It dissolved when she stopped asking whether she could survive this and started asking how she was going to use it. The compliance posture has nothing to maintain itself against.

She places her watch on her wrist.

Thirty-six minutes until the next broadcast checkpoint.

She is a hunter now. She has not started moving yet.
