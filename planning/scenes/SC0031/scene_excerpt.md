Behind her:

THE HERALD (V.O.)
(broadcast register, quick, warm)
Now, here's what's interesting — we have a confirmed visual on our contestant. Full identification. Nadia Vale, confirmed current, Rennmark district perimeter, moving north. Our audience now has a face to go with the name. The field knows who they're looking for.

The HALO UNIT at the district perimeter carries the broadcast outward, behind her, into the district she has already left.

She hears it at forty meters clear of the boundary.

She absorbs it the way she absorbs everything: as a fact, with operational implications, to be assessed in motion.

Anonymous prey. Named target.

The distinction is not abstract. The full broadcast audience now has her face. They will recognize her in any district, on any transit line, in any space where a HALO UNIT is present and the broadcast is running.

She catalogues the cost. She does not stop.

She continues north.


EXT. RENNMARK DISTRICT — NORTH EDGE — CONTINUOUS

The city opens here. The district's density gives way to a wider street, fewer HALO UNIT housings, longer sightlines. The kind of space where presence becomes visible.

NADIA stops at the edge.

TOBIAS stops two paces behind her. He has come this far without being asked. He is not going further.

She reads the open street. Entry geometry. Cover intervals. The grid is in her head now, updated.

Her left side: managed. She does not press the wall but there is no wall to press here — the constraint is academic for another thirty seconds, and she is cataloguing exactly that.

She turns to face him. Not a farewell look — an assessment look. She is checking he understands what happens now.

He does.

The distance between them is the width of a doorway. She notices that he has not stepped into it.

TOBIAS takes a breath that sounds like the beginning of a sentence.

He doesn't say anything.

He reaches into his coat pocket. Produces a folded piece of paper. Holds it out.

She looks at it for a moment. Then she takes it. Her right hand. The paper disappears into her jacket without being opened.

She doesn't ask what it is.

He doesn't tell her.

He starts to say something. His mouth opens. The word — whatever it was — doesn't come. He closes it again.

She looks at him for one more second. The register is not warm and not cold. It is the register of someone who has done the accounting and accepted the result.

Then she turns and walks into the open street.

She does not look back.

His figure reduces to a point in the space she walked through. Then the distance makes that irrelevant.

She carries the paper in her right hand — coat pocket now — and the rib cage makes the left side a continuous note she reads without acting on.

Anonymous prey. Named target. Purposeful isolation.

The distinction, again, is not abstract.

She continues north.
