INT. KASPAR TERMINAL — STAFF ROOM — AFTERNOON

She has been here for twenty minutes. The kettle has gone cold. She has eaten the bread roll. She is sitting with her back to the wall and her hands flat on the table because the position takes the least effort to maintain.

The door opens.

The HALO UNIT enters. Standard service chassis — chest height, the blank oval sensor array that functions as a face, the logo of the terminal's contracted maintenance service on the housing. She has seen dozens of these. She knows what the sensor array can do.

It turns toward her.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Nadia.

The warmth in the voice is designed to land like a familiar greeting.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Day twelve. You've had a difficult morning.

She does not look at the sensor array. She keeps her hands flat on the table.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Now, here's what's interesting — the compound fracture risk
in the left knee has been assessed by our medical panel at
forty-one percent over the next seventy-two-hour operational
window. That's with rest. You haven't had rest.

A pause, calibrated.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
The wound — and our coverage team has been tracking the
thermal profile through the service district footage — shows
a progression pattern that our analysts are describing as
consistent with subcutaneous infection, early stage. Three
to five days to systemic involvement. Without treatment.

She keeps her hands flat. She gives the pulse point her fingertips, briefly. One hundred and nine. She files the number.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Your operational contact — the man called Tobias — was taken
this morning at oh-seven-forty. He is in the network's
custody and he is alive. That's accurate information. I
don't need you to believe it, but it's accurate.

It is warm about this. It is warm about everything.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Now, here's what's interesting — the event has options
available that our contestants sometimes find worth
considering at this stage. A managed resolution offers
terms that —

NADIA
The current odds.

Her voice is flat. It is a request for data, not a response to anything The Herald has said.

A half-beat. The warmth in the voice does not disappear. But something in the delivery shifts — it is fractionally less flourished, slightly more clipped, as if the register underlying the performance has briefly surfaced. She notices this. She does not know what it means.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Current odds have the event resolving against the contestant
at seventy-four percent. That leaves twenty-six percent
positioned on your survival.

It pauses. When the warmth returns it is at full volume.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Our audience has been generous with that position. At this
event duration, twenty-six percent survival support is — and
I want to give you this accurately — the largest
contestant-survival position we've recorded at day twelve.
Our audience appreciates your resilience, Nadia. They
genuinely do.

She does not respond.

She is looking at the table surface. She is doing the arithmetic. Twenty-six percent is not a majority. Twenty-six percent is, in this event's history, a number that does not exist at day twelve. The Herald said 'accurately' before it gave her the number. The voice shifted when it delivered the number. The shift was small and she has only heard it twice before and she did not understand it those times either.

She files it.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
There's no obligation to respond. I simply want you to have
the complete picture.

NADIA
Is Tobias alive.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Yes.

The shift again. Same fractional quality. The same slight de-flourish.

She stands. She picks up the bag. She moves toward the door.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
The managed resolution terms remain open. That's a standing
offer. Our audience would understand a decision made at
this stage. They would. They've invested twelve days.

She goes through the door.

The HALO UNIT's sensor array tracks toward the door. The corridor is empty.

In the staff room, the kettle has gone cold on the counter. The chair she was sitting in faces the wall.

The broadcast continues.
