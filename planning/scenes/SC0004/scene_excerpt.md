INT. RENTED ROOM — VARDOVA EAST QUARTER — AFTERNOON

A single window, curtain half-drawn. A table, two chairs, a bed that has not been slept in. The room belongs to no one. It has been chosen for that.

MARCUS CHEN stands near the window — not at it. A foot back, where he can see the glass without being seen through it. He is not making a performance of this. It is simply how he stands.

NADIA sits at the table. She has not taken off her coat.

They have been here perhaps twenty minutes. The silence between them is not uncomfortable. It is the silence of two people who have already said the difficult things and are now in the space after.

MARCUS
How long do you have.

NADIA
An hour. Less if Birta calls.

He nods. Moves from the window. Sits across from her. His hands are on the table, loose.

She looks at his hands. He has a small scar along his right index finger — she knows the story of it, has known it for two years. There are things about him she has memorized without meaning to.

MARCUS
He's watching more carefully.

NADIA
Roman.

MARCUS
The household staff changed twice in six months. That's not turnover. That's placement.

She already knew this. She let him say it anyway.

NADIA
I know.

MARCUS
Karev.

She looks up. He only uses her maiden name when he means it.

MARCUS (CONT'D)
I want a backup.

He takes a scrap of paper from his jacket — torn from something, not prepared, a small torn corner — and writes on it. Efficient. No hesitation. He knows the address by heart.

He slides it across the table.

MARCUS (CONT'D)
Merin old quay. Unit fourteen-B. Man named Otto. Tell him I sent you.

She picks it up. Reads it. Does not look at him.

NADIA
This is a backup for what.

MARCUS
I like redundancies.

His tone is practical. She has heard him use this tone before — clean, operational, the version of himself he uses for briefings. She is not fooled by it and he knows she is not fooled by it.

She folds the paper and puts it in her coat pocket without further comment.

The silence comes back. Different now.

He reaches across the table and covers her hand with his. She turns her hand over so their palms meet — not romantic staging, just the thing itself, two people in a room that belongs to neither of them.

MARCUS (CONT'D)
Go.

He says it quietly. It is not goodbye. It is a direction.

NADIA
Not yet.

She stays. He lets her.

Outside the half-drawn curtain, VARDOVA goes about its afternoon.

Marcus touches the back of his neck once — a brief, private gesture — and then his hand comes back to the table.

She sees it. She will not understand what it meant until later.
