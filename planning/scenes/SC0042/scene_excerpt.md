INT. ROMAN'S OPERATIONAL OFFICE — HIGH FLOOR — NIGHT

A room that is not a room so much as a position. Floor-to-ceiling glass on the city side. ROMAN VALE stands at the window with his back to the room, which is not a concession — it means he has decided the window is the more interesting view than whoever is behind him.

DIMITRI KOSS sits at the table with a slim folder open in front of him and reads from it with the posture of a man who has rehearsed this delivery and is executing it now with professional precision.

DIMITRI
The exposure model runs through seventy-two hours at an acceptable yield — institutional monitoring at current levels, containment probability above threshold. Past that window the curve compounds. At ninety-six hours we are looking at a seventeen-percent increase in secondary-channel exposure — media adjacency, regulatory trigger probability, the Halverson audit that is already running. At one hundred and twenty hours we breach the containment threshold. The recommendation is to compress the operational window: accept reduced outcome yield, close cleanly before the curve inflects.

He closes the folder.

DIMITRI (CONT'D)
The numbers are modeled off the current operational footprint. They don't account for escalation.

Roman does not turn from the window.

A long beat. The city below is a grid of light that does not require his attention, and he is giving it his attention anyway, which means he is not giving it his attention.

Then he turns.

ROMAN
Walk me through the yield reduction.

DIMITRI
Twelve to eighteen percent on the primary outcome. Higher on demonstration value — the compressed timeline removes the public-facing elements. The event closes as a private transaction rather than a—

ROMAN
A demonstration.

DIMITRI
Yes.

Roman moves from the window to the table. He does not sit. He reads the top page of the folder for a moment, then sets it down and pushes it back toward Dimitri with two fingers — not dismissive, precise.

ROMAN
Understand — the exposure curve is correct. I'm not arguing the numbers.

DIMITRI
The numbers are conservative.

ROMAN
I know they're conservative. The question is what the operation is for.

Dimitri holds this without speaking.

ROMAN (CONT'D)
A private transaction closes a file. That is not what this is. The people who need to understand the cost of this situation need to understand it in a way that cannot be summarized or reframed. That requires the demonstration. Compressing the timeline eliminates the demonstration.

He moves back toward the window.

ROMAN (CONT'D)
The exposure risk is a variable, not a ceiling. We have managed seventeen-percent exposure increases before. Adjust the containment protocols to the extended window and bring me the revised model in the morning.

A beat.

ROMAN (CONT'D)
Understand — the timeline does not compress.

Dimitri holds for a moment that is exactly as long as the situation requires. Then:

DIMITRI
Understood. I'll adjust the containment protocols to the extended window.

Roman has already returned his attention to the city.

Dimitri closes the folder. He sets it to the side. He reaches into his jacket and removes a slim book — narrow, dark, the kind used for field notation — and opens it to a page that is already partially filled.

He writes.

We cannot see what he writes. We see the act: the date — the time — a pause in which he appears to consider something — then several lines, unhurried, in a hand that has clearly filled many pages of similar books. He is not recording this for the operation. He is recording this for a reckoning he has not yet specified.

He closes the book.

He replaces it in his jacket.

He picks up the folder and walks to the door.

DIMITRI
Good night.

Roman does not respond. He is looking at the city, which is organized below him in a way that confirms everything he believes about the relationship between position and perspective.

The door closes.

The room returns to the sound of a city that does not know whose window it is being seen from.
