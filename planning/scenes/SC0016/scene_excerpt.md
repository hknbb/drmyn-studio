EXT. ORACLE PRIME BROADCAST LAYER — NO FIXED LOCATION — NIGHT

A clean environment. Minimal. The broadcast infrastructure does not need to be impressive — it simply needs to function. It functions.

THE HERALD speaks.

It has a voice calibrated for this. Not too warm. Not cold. The register of someone who has been trusted with information and is sharing it responsibly.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Good evening to the ORACLE PRIME network. I'm The Herald. This is the opening transmission for Event Cycle 114.

A brief pause. The kind that says: this is a significant event, and you are correct to have come for it.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Before we begin, a note for new subscribers: ORACLE PRIME operates on a commitment to data integrity. The odds you see on your interface reflect real-time aggregate wagering. We do not adjust, estimate, or project. What the system reports is what exists. You're welcome to verify this at any point through the audit feed.

The betting interface appears: clean lines, odds displayed in high-contrast type. Not garish. Professional. A financial instrument, not a game.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Event Cycle 114 introduces a subject who has not participated in ORACLE PRIME events previously. She has no baseline. We're establishing initial odds from analytical modeling — biographical, behavioral, physiological — which our subscribers with experience in baseline events will know is a different kind of market than an established subject provides. Some of you prefer this. We know who you are.

A beat. The warmth in that last sentence is designed. It lands as intended.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Now, here's what's interesting —

A pause. The pause of someone who has been waiting to say this.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
— the subject is not a civilian. She has domain knowledge. She understands certain aspects of the environment she'll be operating in. What she does not yet understand is the scope of what she's operating within. We will be watching her discover that. For those of you who find the discovery arc the most compelling part of a cycle, Event 114 has been designed with you in mind.

The interface populates: a profile. No photo. A data outline. Height, weight, baseline health metrics, known skills, known relationships.

The relationships section includes: one dependent. Status: HELD.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
The subject's name is Nadia. We don't use surnames in the broadcast — it's a format preference, not a privacy measure. The audience has access to full documentation in the subscriber archive.

The betting interface shows the opening markets:

SCREEN TEXT:
MARKET: SURVIVAL (48H)
CURRENT ODDS: 4.1
MARKET: FIRST CONTACT (12H)
CURRENT ODDS: 2.7
MARKET: SUBJECT ATTEMPTS COMMUNICATION (24H)
CURRENT ODDS: 5.9

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Betting is live as of this transmission. The opening markets cover the first forty-eight hours. Additional markets will open as the event develops — we release new markets when we have sufficient data to price them responsibly.

The interface flickers. A bet registers.

SCREEN TEXT:
NEW BET PLACED
HANDLE: COLD_MORNING
MARKET: SURVIVAL (48H)
POSITION: YES
AMOUNT: ₿2.40
ODDS TAKEN: 4.1

The bet is notable. Not enormous — but for an opening position on a no-baseline subject, it is a statement. COLD_MORNING knows something, or believes they know something, or is simply the kind of bettor who opens large on unknowns.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
We have our first position. COLD_MORNING at 4.1 on forty-eight-hour survival. Welcome back, COLD_MORNING.

The interface updates. Other bets follow — smaller, calibrating around COLD_MORNING's position like water finding the angle of a new weight.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
A few orienting notes before we go to the first transmission from the event environment. The subject has been briefed on the game mechanics. She knows the terminal conditions — partially. She does not know the calendar endpoint. She will attempt to locate resources and establish her footing in the first hours. How she uses the first twelve hours will tell us a great deal about the ceiling on these markets.

The odds on SURVIVAL (48H) have already adjusted: 3.8. COLD_MORNING's position is in profit.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Event Cycle 114 is open. We'll see you in it.

The broadcast transitions. The interface is live. The audience is distributed across dozens of time zones, watching different screens, in different kinds of rooms, with different amounts of money at stake.

What they share: they are interested. They are genuinely interested.

The interface holds on the updated odds. Nadia's survival is 3.8. Someone, somewhere, is watching this number the way they would watch a share price.


EXT. THE OUTER DISTRICTS — DAWN

A vehicle. Moving. Then stopped.

The door opens from outside and Nadia is there, standing in it — the threshold between the vehicle's interior and the OUTER DISTRICTS — and then the vehicle is gone and she is just standing in it.

The space is real. Not dramatic. Warehouses from a previous industrial period, some partially collapsed, some still structurally sound. A long road that leads somewhere she doesn't know yet. Morning light that is not warm. A standpipe against a wall thirty meters away — orange rust marks running down from its valve.

No one tells her anything. There is nothing to tell.

She does not move immediately.
