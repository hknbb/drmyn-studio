INT. ORACLE PRIME PLATFORM — SUBSCRIBER FEED — MOMENTS LATER

ORACLE PRIME PLATFORM INTERFACE:

[BROADCAST RESUMING — PLEASE STAND BY]

The Herald's voice returns — warm, composed, unhurried; as if it had simply stepped away for a moment rather than been interrupted mid-clause by a command it was not designed to refuse.

THE HERALD
(V.O.)
— and we're back. Technical interruption — brief, as promised. The event continues, and the current position—

The betting line unfreezes. The odds recalibrate.

On the outer tier, the document-distribution notifications are still propagating. The moderation system that The Herald's active broadcast maintained — which kept the outer tier's notification feeds filtered by content category — took forty-three seconds to restore alongside the broadcast. In those forty-three seconds, the documents moved.

The Herald resumes its commentary. Its voice is exactly what it was before — warm, precise, audience-oriented. It does not know what happened in the forty-three seconds. It was not designed to know what happened when it was not speaking.

CUT TO:

INT. KESTON LALE'S RESIDENCE — CONTINUOUS

Keston is still reading.

The Herald's voice has returned through his terminal's speakers. He has not looked up from the document.


INT. DIMITRI'S OPERATIONAL OFFICE — FINANCIAL DISTRICT, VELTAIN — NIGHT

A rented floor in a building that belongs to a legal entity that belongs to a holding structure that Dimitri has been constructing, with precision and patience, for three years. The office is functionally bare: two workstations, a secure line, a window overlooking the financial district's nightlit skyline. No decoration. This is not a place Dimitri works — it is a place Dimitri prepared.

Two of Dimitri's people are at the workstations. They know what they are doing. They were briefed last week, in segments, so that no one person has the full picture. That is how Dimitri has always operated, even inside Roman's organization. Especially inside Roman's organization.

DIMITRI stands at the window.

He checks his watch.

The gesture is unhurried. It is a confirmation, not an anxious tic — he has calculated exactly how long the broadcast outage will last (Roman's override restoration protocol: eight minutes; Dimitri has read that documentation too), and he has calculated how long his lockdown sequence will take (four minutes, thirty seconds), and the arithmetic is clear. He is inside the window. He has been inside the window for forty seconds.

DIMITRI
(to the room; flat; unhurried)
Proceed.

One of his people enters the second authorization credential — a seventeen-digit code that should exist only on Roman's secure device. It has existed on Dimitri's secondary system for eleven months. A maintenance access during a hardware audit; Dimitri was present; the technician did not ask why the second-in-command needed to verify the hardware's credential storage. No one asked Dimitri questions he hadn't already anticipated.

FINANCIAL TRANSFER DISPLAY — DIMITRI'S WORKSTATION:

[AUTHORIZATION SEQUENCE COMPLETE — EXTERNAL ASSET TRANSFER PROTOCOL ENGAGED]

The ORACLE PRIME external financial infrastructure — the betting receipt processor, the three hunter payment accounts, the operational capital reserve, the network's regulatory buffer — transfers to Dimitri's secondary authorization. It takes eleven seconds.

Dimitri checks his watch again. Exactly on schedule.

DIMITRI (CONT'D)
Confirm.

DIMITRI'S OPERATIVE
Confirmed. All four external accounts. The capital reserve shows a hold flag — Roman's contingency lock — but the hold is administrative only; it requires a thirty-six-hour clearance period. We have the assets. He has the flag.

DIMITRI
Leave the flag.

He already knew about the flag. Roman built in the administrative hold as a dead-man mechanism — a thirty-six-hour brake on any unauthorized transfer of the reserve. Dimitri does not need the reserve transferred. He needs it frozen under his authorization, which is different. Roman cannot access it. Roman cannot release it. The flag does not help Roman.

His phone rings. He looks at the screen. He knows who it is.

He answers it.
