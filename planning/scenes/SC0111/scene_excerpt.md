INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — ANTECHAMBER CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS

She is adjusted to her left-side deficit. He is watching how she is adjusted to it.

He comes in lower on the third approach — below the sternum contact she landed in SC0104, inside the deflection range her left arm can produce with compromised leverage. He is learning her compensation in real time. He has been in physical confrontations before. He has not been in confrontations with people who fight the way she fights, but he can read the movement and he is updating.

He uses the floor seam.

She knows it is there — he told her it was there in SC0102 when he mapped the corridor's geometry eleven years of muscle memory at a time. What she does not have is the exact position in this low light. He does. He drives her back toward it on a line that is not the shortest path to the server core wall — it is the path that ends at the seam.

She catches it on the outer edge of her right foot. Not a fall. A stagger. A quarter-second of weight displacement she cannot use.

He uses the quarter-second.

The door frame of his retreat catches her right shoulder. Not thrown — she is managed into it, the geometry doing the work. He is not wasting energy on force. He is spending effort on precision, the same way she has been, except he has the map and she is learning the map as the fight erases the options one by one.

Her back is to the server core door.

Her left arm is not reliably functional — the rib injury has shortened the arc of every movement on that side, and he has been methodically narrowing what the right arm can reach. The breathing cost is now operating-expense rather than overhead: every inhale is a decision with a price, and the prices are accumulating.

He has not hit her again in the last forty seconds. He does not need to. He is running out the clock on her margins.

She has no room to establish a counter-line. The geometry is closed.

Here is what she knows about carotid sinus pressure: the bifurcation of the common carotid artery, where the baroreceptors concentrate, sits two centimeters below the angle of the jaw on both sides. Bilateral sustained pressure triggers a reflex response that the autonomic nervous system cannot override — heart rate drops, blood pressure drops, the body's own threat-response shuts down the threat. A military medic learns this in the context of what not to do to a patient in shock. She learned what it does. She has been aware since the fight began that it was the one move available to her from maximum proximity with minimum arm clearance.

She stopped running from maximum proximity four exchanges ago.

She closes distance.

He does not expect it. She has been managing space, extending range, trying to recover room. The shift from retreat to approach takes a half-second he has not allocated — he was not watching for her to come toward him.

Both thumbs, at the bifurcation. She knows the location with the precision she knows every anatomical landmark she was trained to find fast in field conditions, on strangers, in the dark. His neck.

He tries to pull back. The distance is already closed.

She applies. Sustained. Both sides. The pressure is specific and deliberate — not a choke, not a strike: a bilateral hold targeting the baroreceptor reflex, maintained with whatever her arms have left.

Four seconds. He is still standing, his hands trying to find purchase, his body not yet understanding what is being asked of it.

Six seconds. His body understands.

Seven.

He goes down the way vasovagal response takes a person — not a fall, not a collapse, a controlled exit of the legs from load-bearing status. She goes with him, one knee on the corridor floor, because she does not have the margin to release cleanly and step back. She releases.

She tries to stand.

Her left side is settled into a specific, narrow, consuming pain — not acute anymore, categorical. She knows the difference between injury that prevents function and injury that taxes it. This is taxation at a rate she has not been charged before. She gets to one knee. She gives herself the count of three.

At three she stands.

Roman is on the corridor floor. Breathing — she can see the chest moving from here, the way she has been trained to see it from a distance in low light without equipment. He is not confirmed as to what comes next. The film does not resolve this here. She is not standing here long enough for resolution.

Jin is in the building.

She is standing. She moves.
