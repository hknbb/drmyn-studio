INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — CONTROL ROOM — CONTINUOUS

ROMAN has the financial intelligence on the wall display. He has been looking at it for twenty-two seconds — long enough to understand exactly what Dimitri has done and exactly how he did it.

He is on the phone.

ROMAN
(precise, without inflection; not a threat; a statement of the situation)
You have the external accounts. You don't have the compound. You don't have the technical infrastructure. You don't have the broadcast. What you have is four accounts that process receipts and a reserve with a thirty-six-hour hold. I can work around those in twenty minutes. The offer is: you release the reserve flag and take a formal partnership position in the post-event restructuring. You'll have more in six months than you have now.

A beat.

DIMITRI (V.O.)
(flat; no pause; he has been waiting fifteen years for this sentence)
You should have ended it when I told you to.

The call ends.

CUT TO:

INT. DIMITRI'S OPERATIONAL OFFICE — CONTINUOUS

Dimitri places the first phone on the desk. He picks up the second.

He dials. Waits one ring.

DIMITRI
(into second phone)
It's done. Move to phase two.

He hangs up the second phone. He looks out the window at the financial district's skyline — the same skyline Roman's compound overlooks from a different angle.

He checks his watch. He is on schedule.

CUT TO:

INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — CONTROL ROOM — CONTINUOUS

Roman is looking at the financial intelligence display.

He does not move immediately. He gives the situation one full beat — not hesitation; assessment. He has three active crises and he has been resolving them in the wrong order, and he knows this now, and he is recalibrating.

He turns to his team.

ROMAN
Dimitri's team will be in the financial district. Find them. And tell security: we have a second active breach.

He does not raise his voice. He does not need to.

For the first time in the film, Roman is responding to conditions he did not create.


INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — SERVICE CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS

Six minutes, forty seconds since the override.

NADIA is moving at the speed she has been calculating since the moment the broadcast went dark: not the fastest she can move, which would be too loud, but the fastest she can move quietly, which is something she worked out during military service and has not forgotten. The service corridor has not changed since she entered it. The HALO UNIT at the junction stands inert, optical arrays tracking her passage, transmitting nothing.

She keeps count without conscious attention, the way her body keeps heartbeat. Six forty-two. Six forty-three.

The left side is the problem. The wound from MERIN — closed by Tobias's sutures first, re-torn in the ZORAL CORRIDOR, currently held by four strips of closure tape she applied herself in a bathroom in MERIN three days ago — is pulling with every step that requires lateral extension. She has adjusted her stride to minimize the extension without reducing speed. The adjustment is not visible unless someone is looking for it. She has been making this adjustment for seventy-two hours.

She reads her own body the way she would read a patient: systematic, without sentiment. The MERIN wound: managed. Right hand, knuckles: not functionally limiting. Dehydration: present; not yet critical. Hunger: well past the point of discomfort; she has stopped feeling it, which is itself a symptom. Sleep: she has not had more than ninety minutes consecutively in four days. Her hands are steadier than they have any right to be. She knows why: proprioception improves under adrenaline-mediated sympathetic activation. She knows exactly how long that improvement lasts.

She turns left at the junction. Zone 1 ahead.
