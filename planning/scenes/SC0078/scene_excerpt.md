INT. KASPAR TERMINAL — STAFF ROOM — NIGHT

Twenty-three minutes until the broadcast checkpoint.

Nadia has been in this room for twenty hours. She knows its dimensions, its camera coverage, its sound profile, the temperature differential between the north wall and the south. She has been a subject in this space. She is now something else.

She builds the plan.

It has four components. She enumerates them the way she enumerates anything that matters: sequentially, without optimism, each one dependent on the one before it.

One: the broadcast checkpoint. She enters the broadcast zone actively. She feeds false position signals into the grid. The Herald reports them accurately. Roman's assets deploy toward the reported position. She is somewhere else. This is the misdirection layer, and it requires her to be visible in the zone before she can disappear inside it. The cost of this is that she will be targeted. She has already accepted this.

Two: Okafor. Inspector Okafor is the external legal pressure that runs independently of whether Nadia is alive or dead. Chen Mei is carrying the ledger to Okafor now. That chain is already moving. Roman cannot stop it by stopping Nadia. This is the only part of the plan that does not require her to survive it.

Three: Sera. Publication timed to the checkpoint. Sera has the documentation chain and the camera-gap confirmation and twenty years of investigative practice. She will know when. Nadia told her she would know when, and she was right to say it: the story will become self-evidently publishable the moment Nadia stops hiding and becomes a visible force inside the broadcast zone. Sera has been waiting seven months; she will not miss the moment.

Four: VELTAIN.

Marcus's intelligence package named it. Roman's primary operational infrastructure — the location from which the game is run, the network's executive nerve center, the place where Roman's presence is structural rather than intermittent. It is also where Jin is.

She has been carrying this since SC0074 opened Chen Mei's envelope. She has not said it aloud yet. She says it now, internally, as a fact:

VELTAIN is the objective.

Getting there requires Zara.

Zara Okonkwo is a hunter with route access, credentials in Roman's outer network, and a daughter named Lena whose debt to Roman is the mechanism by which Zara has been kept compliant. Nadia has watched Zara operate across nineteen hours of game architecture. Zara has intervened once without explanation. That intervention was not accidental.

Zara wants the same thing Nadia wants. Not the same thing — a different expression of the same structural need: to dismantle the machine that is eating her. She is operating inside Roman's system for the same reason Nadia was. She has not yet found the exit.

Nadia can show her the exit.

But only if the thing she offers is real. Not the promise of dismantling Roman — Zara knows better than to accept promises about futures. What Nadia can give her that is real is this: Inspector Okafor's name, and the confirmation that the ledger chain is already moving. Roman's leverage over Lena depends on a legal and financial trap. The ledger contains Dimitri's documentation of that trap's construction. When Okafor receives it, the trap begins to be dismantled from the outside — independently of anything Zara does or doesn't do, independently of whether Nadia survives, independently of whether the game ends cleanly or catastrophically.

Lena's freedom is already in motion. It was set in motion forty-one weeks ago, in the same chain Marcus built for every other piece of this. Zara does not know this yet.

This is what Nadia has to give her. It is real. It is the only thing that is real enough to earn what she needs.

She maps the risks.

Zara may refuse — if her distrust of Nadia is greater than her distrust of the system she is in, the alliance fails and the path to VELTAIN requires a different route. The broadcast plan may be interdicted before it completes — Roman's network is not incompetent, and the window for misdirection is narrow. The timeline to Jin is unknown — the last confirmed data is nineteen hours old and she is operating without confirmation of his current state.

She maps these. She does not resolve them. They are the risks the plan accepts.

She picks up the watch. Sets it down face-down.

She does not need to count minutes anymore. She needs to move.

The broadcast checkpoint opens in twenty-two minutes. Zara is somewhere in the terminal's outer sectors — she has been in the building since the north bay arrival. She does not know Nadia knows this.

Time to give her something real.
