EXT. CIVILIAN TRANSIT ZONE — ZORAL CORRIDOR FAR SIDE — DUSK

The far side of the Corridor is different from the approach: residential density, not industrial. Low-rise blocks. A few shops still open, their interiors yellow against the dusk. A tram stop with three people waiting. A HALO UNIT on the corner post, its camera array rotating through a standard civic sequence.

NADIA moves through the transit zone on the pedestrian side, inside Lotti's frame for another forty meters before the frame ends at the tram stop. After the tram stop, she is alone again.

She is tracking the HALO UNIT's rotation. The array sweeps left, holds, sweeps right. She has the timing. She passes the tram stop in the array's hold phase.

She does not look at the three people waiting.

One of them looks at her.

OLEG YURTS is forty-something. He is not what hunters look like. He is wearing work clothes that have seen considerable use — jacket worn through at the right elbow, boots resoled once already. He has a phone in his hand. He was going to use it for something else entirely.

He looks at her face.

He looks at his phone.

He does not dial.

She is past the tram stop now. She does not run. Running is data. She maintains the walk-even pace Lotti specified and does not look back.

She turns left at the corner — away from the HALO UNIT's sweep return — and enters the service margin behind the residential block. She stops. She puts her back to the wall.

She presses her fingertips to her wrist. Real number. Elevated.

She waits. Thirty seconds. No sound change from the street. No HALO UNIT shift in rotation. No broadcast tone from the corner relay — the three-second alert tone that precedes a location ping.

Nothing.

She does not know what nothing means.

On the street behind her, OLEG stands at the tram stop with his phone in his hand. He has not moved. The other two people at the stop are looking at the tram route display. They have not noticed him not moving.

He looks at the phone screen. The broadcast app — not the main platform, a secondary aggregator — shows her last confirmed position. The information is six minutes old. There is a contact number. A submission form.

His thumb is over it.

The tram arrives.

The other two people board.

OLEG remains on the platform. He watches the tram close its doors and pull away. He watches it until it is gone.

He puts the phone in his pocket.

He does not leave the platform immediately. He stands there for another moment, not doing anything in particular, and then he walks in the opposite direction from the tram.

In the service margin, NADIA waits. Sixty seconds now. Still nothing.

She does not know what he decided. She does not know if he decided. She knows only that the alert tone has not sounded and the HALO UNIT on the corner has not shifted its rotation pattern.

She checks the route forward. One block, then the underpass, then the market district — the kind of density that costs hunters coverage.

She moves.

The threat is behind her and she cannot read it. This is a new kind of exposure: not the known threat of the hunter field, but the unresolved arithmetic of a stranger she will never be able to verify.

She moves anyway.
