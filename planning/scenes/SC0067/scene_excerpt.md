INT. COLD-STORE EQUIPMENT SHELTER — MERIN WATERFRONT — MORNING

Several hours after SC0065. The dock has moved into full operation. Motor sounds, the mechanical rhythm of freight handling, voices at functional distances — none of it directed toward the shelter. The light through the gap in the shelter entrance is full day now.

She is still against the dock wall. The legs extended. The back to the concrete.

She has attempted to plan. The attempt reached its limit.

She has no phone. She has no money. She has no knowledge of where in the port district she is beyond the water to the east and the cold-store facility at her back and the dock sounds in every other direction. She has Tobias's contact — but Tobias is taken. She has Marcus's number — but Marcus is dead. She has the knowledge Marcus gave her, the intelligence in the package that reached her at SEQ05's midpoint: the man, the compound, the structure of the thing she is inside.

She has that.

She presses her fingertips to her wrist pulse point. The pulse is elevated but not alarming. She is reading it the way she reads everything: giving it a number, filing the number.

She lets herself look at it.

Tobias.

He came in on the same controlled pathway she came in on. She identified the markers. She identified them too late. He sutured her wound in the building's back room with eight interrupted sutures and she felt his hands work with the economy of someone who had done this before — not hesitation, not nerves, just the motion — and three hours later she left him behind in a corridor because the hunters were faster than she had modeled and the choice was his capture or both of them.

She chose.

She is not going to argue with the choice. The choice was correct in the operational parameters she had. That is also true.

Both of those things are true at once and neither of them makes the sutures in her body feel like anything except his absence.

She does not cry. There is still nothing available for that.

She touches the wound margin through the jacket. Eight sutures, his placement, his knotting. The warmth at the margin has not spread. The broad heat of established infection would cover a different geography than this — she knows the difference; she has felt the difference on other people. This is still focal. Still in the window.

She files this.

She thinks about what is still true.

Jin is still alive. The event has not resolved — she has been listening to the dock workers' radios at their break times and no one is reporting a resolution. The broadcast continues. If the broadcast continues, she is still in play, which means Jin is still in Roman's holding rather than Roman's leverage being neutralized. The event continuing is the condition she needs. The event is continuing.

Marcus's intelligence is in her head. The location of VELTAIN. The compound's structure as Marcus described it from the outside looking in. The name of the shutdown code and the fact that it exists. These are not actionable today, in this shelter, with these resources. But they are hers. No one can take them.

Her knee bears weight. She confirmed this on the run out of the safe house. She cannot run. She can walk with a specific gait modification that minimizes the rotational load on the damaged cartilage — she cataloged the modification during the MERIN streets sequence, learned it from the movement that hurt least. She has the modification. She can move.

The wound is in the decision window.

She is in a port city with an operating dock. Working docks have labor flows. Labor flows produce opportunity — transport, untracked movement, the particular anonymity of people who are paid to carry things from one place to another without asking about the things. She has five hours of dock observation. She knows the shift change timing. She knows which gate the freight traffic uses. She knows where the vehicle queue forms before loading begins.

She does not have a plan.

She has a catalog.

She sits with the catalog for a moment. Lets it be what it is.

It is thin.

It is also real.

She has been here before — not this shelter, not this dock, not this exact collection of costs — but here: the place where the inventory comes up short and the forward path requires beginning from less than she had last time. She knows what this place feels like from the inside. It feels like this: the concrete wall, the dock sounds, the weight of what is gone, the small list of what is not.

She does not perform the decision to continue. There is no one to perform it for. She simply arrives at it: the catalog is thin, the catalog is real, the catalog is enough.

She straightens her back against the wall.

She opens the problem.

The dock gate timing: the freight queue forms at eight-fifty. She has approximately forty minutes. She needs to be in the queue as labor, not as freight, which means she needs to look like someone who belongs in the queue. She looks at her jacket. The dock workers' jackets she observed are similar in weight and color — dark synthetic shell, high collar, the kind of thing purchased in volume for outdoor labor. She is wearing a dark synthetic shell. High collar. She bought it eleven days ago for a different reason.

She checks the wound dressing once more.

She stands up.

The left knee registers the movement. She gives it a moment. Then she moves toward the shelter entrance and looks at the dock without showing herself in the gap.

The queue has not formed yet. The freight gate is where she observed it. The vehicle line has two trucks.

She has forty minutes and a catalog.

She begins.
