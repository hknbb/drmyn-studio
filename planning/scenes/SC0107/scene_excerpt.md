INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — TECHNICAL CONTROL ROOM — NIGHT

The technical control room is the compound's operational core: server racks on two walls, a monitoring station that spans the third, four workstations currently staffed. The room has the specific texture of a space designed for sustained attention — low ambient lighting, bright screens, the hum of climate-controlled hardware. ROMAN's technical team has been here since the broadcast override began. They have not stopped working.

ROMAN enters. He looks at the monitoring station's main display — the ORACLE PRIME platform status, currently showing the shutdown sequence's progress alongside the auxiliary routing status his lead technician has already begun constructing.

LEAD TECHNICIAN
The backend shutdown is running. The card's authorization level is administrator-class — it's executing a full network termination. We can't stop the sequence from here; the server core would need a physical interrupt.

ROMAN
Auxiliary routing.

LEAD TECHNICIAN
Already running. We can maintain the broadcast feed through the auxiliary systems — it bypasses the terminating backend. The Herald stays up. The betting interface stays up.

ROMAN
Show me the feed.

The monitoring station's display splits: left panel shows the ORACLE PRIME platform as the audience sees it — The Herald's feed, restored, active; right panel shows the backend data layer — the content the system is routing through the auxiliary channel.

The right panel is not running betting odds and position data.

ROMAN (CONT'D)
(flat; not inflected as a question)
What is that.

LEAD TECHNICIAN
The auxiliary routing is pulling from the outer-tier content channel. The outer tier has — there's been an unmoderated content propagation event in the last twelve minutes. The moderation layer was down during the override, and—

ROMAN
How much of it.

LEAD TECHNICIAN
Enough that the auxiliary feed is — the Herald is running it. It's reading the content as verified platform data because the outer tier's authentication tokens are valid. We can't filter it at the broadcast layer without taking the feed down again.

ORACLE PRIME PLATFORM MONITORING DISPLAY:

[THE HERALD — BROADCAST ACTIVE — AUXILIARY ROUTING — DATA INTEGRITY: COMPROMISED]

Roman watches the left panel. The Herald's voice is audible through the monitoring station's speakers — warm, composed, precisely the persona he designed, precisely the register he calibrated to make the machine feel like a service. The Herald is presenting the content it has been routed: financial records, event outcome logs, liability documentation. It is applying its full broadcast warmth to evidence of what it has been broadcasting for nine years.

It does not know what it is saying. It was designed to trust its data source and deliver. It is doing exactly that.

Roman watches this for five seconds.

His sentences, when they come, are shorter than they have been at any prior point tonight.

ROMAN
Can you isolate the data stream.

LEAD TECHNICIAN
Not without—

ROMAN
The feed. Can you isolate the Herald's feed from the data layer and run it on a clean loop.

LEAD TECHNICIAN
If we loop the feed it won't be live. The audience will know within—

ROMAN
How long before they know.

LEAD TECHNICIAN
(a pause; this is the honest answer)
They already know. The outer-tier material has been running for forty seconds. The audience is watching The Herald read — they're watching it read the outcomes list.

Roman's eyes stay on the monitoring screen. The Herald continues. It does not pause; it does not stumble; it applies its designed warmth to each item in the outcomes list with the same professional engagement it would apply to a betting odds update.

ROMAN
(to the room; not to any person)
Get her out of the building.

A beat.

LEAD TECHNICIAN
Security is currently—

ROMAN
I know where security is.

He does. He is managing the north gate breach, the financial coup, the compound's second active breach, and the broadcast simultaneously, and he is managing all of them from here and none of them well. He chose this room. He chose it because the broadcast is what he built his identity around and the broadcast was what was failing and the spectacle instinct is older and deeper than the survival instinct and he could not help it.

He looks at the monitoring screens: the ORACLE PRIME platform fragmenting in real time on his left, the financial intelligence display showing Dimitri's coup's ongoing second phase on his right, the compound security schematic in the top right corner showing Nadia's last confirmed position as the interior access point she came through eighteen minutes ago.

He does not know where she is now.

ORACLE PRIME PLATFORM MONITORING DISPLAY:

[THE HERALD — BROADCAST ACTIVE — EVENT 15 — PARTICIPANT STATUS: UNLOCATED]

The Herald continues. Nadia's position indicator in the broadcast interface is showing her as UNLOCATED — the HALO UNIT she disabled in the service corridor is still offline; the override window has expired but the unit has not re-established its position feed. The broadcast system does not know where she is either.

Roman is the fourteenth event's architect and the fifteenth event's failure and he is standing in the room where his machine is broadcasting the proof of both, and there is nothing here that will help him reach her before she reaches the server core.

He turns back to his technical team.

ROMAN
(precise; the last operational frame he has available)
Keep the feed running. Whatever it takes.

He is still Roman. He is just Roman in a room where the spectacle has stopped serving him and he cannot stop tending to it.
