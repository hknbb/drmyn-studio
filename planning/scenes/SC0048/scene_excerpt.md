INT. MERIN — QUAY DISTRICT, UNIT 14-B — MORNING

The message has ended. The phone is still in her hands.

OTTO has not moved from the far end of the room. He is looking at the quay. He gives no indication that time is passing.

NADIA opens the documentation partition. She works in order.

The broadcast architecture file is first. It loads as a schematic — infrastructure map, node tree, relay architecture. She spends four minutes in it. The maintenance access layer is not buried; Marcus flagged it directly. She reads the specification twice: what it requires is a physical access point at one of three relay installations, a twelve-digit authentication sequence, and a two-minute window without broadcast interruption. She does not have the sequence. She understands now what getting it would mean.

She notes the three relay installations by location.

The network structure file is next. She reads it the way she reads terrain — top-down first, then detail. Roman's operation has four functional tiers: event management, financial routing, enforcement, and audience infrastructure. Each tier has its own personnel layer. The scale is larger than Marcus communicated in the message; either he was conservative, or the network grew in the time between assembly and delivery.

She notes the names she recognizes. There are not many.

ORACLE PRIME's operational history: six years of formal events, three years of informal precursors before the infrastructure was stable. The platform's current form is the fourth version. The earlier versions were smaller, dirtier, more exposed. This version is engineered for longevity.

Marcus flagged the financial infrastructure section as the weakest link in the documentation — he had structure but not account specifics. He noted a gap: DIMITRI's operational ledger. He had confirmed its existence and could not get to its contents. He flagged it as the single item he would pursue next.

She marks it as an open thread.

She has been putting off the last file. She opens it.

The previous hunt victims. The file is clean — not assembled for her; assembled because Roman documents everything. Fourteen subjects across six events. Names, dates, entry point, event duration, final confirmed status. Every entry ends the same way.

She reads to the end of the list.

She reads it again.

Directly after the victim records: the acquisitions log. Roman's collection. Each event subject represented by a single catalogued item — an object retrieved from the final location, photographed, stored. The log has the same clean structure as the victim records. Fourteen entries. Fourteen trophies.

She holds this.

The machine has a perfect record. She is the fifteenth subject. She is reading her projected outcome, and there is no version of this documentation in which anyone survived long enough to read what she is reading now.

She stays with it for as long as she can afford to.

Then she goes back to the broadcast architecture file.

The maintenance access layer. The three relay installations. The twelve-digit authentication sequence she does not yet have. She reads the specification a third time — not for new information, but because the thing she is looking at is the thing that stops it, and she needs to know its shape precisely.

OTTO has not moved. The light through the high window is fuller now. Late morning. The quay has been active for a while.

She closes the partition.

She looks at the phone in her hand. Then she sets it down on the folding table.

She is not the same person who picked it up.
