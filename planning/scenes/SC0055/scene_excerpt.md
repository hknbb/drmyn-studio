INT. EASTERN JUNCTION — STATION CAFÉ — MIDDAY

A small room. Four tables. A counter with coffee equipment. The person behind the counter has gone somewhere. She is alone.

She has coffee in front of her. She has not touched it.

The device is open. She has been looking at the same set of documents for eleven minutes, not reading — accounting.

What she has:

UNIT 7-ECHO. Location confirmed. Marcus's relay installation documentation. The broadcast architecture of ORACLE PRIME mapped in sufficient detail to know what breaking it does.

The trophy room. VELTAIN private residence. Roman's archive. Operational — not symbolic. Broadcast access layer credentials inside. Shutdown code: physical analog, high-confidence hypothesis, same archive.

Jin. VELTAIN-7, eastern residential block. Alive eleven days ago.

A tracking blind spot that the system does not know she knows about. A Transparency Protocol window that opens in approximately five hours, which means she needs to be invisible in approximately five hours.

Zara. A route incoming. A monitoring node map incoming. A person who has operational clearance inside the VELTAIN corridor and has been working there for two years.

She looks at what she has. It is more than any previous subject had. It is also not a plan yet.

She opens a new document on the device. She writes:

*1. Receive Zara's route and monitoring node map.*
*2. Use the monitoring blind spots to reach VELTAIN before the next Transparency Protocol window.*
*3. Access the VELTAIN facility. [Mechanism: Zara's clearance and operational knowledge. Open variable: exact entry method TBD with Zara.]*
*4. Reach the residential archive. [Hardest target. Open variable: method of entry to private residence interior.]*
*5. Locate the shutdown code physical analog inside the archive.*
*6. Deploy it. [Collapses broadcast routing. ORACLE PRIME loses its financial infrastructure. The event ends.]*
*7. Reach Jin at VELTAIN-7 during the collapse window and extract.*

She looks at the list.

Three of the seven steps have open variables — steps three, four, and seven. Step three has a partial answer (Zara). Step four is the hardest problem and she does not yet have the answer. Step seven depends on step six creating a window.

She considers waiting. Not for long — she knows the argument: wait for more intelligence, close the open variables, don't commit until the path is clear.

The counter-argument is: the Event Deadline is running. It has been running since The Herald's announcement. She has seventy-two hours, less whatever has elapsed. The deadline is not a variable she can close by waiting. Waiting trades open variables for a shorter deadline, which is worse.

There is also this: Roman has hosted seven events. The trophy room has existed since the first one. In seven events, no subject has ever known the trophy room was the target. In seven events, no subject has ever reached it. In seven events, Roman has assumed that the archive is safe because it is inside his private residence and because no subject has ever known to look for it. He trusts the architecture of his own invincibility. He trusts the monument.

She sees this clearly for the first time.

The trophy room is invulnerable to everyone who has ever tried to reach it for the simple reason that no one has ever known to try. The machine's most critical asset is kept in the place Roman believes is safest not because it has been defended against attack but because it has never been a target.

It will be a target.

She has seventy-two hours and a list with three open variables and a person with operational clearance who is building a route.

She looks at the plan on the device. She does not add qualifications. She does not write *provisional* at the top or *pending Zara's confirmation* in the margins. She writes one more line at the bottom:

*This is the plan.*

She leaves it.

The coffee is cold. She drinks it anyway.

She opens the relay installation documentation and goes back to mapping the infrastructure.
