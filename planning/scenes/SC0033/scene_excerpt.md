INT. DISUSED INDUSTRIAL UNIT — NORTH RENNMARK BORDER — DAY

A cleared space: concrete, high ceiling, no windows at ground level. A defunct conveyor bracket along the south wall. No behavioral monitoring registered on the approach. She confirmed it before she entered.

She closes the door.

She stands in the center of the space and does not move.

She has not been fully still since the game began. Her body does not know what to do with the absence of direction. It takes a moment to stop transmitting.

Then she sits down. Back against the east wall, left side managed automatically, the rib cage calculating its own weight.

She runs the inventory.

Rib cage: lateral compression, right of center. Two ribs, possibly three. The bruising is at maximum expression now — the deep color she associates with day four. She is at day three. Tomorrow will be worse before it begins to resolve. She notes this. Filed.

Left shoulder: developed stiffness from the compensating posture. Manageable. Secondary item.

Accumulated fatigue: she cannot assess it accurately because she has not stopped long enough to know the real number. What she can measure is response time degradation. Her assessment of the ventilation passage — the four-meter calculation, the one-second contact — ran at normal speed. The processing has not degraded yet. She has a window before it will.

She allows herself this information without interpreting it as encouragement.

Then she allows the other things in.

Jin first. She has been moving ahead of the specific texture of his absence — not the abstraction but the concrete: the way he holds his shoulders when he is trying not to cry, the last thing he said. She lets herself have it for seven seconds. Seven seconds is what she can afford.

She feels the full weight of it for seven seconds.

Then she files it. Not suppression — operational categorization. Cannot resolve with current resources. Priority target. Continuing.

Marcus.

She allows one thing only: the quality of the last communication between them, which was not a silence she read correctly at the time. She had categorized it as professional distance. She now knows what it was.

She gives this five seconds.

Then she closes it.

She looks at the east wall for a moment. The concrete is unremarkable. She is aware of the phone number in her jacket pocket without reaching for it.

She has been still for four minutes and twenty seconds. She has counted.

She cannot afford four minutes and twenty seconds.

She stands.

The rib cage registers the movement and she registers the rib cage and neither of them stops.

She has a direction. She knows the next vector. She knows what resources she has and what she needs. The phone number is not yet a resource she will pull; it is a resource she holds.

She goes.
