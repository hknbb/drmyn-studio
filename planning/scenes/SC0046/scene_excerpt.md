INT. ZORAL CORRIDOR — FREIGHT TRANSFER DEPOT, MAIN FLOOR — CONTINUOUS

The depot is a low industrial shed, fifty meters long. Freight crates along the south wall. A dispatcher's office — DRUNE — window facing the main floor, frosted glass, the silhouette of a man at a desk. He looks up when they enter. Looks at LOTTI. Looks at Nadia. Looks at LOTTI again.

Drune files that.

LOTTI moves toward the freight marshal's station at the north end. She has done this before; she knows where things are. Nadia stays near the crates, out of Drune's direct sightline.

LOTTI
(to freight marshal)
I'm here for the Vandermeer consignment. Unit seven.

FREIGHT MARSHAL
It's been held for re-inspection.

LOTTI
It was inspected Thursday.

FREIGHT MARSHAL
Thursday's inspector had questions.

LOTTI
Thursday's inspector always has questions. The answers are the same as last time.

The argument begins. It will take the shape of arguments in freight depots everywhere: two professionals each asserting a procedural position neither intends to yield, until one of them does. Drune watches through his frosted window. Both of them are occupied.

INT. ZORAL CORRIDOR — FREIGHT TRANSFER DEPOT, MARSHAL'S ANTEROOM — CONTINUOUS

The anteroom door is unmarked. It opens without a key. Nadia is inside in three steps.

She counts from the doorway. Third pipe manifold from the left, lower coupling joint, between the housing and the wall — a gap of four centimeters. She has to angle her fingers to reach it.

It is there. Exactly there.

A waterproofed envelope, thin, the kind that does not crinkle under pressure. She pulls it out.

INT. ZORAL CORRIDOR — FREIGHT TRANSFER DEPOT, MAIN FLOOR — MOMENTS LATER

She is back at the crates before the freight marshal has finished registering his second objection. Drune is still at his window, watching the argument. He glances toward the crates. Nadia is leaning against a support post, examining nothing.

He looks away.

Nadia does not open the envelope here.

INT. ZORAL CORRIDOR — FREIGHT TRANSFER DEPOT, MARSHAL'S ANTEROOM — MOMENTS LATER

She does not open it here, either.

She waits until LOTTI and the freight marshal have moved to the loading bay — their argument resolved in LOTTI's favor, as these arguments apparently always are — and Drune has followed to the bay window to observe the loading.

She opens it in the doorway of the anteroom, angled away from the bay window.

A card. Index card size. The handwriting is precise, compact, left-margin-aligned.

MERIN — Quay District, Unit 14-B.
OTTO.
Five digits: 7-3-1-1-4.
Below that, in smaller letters: I like redundancies. Go.

She reads it twice. She puts the card in her inside pocket, against her ribs. Closes the envelope. Returns the envelope to the cache.

She does not stand there.

EXT. ZORAL CORRIDOR — FREIGHT TRANSFER DEPOT, OUTER GATE — MINUTES LATER

LOTTI emerges from the loading bay with a receipt in her hand. She finds Nadia at the gate.

LOTTI
Done.

NADIA
Thank you.

A beat. LOTTI looks at her the way she looked at her at the start — workable, but noted.

LOTTI
You're not coming back this way.

It is not a question.

NADIA
No.

LOTTI
All right.

She goes. NADIA watches her for a moment. LOTTI does not look back.

Drune is still at his bay window. He watched LOTTI load. He watched the second figure — female, quiet, stayed near the door — leave with her. He will write this down.

He probably already has.

Nadia turns toward the east road. MERIN is four hours by the freight rail, which she will not take, or six on foot through the corridor access roads, which is what she has.

She moves.
