INT. VELTAIN SERVICE INFRASTRUCTURE — JUNCTION CORRIDOR — PRE-DAWN

The junction is where three service corridors meet — a cross-point in the maintenance grid, ceiling height three meters, conduit infrastructure at two. The floor is concrete. Emergency lighting at the base of each wall: enough to see silhouettes, not faces.

Nadia comes through the eastern corridor at pace. Not running — controlled movement, the kind that reads as maintenance worker until the last ten meters.

REED is at the junction's center. Piotr's advance element. He is facing north, not east. He has been watching the wrong corridor.

He turns when he hears her.

He is less prepared than she has been for this moment. She has been prepared for this moment since the staging position. The exchange is brief — four seconds, Reed going down hard against the conduit mount, not dead, and Nadia already past him, moving to the junction's north wall.

She presses her back against it. Looks at all three corridor mouths.

PIOTR steps out of the western corridor.

He does not rush. He moves at the pace of a man who has assessed the geometry and found it acceptable. He is taller than she expected — her dossier had measurements, but measurements are not presence. His presence is the absence of urgency. He looks at Reed on the floor. Looks at her.

PIOTR
(measured, unhurried)
Ms. Vale. You're earlier than I anticipated.

He stops eight meters away. The junction's geometry puts her against the north wall, him at the western mouth, Reed between them on the floor as an obstacle that serves neither of them. He will be calculating the angles. This is what he does — he calculates angles and then he waits for the best one.

She does not give him the wait.

She moves.

Not toward him — lateral, east along the north wall, forcing him to track rather than hold position. He tracks. She is reading his body as she moves: the shoulder carriage, the weight distribution, the slight forward lean that tells her he is right-hand dominant and has trained his right side to lead. The brachial plexus on the right — the nerve cluster at the junction of neck and shoulder — is the target. The strike that delivers it requires proximity she does not yet have.

He adjusts. Steps into the junction's center.

PIOTR (CONT'D)
(slightly more formal)
This isn't the most efficient —

She is already committed to the line. She drives directly at him — the one move patience cannot anticipate because patience waits for the opponent to make a tactical choice, and this is not a tactical choice, this is a removal of the space he needs to work in.

He is good. He gets his arm across in time — not to block, to redirect — and the redirect sends her left into the wall and he follows with full professional weight, landing across her right side, three ribs compressing against the concrete.

The sound is specific. She knows it by feel before she hears it.

She does not stop.

She has her left hand at his right shoulder — the right shoulder, not the left, because the right side leads and the right side is where the brachial plexus sits closest to the surface in this grip position. She has her thumb at the precise junction. She has trained this. She has the force and the angle she was trained to know would work.

She delivers it.

Piotr goes down. Not a fall — a collapse, the way the body collapses when the right motor signals fail simultaneously. His right arm is not working. The left is, but the left is slow and he is on his knees and by the time the left hand reaches for anything she is already two meters away, her back against the east wall, her right hand pressed to her own side.

She counts. Three. One is cracked through — she can feel the specific grinding quality that distinguishes a crack from a bruise. The other two are compressed, not broken. She can breathe. She cannot breathe deeply.

Piotr is on his knees on the junction floor. His right arm is at his side, non-functional. He looks at her. His face gives nothing. He does not speak.

She picks up Reed's comms unit from the floor. She looks at Piotr.

NADIA
(low, even)
The frequency in the note. Don't use it.

She means: if you contact Roman, he will know you failed. He will not deploy another asset before the compound action. He will manage this himself. Which changes everything.

Piotr understands this. He is a professional. He understands exactly what she is doing and exactly why it is correct.

He does not answer.

She goes through the northern corridor at the same controlled pace she arrived at. Her right hand stays at her side. Her breathing is short and even — the medic's breathing, the kind you learn when you are working in the field and the body cannot be allowed to fall behind the work.

She counts to herself as she walks. Not the ribs — the time. Eleven minutes to the compound's eastern service access. Zara is holding the staging position.

She walks.
