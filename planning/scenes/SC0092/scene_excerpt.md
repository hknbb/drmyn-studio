EXT. VELTAIN OUTER APPROACH — PERIMETER RIDGE — PRE-DAWN

The ridge runs east-west along the city's outer edge — not a natural formation but a managed one, the kind of topography that is engineered over decades by a city that has decided its borders are a security question. The vegetation on the northern slope is maintained at forty centimeters. Nothing grows taller without being cut. The cutting is automated.

Nadia lies flat at the crest and looks at what lies below.

VELTAIN is not a dramatic city. It is a careful one. The lights are evenly distributed — not the organic clustering of a place that grew, but the grid precision of a place that was designed. The compounds in the inner district are separated by service corridors that are wide enough to allow vehicle access and narrow enough to prevent assembly. The outer wall runs two kilometers along the western approach, concrete and sensor-equipped, with the sensor infrastructure mounted at intervals that reflect the threat model of a man who trusts the calculation more than the guard.

She has been reading this for four minutes.

Zara is beside her. She arrived at the ridge two minutes after Nadia — the wound, at this pace, costs approximately two minutes per kilometer. She has not said this, but Nadia has counted it.

She looks at the wall's western sector. Identifies the gap.

It is not a flaw in the design. It is a feature of an older design — a maintenance access corridor that predates the current sensor grid by eleven years. The deployment documents identified it as a structural inconsistency in the perimeter coverage: a twelve-meter span where ORACLE PRIME's fixed sensor array has a shadow, introduced when the maintenance access was cut and never reconciled with the updated grid because the gap, at twelve meters, falls below the system's threshold for flagging as a vulnerability.

Twelve meters. Enough.

She looks at the patrol interval. Marks the timing.

NADIA
(very low)
Seven minutes between passes. The access point is at the midpoint of the gap. Three minutes to cross to the wall, two to the access, two inside before the next pass clears the eastern angle.

ZARA
Seven minutes.

NADIA
We need six.

Zara looks at the wall. Looks at the timing. Runs the geometry.

ZARA
When.

NADIA
Next pass in four minutes. We move at two.

A pause. Below them, VELTAIN is quiet in the way that things are quiet when the silence is maintained rather than natural. The sensor grid hums at a frequency you cannot hear but you can infer from the behavior of everything around it — the birds don't settle near the outer wall; they have learned the hard threshold.

Zara presses two fingers against her side, below the jacket. Checks the compress. The wound from the northern corridor — left side high, dressed at the first covered position an hour ago — is stable. Not comfortable. Stable.

Nadia watches this.

NADIA (CONT'D)
(low)
How is it.

ZARA
Managed.

NADIA
That's not what I asked.

A beat.

ZARA
It will hold for what we need it to hold for.

She says it the way she states everything — flat, declarative, the report rather than the reassurance. Nadia accepts this as the data it is. She also finishes retaping the field dressing she has been tightening for the last thirty seconds — tightening it, not beginning it, which is the behavioral tell that she has been watching and managing the wound since Zara checked it and said nothing.

Zara notices this. Does not comment.

The patrol reaches the eastern angle. They watch it pass.

Two minutes.

NADIA
Your shoulder.

NADIA (CONT'D)
My shoulder has been —

She stops. Starts again.

NADIA (CONT'D)
It will hold.

She has given the same data Zara gave her. Both of them have assessed their own damage and returned the same answer. Zara looks at her.

ZARA
(very low)
After this.

NADIA
After this.

The patrol passes the maintenance access point. Continues east. At ninety seconds, it reaches the far angle of its interval and begins the return. By then, if the geometry holds, they will already be inside.

Nadia moves.
