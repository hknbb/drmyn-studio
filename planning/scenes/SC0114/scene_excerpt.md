INT./EXT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — HOLDING AREA / EXTERIOR — DAY

Guard Pike has been at this post for four years and four months. He has a routine. The routine involves a radio check at the top of every hour and a logbook entry at the bottom and the knowledge that the authority behind his position is continuous and funded and not going away.

At 14:32, his radio stops responding.

Not static — silence. The channel is open and nothing is coming through it and the nothing is a different quality from the nothing that would mean the radio needs a battery change. He knows the difference. He has been a guard for four years and four months.

He looks at the radio. He does not look at the door to the holding room.

He is still looking at the radio when the door opens.

Zara has been in this room for eleven days. She has been in rooms like this — bounded, watched, the air quality of authority over her — for long enough that she can read the acoustic change in a building with precision. The compound changed register an hour ago: the broadcast hum stopped, the security alert pattern shifted from rotating to static, and the guard outside her door has been on his radio at intervals that are no longer routine. She has been ready for this since the first change in the building's sound.

The door is not locked.

She noticed it was not locked fourteen minutes ago, when the magnetic seal's cycling sound — which she had been tracking since they put her here — stopped cycling and did not resume. She waited fourteen minutes. She wanted to understand what she was walking into.

She understands it now.

Guard Pike is standing at his post. His radio is silent in his hand. He is a person whose obligation was to an authority that no longer has the infrastructure to sustain it — the network that paid him, validated his access credentials, and defined his function has been legally seized and structurally dismantled in the last forty-seven minutes. He does not know the full scale of this. He knows enough.

He does not stop her.

He does not look at her as she passes him. He is still looking at the radio. This is not cowardice — it is a person doing the specific arithmetic of his situation in real time and reaching the correct conclusion that what he is holding is not a valid instrument of an authority that currently exists.

Zara walks past him.

The compound's exterior is in transition: two vehicles from the outer authority at the perimeter approach road, not yet inside, their lights visible through the tree line. The law-enforcement response to a compound of this designation takes time to mobilize and she has a window. She has been calculating the window since the building changed.

She moves through the compound's exterior without running. Running is a signal; she does not need to be a signal. She has the bearing of a person who knows exactly where she is going and is not asking permission to go there.

The network's leverage over Lena's situation died with the network. The debt instrument Roman's machine used to ensure Zara's compliance — the exposure it held, the threat it maintained over her family — is in a frozen asset registry in three separate legal jurisdictions. It cannot be exercised. It cannot be transferred. It does not exist anymore as a functional threat.

Lena does not know this yet.

Zara is going to tell her.

She reaches the perimeter's unmanned section — the service road access that has not been staffed since the security recall — and she does not slow down at it. She passes through the gap and she is in the city and the city is beginning to understand what is happening to the compound she just left.

She does not look back.

She is moving toward Lena. She is not there yet. She will be.


INT./EXT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — SERVICE CORRIDOR / PERIMETER / CITY EDGE — CONTINUOUS

She is already moving when we meet her here.

Jin is against her right hip — shifted from the left before she was out of the room. The ribs on the left side do not permit proximity to load-bearing positions. He is six kilograms heavier than she planned for. Not a problem. A variable.

He is watching the ceiling over her shoulder. She does not explain motion to him. She has been preparing him for motion since the room — her hand pressure, the quality of her voice, the specific pace of transition. He accepts it. His weight settles against her.

---

The service corridor runs sixty meters before it branches. Zara's notation is on the branching pipe — a smear of yellow chalk at shoulder height, barely visible, pointing right. Not a mark that reads as a mark. Reads as a scuff, a maintenance record, an old paint transfer. She turns right.

Through the window at the junction: two law-enforcement vehicles on the main approach road, a third behind them. Converging on the primary entrance. The primary entrance is not her entrance. She keeps her pace.

The pace is this: not running. Not stopping. Not because she does not want to run — because the ribs will not allow it, not carrying this weight, not at this stage of the calculation. The pace is what remains when you subtract what you cannot afford. It is enough. She has run the arithmetic on it. It is enough.

---

VEHICLE: GRAY COMPACT / SERVICE LOT NW / WHEEL WELL RR / KEY: ALPHA

---

The vehicle is where Zara said it would be.

Service-road parking, northwest corner, invisible from the primary entrance and from the response corridor. She presses the key. The lock opens. Jin looks at the door handle with the attentiveness of someone tracking a new thing. She opens it. She puts him in the rear seat with the same specific pressure and transition quality she has been using since the room. He does not protest. She closes the door with care.

She drives at the speed limit.
