INT. KESTON'S APARTMENT — CONTINUOUS

Keston on the sofa. Comfortable. Evening. The television is on — full screen, the ORACLE PRIME interface in its corner position, broadcast front and center. He has a glass of something on the side table. He leans forward slightly.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
For context: first contact at fifty-two minutes is a statistical outlier. Our analytical model projected initial contact in the four-to-six-hour window. The subject's early positioning narrowed that window. She was operating on information she shouldn't have had access to, or she made extremely good guesses about field architecture. We're not certain which yet. Both explanations are interesting.

Keston watches the odds update in real time on the broadcast interface.

SCREEN TEXT:
EVENT CYCLE 114 — FIRST CONTACT CONFIRMED
SURVIVAL (48H): 3.4 → 3.1
NEXT CONTACT (6H): 4.8
NEW MARKET OPEN: INJURY SEVERITY — MINOR/MAJOR/TERMINAL

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Odds on forty-eight-hour survival have moved to 3.1 following confirmation that the subject is mobile and uncompromised. The injury market is open — early data suggests minor classification, but we'll have better data as the session continues. For subscribers who positioned at 4.1 this morning: you are looking at a live profit.

Keston opens his tablet. The ORACLE PRIME subscriber interface: clean, professional, exactly as designed to look like something you would use for financial instruments rather than this.

He navigates to the odds. Reads for a moment. His thumb hovers.

KESTON
(to himself, quiet)
It's not like —

He doesn't finish. His thumb is still hovering.

The broadcast continues.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Audience engagement has spiked twenty-three percent since the first contact confirmation. For a no-baseline subject at this stage, that's a strong signal. Our analysis: the audience response pattern suggests the subject is performing — in the theatrical sense. People are watching her. They want to know what she does next. That is a market condition, and we'll see it reflected in the activity volume over the next hour.

A beat. The Herald's voice shifts fractionally — the pitch of genuine enthusiasm, calibrated down to the level that sounds like expertise.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
I'll note: a subscriber who positioned at opening on SURVIVAL took an early and aggressive position. That kind of market confidence on a no-baseline subject is itself information. It's always worth knowing what the experienced participants think.

Keston's posture changes. Barely. He is listening to the part of this that is addressed, in design if not in fact, to him specifically. Someone who knew. A good early read.

His thumb taps the SURVIVAL (48H) market.

His other hand picks up the glass. He takes a sip. Puts it down.

KESTON (CONT'D)
(quieter still)
It's not like it changes anything.

The interface populates: position entry. Clean, frictionless. The amount field blinks, waiting.

He enters a number. Not large. Considered. The kind of amount that says: I've thought about this.

He confirms.

SCREEN TEXT:
BET PLACED
HANDLE: KESTON_L
MARKET: SURVIVAL (48H)
POSITION: YES
AMOUNT: ₿0.80
ODDS TAKEN: 3.1

The interface returns to broadcast mode. The Herald continues.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
We have new activity on the survival market —

Keston sits back. Looks at the screen. His position is confirmed in the corner of the interface, small and precise: ₿0.80 at 3.1.

He is now in it.

He sits with this for one beat — not the duration of a crisis, just the length of something being registered. Then he reaches for the glass again.

The broadcast continues. The Herald continues. The engagement metrics continue.

The interface holds Keston's position alongside a dozen others. He is one entry in a list. The list is the machine's memory, and it has recorded him.
