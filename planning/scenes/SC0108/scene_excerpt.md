INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — INNER CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS

She has been descending.

From the trophy room — upper level — through the broadcast terminal room — private level — and now through the inner corridor below it, where the building's architecture changes: the carpeted residential register of the upper levels gives way to something more functional, more secure. Composite flooring again. The climate-control hum at a slightly different frequency, the signature of server-grade cooling rather than residential comfort.

NADIA moves through the corridor at the speed she has been maintaining since she entered the building — fast, quiet, calibrated. Her left side is the problem. It is always the problem. She has stopped monitoring it consciously; the adjustment has become part of her movement. This is either adaptation or the specific kind of numbness that follows sustained injury past a threshold she does not want to calculate.

The corridor runs thirty meters before it reaches the security station outside the server core level's antechamber.

She stops at twenty meters.

The security station is unmanned.

She reads this with the same attention she gave the empty Zone 1 checkpoint in SC0098: not relief, not haste — assessment. An occupied station has visual tells of sustained presence: the specific chair position of a person who has been sitting for hours, the accumulated items on the desk surface, the orientation of the monitoring terminal toward where the operator's eyes would naturally settle. This station has none of those tells. The chair is pushed back at an angle that means the person stood quickly. The monitoring terminal is still live. On the desk's edge, a paper cup — the kind from a facility dispenser, not personal equipment.

She walks to the station.

She places her palm against the paper cup.

Warm. Not hot — the coffee, or whatever it is, was poured ten minutes ago at most and has been cooling since. The person who left it stood and walked away inside the last ten minutes. They did not finish it. They did not take it.

The coup recall.

She does not know Dimitri's name in connection with this specific gap. She knows the gap exists; she knows its likely duration; she knows that a recall event with this profile — all personal detail, immediate departure — comes from a higher authority than compound security and addresses a threat that Roman has assessed as more urgent than her. She knows this tells her something about the state of Roman's position.

She files it and moves.

The antechamber door is three meters past the security station.

It stands open. Not ajar — open. The recalled detail pushed through it in a direction that left it standing wide. The antechamber beyond is lit: overhead panels, functional lighting, the specific visual register of a room that is always on.

She steps through.

INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — SERVER CORE ANTECHAMBER — CONTINUOUS

The antechamber is a transition space: eight meters square, storage on two walls, the server core's main door on the far wall with its physical credential lock, a secondary door to the left leading to Roman's private retreat — the room below the broadcast terminal room, the room at the building's center.

The secondary door is not fully closed.

Through the gap: light. The specific quality of a room in use.

She stays at the antechamber's entrance. She reads the geometry — the server core door to the right, credential lock intact and active; the secondary door to the left, open a few centimeters, light visible, the faint sound of something she cannot yet identify.

She has come for the server core. The server core is to her right.

Roman is to her left.

She looks at both doors for one full second. Not deciding — confirming a decision already made in the trophy room, where she read fourteen closed events and understood that this ending would be made entirely of her own choices, without a path to follow, without anyone who had done this before.

She moves toward the server core door.

Behind her, from the secondary door, a sound she can now identify: the specific cadence of a man walking from one side of a room to the other, back and forth, the behavioral tell of a person who is thinking and cannot be still. Roman. Pacing in his retreat, the way he probably paced in the years before he built a machine that converted his pacing into architecture.

She does not look back.

She is at the server core door.

The card is in her hand.
