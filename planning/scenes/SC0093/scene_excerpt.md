EXT. VELTAIN OUTER WALL — WESTERN SECTOR — CONTINUOUS

No sprint. The approach to the wall is controlled, calibrated — the pace that produces the least thermal signature against the ambient ground temperature at this hour. Forty-three seconds to the wall.

Zara is eight meters behind. Same pace. The two-minute gap has narrowed to ninety seconds.

They reach the wall's surface. Nadia moves along it to the maintenance access — a recessed panel in the concrete, secured with a combination lock that uses the same model as the outer transit hub from SC0081. She has the override sequence from the deployment documents.

She runs it.

The panel opens.

They go through.

INT. VELTAIN MAINTENANCE CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS

The corridor is narrow — single file, concrete on three sides, utility conduit running overhead. The overhead lighting is emergency-level: enough to see the floor, not enough to see anything beyond three meters.

Nadia moves along it. Counts the distance.

Behind her, Zara's breathing is controlled. The wound is costing something in the narrow space — the posture the corridor requires puts pressure on the left side — but her pace does not change.

Forty meters. A junction — service access right, residential infrastructure left. The deployment documents show left leads to the inner district's maintenance layer. A grid that runs beneath the compound ring for two hundred meters before surfacing in the service corridor between the secondary residential compound and the western utility block.

Nadia goes left.

She stops.

In the corridor's silence — the faintest sound. Not a footstep. The absence of the hum she has been tracking since the ridge. The sensor grid's ambient frequency. It is gone.

Not a power failure. A scheduled cycle. The grid runs a calibration silence at fourteen-minute intervals — a brief window, not long enough to be operationally useful under normal conditions.

She knows this because the deployment documents told her. Because Marcus, forty-one weeks before he died, was thinking about exactly this corridor.

She moves through the calibration window. Reaches the surface access at the two-hundred-meter mark.

Opens it.

EXT. VELTAIN SERVICE CORRIDOR — INNER DISTRICT — PRE-DAWN

The service corridor runs between two compound walls. Overhead — the sky, just beginning. Not light yet. The suggestion of light, the kind that exists only as the absence of full dark.

Nadia looks at the corridor. Looks at the compound walls on either side.

She is inside VELTAIN.

Behind her, Zara comes up through the access and closes it quietly.

They stand in the service corridor. The city's geometry is different here — the same grid precision from the ridge, but now the grid is all around them rather than below. The walls are taller than they appeared from the approach. The sensor infrastructure that looked like a design feature from the outside is, from the inside, simply the environment.

She has been in Roman's compound twice. Not this compound — the operations center, the formal property, the one she was supposed to inhabit as his partner. Not this. This is the inner architecture. The place where the machine actually lives.

ZARA
(very low)
Which direction.

Nadia looks at the compound wall to their east. The secondary residential compound. The one the deployment documents show as the holding location for non-operational assets.

NADIA
East.

They move.
