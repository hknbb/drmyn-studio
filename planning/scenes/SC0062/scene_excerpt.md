INT. 14 HAVEL COURT — SAFE HOUSE UNIT — THIRD FLOOR — PRE-DAWN

Four forty-three in the morning.

The building is quiet. From two rooms away, the faint sound of Tobias — a drawer opening, or the specific silence of a person lying awake and choosing not to move.

She has been here five hours. The suture is holding. The wound is not infected — not yet, not by the fever profile she has been running against her own data. She is alive and stationary and the building has been quiet since they stopped speaking.

This is the closest to safe she has been in days.

She opens the odds feed.

ORACLE PRIME — LIVE MARKET

SUBJECT REACHES VELTAIN: 2.1 — last adjusted 19:47

She checks the time. 04:43.

The odds were last adjusted at 19:47. She entered the building at 23:12. She has been inside for five hours and seventeen minutes. In five hours and seventeen minutes, the ORACLE PRIME betting market has not adjusted once.

She does not move.

The Herald cannot fabricate live betting data. That is the protocol — the single constraint on the broadcast system that Roman cannot override because it is the system's commercial guarantee. If the audience could see her, the odds would move. They always move. She has been watching them move for two weeks; she knows what the feed looks like when the audience has a live sighting. She knows what it looks like when they don't.

It looks like this.

The audience has not seen her since 19:47, which is nine minutes before she arrived at this building.

The building is in a broadcast dead zone.

She sits with this for the length of time it takes to be certain.

An off-broadcast location is not invisible by accident. Camera-blind alleys are invisible by accident — geography, angle, coverage gap. A building in the service quarter of a managed city district, three stories, east-facing windows, four hours of undisturbed occupancy: this building is not invisible by geography. It is invisible by design.

Roman's network does not accidentally remove locations from broadcast coverage. It removes them for a purpose. The purpose is to hold people in places where the audience cannot watch.

The trap is not designed to capture her on-broadcast for the game's entertainment.

It is designed to hold her off-broadcast. Out of the market. Out of the feed. Roman's private inventory, not the audience's spectacle.

She looks at the door to the corridor.

Tobias is two rooms away.

She cannot warn him silently. The monitoring architecture that makes this building off-broadcast requires active infrastructure — sensors, relays, at minimum passive recording systems tied to the building's network. That infrastructure hears her if she speaks. It registers her movement if she moves through the corridor. Any warning she gives Tobias announces to the building that she knows.

She is still.

She opens the message queue.

Zara's warning has been sitting there for four hours and eleven minutes.

WARNING — LINDEN JUNCTION
Contact phrase compromised: monitored Roman architecture. Address flagged in prior operation. Do not use the phrase. Exit if mobile.

She already knows. Zara reached the same conclusion through a different instrument — inside documentation of a seeded phrase protocol, not the odds behavior. Two independent lines of analysis arriving at the same architecture.

She reads the alternate route notation: freight corridor east from the service quarter junction. She files it.

She puts the device down.

The building is quiet.

The odds have not moved.

She begins calculating: how long it takes Tobias to reach the corridor from his room. The distance from the corridor to the fire escape. The distance from the fire escape to the alley. The noise the fire escape makes. Whether the monitoring architecture distinguishes between a single person moving and two people moving. Whether it matters.

The five hours of quiet were not absence of danger. They were the trap being patient.

She has been comfortable inside a machine that was designed to hold her still long enough for the door to close.

It has not closed yet.
