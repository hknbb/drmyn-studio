INT. ORACLE PRIME OPERATIONS SUITE — DAY

A room designed to look like a trading floor. Clean lines, no unnecessary surfaces. Four screens carry the same broadcast feed at different data layers: the visual stream, the audience engagement metrics, the betting volume index, the hunter position summary. The Herald's voice runs at low level — ambient, the way music might run in a different kind of room.

ROMAN VALE is seated at the primary workstation, reviewing the performance summary for the last eighteen hours. He is not reading it with urgency. He is reading it the way an architect reads the stress reports on a structure he has designed and tested: confirming.

DIMITRI KOSS stands two paces back, to his right. He has the same data on a tablet.

A beat of shared attention on the screens.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
...identification confirmed at perimeter, full visual, current appearance locked. The audience's engagement index has moved to a new plateau —

ROMAN
The associate event drove the spike.

Not a question. He is notating to himself. He scrolls to the next data set.

ROMAN
Tobias Orell. Four-year disappearance record. The audience read the reemergence correctly — it widened the game's emotional scope.

DIMITRI
The associate event also widened the exposure surface.

He says this carefully. The flatness of his voice carries all the weight. He is not emotional about it. He is stating a variable.

DIMITRI
The game is past the standard window. Collateral associations — Orell, the district contacts — create a documentation trail that a structured inquiry could follow. Each day of extension increases the trail's surface area.

He waits. He has stated the case. He does not re-state it.

ROMAN
Understand — you're pricing the exposure as a cost against a fixed timeline. That's the wrong model.

He does not turn from the screen.

ROMAN
Extended duration is the product. The audience's investment in an unresolved outcome is a function of time: the longer they watch, the more they've committed, the more the resolution matters to them. An early close crystallizes their investment at a low yield. They do not return.

He scrolls again.

ROMAN
The correct model is maximum duration before the resolution event. The exposure surface is priced into the premium.

Dimitri says nothing for a moment.

DIMITRI
Understood.

One word. Delivered correctly. He accepts it. The performance of loyalty is well-maintained.

Roman's attention has already moved to the next data set. The identification event data. He is reviewing the Herald's announcement delivery timing.

Dimitri checks his watch. A precise, unhurried gesture.

He opens his operational log — a slim physical ledger, not a device. He writes. Not quickly, not dramatically. He notes the date, the time, the subject of the meeting. Something brief.

He closes it.

On the screen behind him, the audience engagement index continues its plateau. The machine is performing above projections.


INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — NURSERY SUITE — DAY

A room designed for the long-term welfare of an infant. Climate-controlled; soft furnishings in neutral tones; a mobile above the crib that turns slowly without music. The windows are narrow and high — the view they provide is of sky only, no external reference. This is a room that contains everything needed and nothing that would locate it.

JIN is awake. He is on a padded floor mat, on his back, tracking the mobile's movement with the particular concentrated attention of eight months. He is well. This is immediately clear.

NURSE IRINA is finishing the morning's care. She moves with the efficiency of someone who has done this many times and finds it genuinely satisfying. She checks his temperature log. She records the overnight measurements on the care sheet.

NURSE IRINA
Good morning, Jin.

He turns at his name. He tracks her voice. He reaches toward her with both arms — the reaching of someone who expects to be picked up and has been picked up correctly before.

She picks him up. She holds him correctly.

NURSE IRINA
Here. Good. Yes.

The small domestic sounds of a morning. She carries him to the changing station.

During the change, she notices the hospital bracelet has shifted on his wrist — small wrist, the bracelet moves. She adjusts it. It is an infant ID bracelet: white band, printed name, hospital registration number, a small passive chip in the closure. The bracelet is from before. It has been on his wrist since the first day.

She checks the fit. Not too tight. Not too loose.

NURSE IRINA
There, Jin.

She finishes. She carries him to the feeding station.

He is well. He is in the wrong place. He does not know either of these things.
