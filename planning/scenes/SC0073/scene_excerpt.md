INT. KASPAR TERMINAL — STAFF ROOM — NIGHT

The fever is still in her blood — thirty-seven point two, below the threshold of incapacity. Suppressed. She can think. That is sufficient.

Chen Mei opens the courier bag on the break table. She removes a sealed polymer sleeve and sets it down. She does not hand it to Nadia. She steps back.

CHEN MEI
The original is held in two separate locations. This is a copy. Marcus specified the copy was for transfer — not for retention. I need you to understand that before you touch it. The copy cannot stay here.

NADIA
Then why bring it.

CHEN MEI
Because you need to know what it contains before you can decide where it goes. He —

She stops. Then, quietly, to herself.

CHEN MEI (CONT'D)
— he said that too.

Nadia opens the polymer sleeve. She reads.

The ledger is not narrative. It is structure: dates, location codes, abbreviated subject notations. Column left: subject. Column right: decision recorded, with a verbal response notation compressed to two or three words — these are Dimitri's characterizations of Roman's words, not transcripts. The notations are precise and systematic. Someone trained in observation made this record. Someone who knew it might need to survive him.

She reads for three minutes. Chen Mei does not speak. Her hands perform small unconscious sorting motions with a ballpoint pen on the table edge.

NADIA
Dimitri documented his own exposure.

CHEN MEI
Yes. Marcus said he would. He said Dimitri was too careful not to — if the network failed, Dimitri would need his own record. His own version.

NADIA
Which means the ledger implicates him as well as Roman.

CHEN MEI
Both of them. The decisions overlap. The timestamps are confirmed. Marcus confirmed them twice.

Nadia closes the document. She returns it to the polymer sleeve. She does not retain it.

NADIA
Who does Marcus name as the delivery channel.

Chen Mei reaches into the courier bag. A single folded page, separate from the ledger copy.

CHEN MEI
Inspector Okafor. Cross-jurisdictional financial crimes unit. He confirmed she is reachable. He confirmed she is not —

She repeats it, quietly.

CHEN MEI (CONT'D)
— not compromised.

NADIA
Okafor.

A beat.

NADIA (CONT'D)
How long has he been building this chain.

CHEN MEI
The earliest timestamp in the copy is forty-one weeks ago. He didn't tell me when he started.

Forty-one weeks. Nadia does not react to it. Marcus working forty-one weeks ahead is not new information about Marcus. It is the weight of it — set down on a break table, in a terminal, at night, while she is still fighting the residue of a fever.

NADIA
The copy can't stay here.

CHEN MEI
No. I carry it to Okafor. That was always the plan. What you have now is the knowledge that it —

She stops.

CHEN MEI (CONT'D)
— that it exists. And where it is going.

NADIA
And what I can do with it.

Chen Mei is very still. Her hands are flat on the table. No sorting motions. She has finished the documentation framing. She is simply present.

CHEN MEI
Yes.

Nadia looks at the table where the polymer sleeve sat. It is empty. The fight she has been running as a single interior operation inside the game has a second vector now: external, legal, building in parallel to everything happening inside the broadcast zone. Marcus built it. Chen Mei is carrying it. Now Nadia knows it exists.

She doesn't need to perform the recognition. She simply has it.
