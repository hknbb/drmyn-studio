INT. RENNMARK STAGING COMPLEX — HUNTER COORDINATION HUB — DAY

A converted freight office. Folding tables, cable runs, HALO UNIT data terminals. A dozen hunters in various configurations of professional attention — standing, seated, some already pulling on gear. The air is the air of a market that has just moved.

THE HERALD's announcement runs on two screens at the room's far end, looping.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Our audience now has a face to go with the name. Confirmed visual. Current appearance: as displayed. Last known vector: north, from the Rennmark district perimeter. The field is active.

The hunters respond at professional speed. One is already on comms, claiming a coverage zone. Two are cross-checking their last position data against the new broadcast frame. A fourth is revising his bid.

ZARA OKONKWO stands at a terminal to the left of the room. She is watching the broadcast loop. She has not moved since the announcement began.

She is not the only competent person in this room. She is the only one not moving.

She watches the footage from the identification contact — the four-meter pass, one second, the ventilation passage. She plays it twice.

What she is looking at is not what the other hunters are looking at. They are looking at the face. She is looking at the route.

The route is wrong.

Not wrong as in incorrect — wrong as in expensive. Someone forced her off a better line. The deviation is a meter and a half at the north junction, and it cost her twelve seconds. You do not accept that cost unless something makes the primary route unavailable.

She pulls up the prior structural data. The rib cage injury register from SC0027 is not in the hunter pool's packet — Tobias is not a listed associate — but the movement signature is there if you know how to read it.

She reads it.

She makes a note in her field log: PHYSICAL CONSTRAINT — LEFT SIDE. MOVEMENT SIGNATURE CONSISTENT WITH LATERAL COMPRESSION INJURY. OPTIMAL ROUTE PREFERENCE INTACT; DEVIATION INDICATES INVOLUNTARY.

She looks at the screens again. The other hunters have her last vector. They are overlaying it on district exit geometry. Standard protocol.

She opens the field log to the position entry. The cursor sits on a blank line waiting for coordinates.

She doesn't enter coordinates.

She scrolls up to the data from the last two hours: the Drasc sweep pattern, the HALO UNIT positional corrections, the coverage updates. She puts them next to each other.

She looks at the coverage geometry.

The sweep covered thirty percent more district than the bounty geometry required. The surplus positions cluster around infrastructure nodes: water access, power distribution junctions, a fiber relay at the north market. The Drasc pair did not plan this — the route data came from network dispatch. Someone above the Drasc pair decided those positions.

She writes: DEPLOYMENT GEOMETRY EXCEEDS HUNT PARAMETERS. SURPLUS COVERAGE — INFRASTRUCTURE NODES (WATER / POWER / RELAY). SYSTEMATIC. NOT SUBJECT-SPECIFIC.

She closes the log.

In her kit, behind the assignment packet, there is a photograph. Passport-sized. A teenage girl. Zara does not look at it.

She picks up her gear and takes her assigned position at the hub's south terminal.

She is in the hunter pool.

She is doing her job.
