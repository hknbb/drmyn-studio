```fountain
The second filing had gone out at the same time as the first. Different jurisdiction — a Nordic financial regulatory body with an independent evidentiary standard that did not require the same procedural authority the first notice had used. Cass Wren's challenge applied specifically to the first jurisdiction's standing. It did not apply to the second.

Riko checks the queue again.

RIKO
They've identified the second filing. Response is being prepared.

OKAFOR
How long?

RIKO
Whoever is drafting it is slower than the first. Different hand or different clock.

OKAFOR
Three hours, minimum. The second jurisdiction requires a separate authority challenge and a separate procedural basis. They cannot reuse the framework from the first.

RIKO
So —

OKAFOR
So we wait.

He is still. This is the most unusual thing Riko does: he goes completely still when told to wait, the way someone goes still who has not yet decided whether to comply.

RIKO
The confirmation window for the second notice closes in ninety minutes. If they get the challenge in before the registry processes it —

OKAFOR
They will not get the challenge in before the registry processes it.

She says this without looking up from the document she has moved on to reading. Her certainty is not performance — it is calculation.

RIKO
How do you know?

OKAFOR
Because I know how long it takes to draft a proper authority challenge to that jurisdiction's registry standard. And I know how long it takes them to process a notice. And I know which is faster.

---

The confirmation arrives at 10:23.

It is a standard registry notice — automated, formal, four lines. ORACLE PRIME FINANCIAL HOLDINGS (REGISTERED INSTRUMENT 7741-DELTA) has been formally noticed under cross-jurisdictional financial crimes investigation REF: XJ-0084-OKAFOR. The notice is now in the public regulatory record.

Riko looks at his screen.

RIKO
There it is.

Okafor is already reading the next document. She does not look up.

She reaches for her phone. Sets it down without using it. Then she picks it up again and dials.

OKAFOR
(into phone)
This is Okafor. We have registry confirmation on the second notice. Begin the documentation preservation order — I want all accessible transaction records for the flagged instruments archived under the investigation reference before close of business.

She listens.

OKAFOR (CONT'D)
I understand the workload. Do it anyway.

She ends the call.

RIKO
He'll know by end of day. His monitoring infrastructure will flag the registry entry.

OKAFOR
He already knows. The challenge to the first notice told him everything he needed to know about our approach. The registry confirmation is not new information for Roman. It is new information for the record.

She looks at the window for a moment. The street below is ordinary. It is not involved in any of this.

OKAFOR (CONT'D)
The record is what matters. Not what he knows. What can be proven that he knew.
```
