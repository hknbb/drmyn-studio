```fountain
She looks at the space the way someone looks at a space when they are deciding what it means. Not aesthetically. Categorically. She is building a taxonomy: structures that could provide shelter, structures that could collapse on you, structures someone else might already be using, structures with sight lines she would want and structures with sight lines she would not.

The road.

The standpipe.

The sky — overcast, diffuse light, no shadows to orient by, which means she'll have to use other methods if she needs to navigate.

She turns in a full slow circle. Takes the full perimeter in.

---

She looks at her hands.

Her wrists have restraint marks — not deep, not broken skin, but the transport had been efficient and the restraints had been applied with the kind of tightening that accumulates over time.

She presses her thumb along the inside of her left wrist. Follows the bone. Presses.

Then she closes her left hand into a fist, opens it, closes it again.

Grip intact. No referred pain. The swelling is superficial.

She does the same with the right. Same result.

She rolls her shoulders. Notes the stiffness in her neck from however long she was restrained in transit. That one will work itself out. She doesn't count it.

---

Physical inventory. She does this without theatrics, the way you do a thing that needs to be done.

Clothing: practical, warm enough for the current temperature, not ideal for sustained outdoor exposure if the weather changes. No additional layers. No pockets that weren't emptied before transport — she checks them anyway, the reflex, finds nothing.

Footwear: good. The boots are hers from the residence. Whoever prepared her for transport was efficient but not thorough.

Hair: down. She pulls it back with nothing because she has nothing to pull it back with. Then she lets it go. She'll manage.

She looks around the immediate area. Finds a short piece of wire by the base of a wall — electrical, stripped at one end. She pockets it. Not because she knows what she'll use it for. Because it is a thing, and she has almost nothing, and almost nothing is the inventory she is working with.

---

The standpipe.

She crosses to it. The valve is stiff but it moves. The water that comes out is rust-colored for the first three seconds, then clears.

She lets it run clear. Drinks. The water is cold and tastes of old pipe and she drinks it anyway and does not make a face about it because there is no one to make a face for and the water is there.

She checks the structure the standpipe is mounted to. Load-bearing wall. Sound, she thinks. Probably sound.

---

She stands with the water still running.

She thinks about Roman.

Not the way she has been thinking about him — not the way she has been thinking about him since the room with the white table and the photograph of Jin. The other way. The operational way.

She has lived in his space for years. She has watched him design things — not just business systems, not just the event architecture — but the smaller systems too. The household routines he couldn't help imposing. The way he briefed his security staff. The way he arranged a room before a meeting. The way he handled variables he hadn't anticipated.

Roman does not tolerate redundancy. His systems are elegant in the way that things are elegant when they have been stripped of everything non-essential. He finds redundancy aesthetically offensive and practically wasteful.

This means his systems have single points.

She doesn't know what the single points in this system are yet. She doesn't know the territory, doesn't know the hunters, doesn't know what resources exist within the designated area. But she knows that the single points exist because Roman built this, and Roman builds everything the same way.

The hunters know this space. She doesn't.

She knows the architect. They don't.

She turns the standpipe valve closed.

---

The light is getting stronger. Whatever grace period she has before first contact — twelve hours, less, The Herald had implied something about twelve hours — she needs to use it.

She looks at the space again. Same space, different question: not what is here, but what can be done here.

She starts walking. Not away from anything. Toward the part of the district where the buildings are densest, where a person can move through multiple structures without being visible from a single vantage point.

She moves quickly but not at a run. She is not fleeing. She is beginning.

The wire is in her pocket.

She is in the game.
```
