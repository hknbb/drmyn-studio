Roman sets the phone on the shelf.

He looks at the document again. Then he picks it up, folds it once, and places it in his jacket pocket. It belongs to him. It is his now in the way that all confirmed facts become his — they enter the model, they update the price, they stop being events and become conditions.

He goes back to the small glass case.

He looks at it for a second time — longer this time, unguarded in the way a man is unguarded when he believes he is not being watched, which in this room he is not.

We still cannot see what is in the case.

He straightens. Adjusts his jacket. The decision is made and the man who made it has already moved past it.

He leaves the trophy room, closing the door behind him.

The objects on the shelves remain. The glass case. The document is gone but the room is the same room it was.

It is still expanding.


INT. NADIA'S MORNING ROOM — VALE RESIDENCE — MORNING

Early. The light is the specific pale of a morning that has not decided what it will be yet.

JIN is on a blanket on the floor. Eight months old. He is occupied with his hands — the specific serious occupation of a person who has recently discovered that hands exist and finds this remarkable. He is not doing anything that requires commentary.

NADIA sits on the floor beside him. She is not doing anything either, in the way that parents sometimes sit beside their children and simply be in the same space. She has a cup of something. She has not drunk from it in a while.

She watches him.

He does not perform for her. He is simply there, doing the work of being eight months old, which is considerable.

---

She leans forward and picks him up. Not urgently — the easy lift of someone who has done this thousands of times and knows exactly how he fits against her shoulder, the precise weight of him, the particular smell of the top of his head.

He makes a sound.

She makes a sound back — not a word, the register before words, the frequency between a person and the specific small person they are responsible for.

He settles.

---

She looks at his wrist.

The hospital bracelet from his last check-up — a pale blue band, his name printed on it in small type. It is slightly loose. She adjusts the clasp with one hand, the way she always does, the small automatic correction of a detail she has corrected before. She does not look at it as anything other than what it is.

He does not notice. He is looking at something above her shoulder with the focused attention of an eight-month-old who has found something worth examining in the middle distance.

She follows his gaze.

There is nothing there except light.

---

A sound. From another part of the house.

A door, or something like a door. The ordinary sounds of a large house in the morning — staff, the day beginning, nothing that requires attention.

Nadia looks up.

She listens for one beat. Two.

Nothing follows.

She looks back at Jin.

He is still looking at the light.

She puts her hand flat on his back — the whole hand, the whole warmth of it — and feels him breathe.

The morning continues.


INT. ROMAN'S FORMAL RECEPTION ROOM — VALE RESIDENCE — DAY

A room for occasions. Long table, formal chairs, the specific quality of a space that does not get used often and knows it. Roman has chosen this room deliberately.

He is already seated when NADIA enters. This, too, is deliberate.

DIMITRI stands near the far wall. Not threatening — positioned. The geometry of the room has been arranged before Nadia arrived.

She sits across from Roman.

She does not look at Dimitri.
