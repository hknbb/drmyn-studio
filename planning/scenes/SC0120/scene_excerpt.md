```fountain
The approach is the one she uses for everything that requires precision without sentiment: triage first. The documents in the first folder are foundational — the ones that generate the others, the ones that must be memorized first because everything else references them. She reads them the way she would read a patient's chart: date of birth, address history, employment record. She reads it twice. She does not highlight. She does not take notes. She puts it face-down on the table.

The photographs are next. They were taken three days ago. She needs to memorize them as documents of herself — not as images but as facts. Hair length. The angle of the jaw in this light. The specific way the shadow falls. She is studying the photograph of someone she will need to be able to describe accurately without looking at a photograph.

She raises her left arm to check the angle of a photograph against her mirror. The ribs object. She adjusts. She finds the position that gives her the information without the cost.

The position works. She notes the geometry. She will not need to raise the arm that way again.

---

The biographical materials on the plain paper are the most synthetic part — they require not memorization but inhabitation. A person who grew up somewhere has a relationship with that place. She reads the address on the paper and constructs the relationship: the specific quality of the light in that city, the name of a street she looked up, the kind of work the area supports. She is building a person the way she would build a wound dressing — from the bottom layer up, each layer supporting the next, nothing left exposed.

From the next room, the sound of Jin's breathing. He is asleep. He does not know yet what he will be called. She has chosen that too. The name she chose for him is ordinary and fits the profile and will give him the least distance to travel from what he already answers to.

She did not allow herself to choose a name she liked for sentiment. She chose the name that works.

---

The last thing on the list is her name.

She has known what it will be for two days. She did not write it down until now. She picks up the pen and writes it on the corner of the plain paper — just the name, nothing else — and looks at it.

Three seconds.

The name is ordinary. That is the point. It is the name of a person who has no history that intersects with ORACLE PRIME or VELTAIN or any of the architecture of the last four years. It is a starting position. It is not a hiding place.

Nadia Vale.

She looks at that name too — not written, just present — for the same three seconds. It was the name she came in with. It served. It is not the name she will go out with.

She folds the paper with the new name written on it. She does not fold the old name. There is no paper to fold. It is just a fact, now historical.

She picks up the next document in sequence.


INT. A ROOM — MORNING

Light through a window. A table. Two chairs. A glass of water that no one has drunk yet.

Jin is at the table. He is looking at the glass.

---

The light comes through the window at the angle that morning light comes through windows when the day is new and not yet doing anything in particular. It hits the glass of water and refracts — breaks into a small spread of color on the tablecloth, nothing dramatic, just the ordinary physics of light and water and the specific angle of the sun at this hour. Jin is looking at it. He has been looking at it for a while. He is not going to stop looking at it until the light changes or until something else becomes interesting. He tracks the color on the tablecloth as it shifts slightly with the movement of the glass, the small vibration of the table when she moves.

He is alive.

He is doing what he does.

---

She is at the other end of the table with something she is reading — practical documents, the work of the new beginning, nothing that requires crisis management, just the ordinary work of a person who is building something. She is not holding herself the way she held herself three weeks ago. The left side is still present — it will be present for some time longer — but the acute geometry of managing around it has loosened into something that is less calculation and more habit. The body is doing the slow work of repair. She is letting it.

She looks up.

---

He is still looking at the light on the tablecloth. The color has shifted slightly — the sun has moved, which it does, and the refraction has moved with it, and he has tracked the movement with the attention he brings to things that interest him and that do not require him to do anything except watch. He does not know she is looking at him. He does not need to.

She looks at him for a moment.

She does not mark the moment. There is nothing in the room that requires marking. She is not in a system that requires her to assess what she sees and produce a response to it. She is at a table in the morning with him. The light is doing what light does. He is watching it.

She looks back at what she was reading.

---

The room continues. The light moves. Jin watches it move.

Outside the window, the world is doing what the world does: ordinary, indifferent, continuing. The city. The street. The specific sound of a morning in a place she has not yet learned the sounds of. She will learn them. She has time.

The cost of everything that produced this morning is not gone. It is in her body, in the documents on the table, in the new name she answers to, in the specific quality of the caution she will probably always carry. The cost is real. It is not the whole of the picture.

Jin looks up from the tablecloth. He looks at the window.

He looks at the light at its source.
```
