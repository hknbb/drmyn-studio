INT. TRANSIT DISTRICT RENTED ROOM — NIGHT

A room that is used for this and nothing else. A bed that has not been slept in. A table with a field kit organized on it with the precision of a person who cannot afford to waste seconds on location. ZARA OKONKWO sits at the table with a secure reader in front of her and reads the assignment packet that came through forty minutes ago.

She has been in the hunter pool for fourteen months. She knows how to read a packet.

This one is maximum classification.

VALE, NADIA. She reads the name twice — not because she didn't catch it the first time but because the name carries a weight that her professional register identifies and sets aside. She has heard this name. Most people in the pool have heard this name by now. The packet tells her what she already knows and a great deal that she did not: movement history over the preceding seventy-two hours, last-known-position data from three separate sources, a physical damage register that is notably specific — broken ribs, right lateral, recent; impact-pattern consistent with corridor-scale engagement. Associates list. Threat assessment in the upper tier.

She reads the threat assessment carefully.

Whoever wrote it has spent real time with the data. The assessment is not promotional — the assessors who write for the pool sometimes inflate threat classifications to justify premium rates, but this one is flat and specific. VALE, NADIA is assessed as a trained close-combat operative with a documented history of improvised tactics under constraint. She does not fight predictably. She adapts faster than the standard operative window. The assessment notes: expect cost.

Zara reads: expect cost.

She closes that section and opens the payment terms.

Maximum bounty. Kill or capture, both classified at the same rate. A 72-hour operational window from assignment acceptance. Payment on confirmation of outcome.

She picks up the secure handset.

DISPATCH (V.O.)
(filtered, institutional)
Confirming receipt on assignment 1147.

ZARA
I need the payment credited against the account. Not transferred.

A pause that is about three seconds long.

DISPATCH (V.O.)
Clarify the account reference.

ZARA
You have it. The liability account. Full bounty, credited on confirmation.

Another pause. Shorter.

DISPATCH (V.O.)
Confirmed. Assignment 1147 active. Seventy-two hour window begins on this call.

ZARA
Understood.

She sets the handset down.

She does not move for a moment. She is aware of the confirmation she just received — the speed of it, the absence of pushback. They confirmed without routing it up. Which means the account is flagged as the preferred settlement mechanism, which means someone in the network's payment architecture has already categorized her as an operator who will negotiate toward the debt rather than cash. She is not a mystery to them.

She has just confirmed she is not a mystery to them.

She exhales once, through her nose. A small, controlled sound. Then she opens the target file again.

The intelligence review she conducts over the next twenty minutes goes further than standard assignment prep. She is not just establishing a pursuit profile — she is reading Nadia Vale the way you read a person you need to understand rather than simply locate. The improvised-tactic notation. The movement patterns that suggest training in disruption protocols. The damage register that says she has been in sustained engagement and has not broken.

There is a line in the associates section that she reads twice: CONTACT NETWORK — COMPROMISED. All primary channels under surveillance. Subject identified as attempting external contact.

Attempting.

Zara notes this. Subject has not yet reached anyone. She is isolated and she knows she is isolated and she is still moving.

Zara understands this condition with a specificity that the assignment packet cannot account for.

She begins organizing her kit. The photograph is in the kit — passport-sized, tucked behind the cipher card in the secondary pocket, exactly where it was in SC0031. A teenage girl. She has not moved the photograph to a safer location because there is no safer location and because moving it would be an admission of something she is not prepared to admit.

She looks at the photograph.

Three seconds. She gives it three seconds.

Then she opens the pursuit section of the target file and begins.

She has seventy-two hours and a name she has heard before and a threat assessment that says: expect cost.

She is the cost.
