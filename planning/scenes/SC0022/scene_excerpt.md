EXT. INDUSTRIAL LAYER — EAST OF THE OUTER DISTRICTS — MORNING

She enters the district from the south, using a service road that runs between two warehouse structures. The road is legitimate — delivery vehicles use it; there is no reason for a pedestrian not to. She walks at the pace of someone who has a destination, not someone who is hiding.

This is the first discipline of moving on-broadcast: look like a direction, not a flight.

The industrial layer is different from the outer districts. The architecture is working rather than residential. Metal roll-up doors, ventilation exhaust, the faint chemical smell of manufacturing process. The HALO UNIT grid here is standard resolution — she noted the coverage differential in the SC0017 reconnaissance data, the sensor density decreasing as the zoning shifted from residential to industrial. It does not mean unseen. It means the sensor sweep interval is longer. A different kind of problem.

She has approximately three minutes before the last known sweep position recycles to this corridor.

She uses them.

---

Walking. Observing.

The industrial layer runs its own economy on its own schedule. At this hour: two delivery vehicles, both at separate loading docks, both with drivers who have the body language of people working a regular route. A maintenance crew at the far end of the road replacing a section of ducting. They are not watching her.

She moves past them.

Her left hand is in her jacket pocket. The dressing is intact — she can feel the wire through the fabric, the slight raised profile of the outer wrap. The grip compensation she made at the standpipe earlier has already become automatic: when she reaches for anything with her left hand, the wrist angles slightly outward. She has done this before. Not this injury, but this category of injury. The adjustment settles into her movement within the first hundred meters.

She notes this. Filed under: operational, continuing.

---

She stops at the south end of a long building — pressed flat to its wall, out of the sweep arc for another sixty seconds. Not hiding. Resting her route between surveillance intervals, which is something entirely different.

Here, for one moment, she stops doing.

The sequence is behind her: the drop, SC0016's broadcast launch, the street reconnaissance, the HALO UNIT encounter, Solv, the courtyard, the dressing. Forty-five minutes of sustained operational demand, and she is still here, still mobile, wound managed, cover broken but not terminal. The game's first phase has concluded.

Her body knows this before she names it. The chest unlocks fractionally. Not relief — the word is wrong. A recalibration. The body's post-crisis systems running their audit: heart rate, respiratory, muscle readiness. She lets this take exactly as long as it needs.

Nine seconds. Then she is back.

---

She scans the district with the same attention she gave the outer districts during reconnaissance, but slower. The immediate threat index is lower here. She has time to read rather than just survive.

What she reads:

A repair and refurbishment unit, three buildings north on the eastern side of the road. Operating now, at this hour. Lights on, a vehicle backed to the loading dock — not a delivery vehicle, something older, modified, the kind of modification that suggests the owner does their own mechanical work. The personnel count: two visible, one probable inside. Movement rhythm: unhurried, practiced, the specific quality of people performing a routine they have performed many times in a space they own.

The detail she notes: no visible branding. The unit has a registration number on the door — every building here does — but no company name, no logo, no presence on the surface visible to a passerby. Invisible by design or by preference.

A micro-economy operating adjacent to the visible city. She has seen the type before: not illegal, exactly, but running on a different set of relationships than the registered economy. The people in these spaces know each other. They solve each other's problems. They do not ask whose van it is.

She cannot confirm this inference. She has a registration number, a building silhouette, and a movement pattern. That is not enough to approach.

But it is enough to note.

She memorizes the address. Cross-references it to the sweep interval geography: approaching from the east, using the ducting maintenance crew as cover for the final thirty meters, would bring her to the unit's rear entrance in a single surveillance gap.

Not now. When she has more.

She pushes off the wall and continues north, at the pace of someone who has a destination.


INT. INDUSTRIAL LAYER — MAINTENANCE RECESS — MORNING

She is in the gap between a warehouse's eastern wall and a ventilation infrastructure unit — approximately two meters of cover, not visible from either road surface. The ventilation unit runs at low continuous frequency. A four-minute sweep interval here; she timed it on the approach. The longest interval she has found.

She uses it.

---

What she knows, confirmed.

The game is live. The broadcast is operational. The HALO UNIT sensor network covers the city at standard resolution in industrial zones, enhanced resolution in residential and commercial zones — the differential is a structural feature, not a deployment error. Two hunters confirmed in field deployment when she spoke to The Herald at the warehouse passage. One in transit. Hunter roster unknown beyond that.

The terminal conditions: a duration-based event. She does not know the endpoint. She knows the event has one because Roman told her the game runs 'for as long as the event runs' — which presupposes a scheduled close. She cannot derive the endpoint from available data.

The betting markets are live. The odds on her survival move in real time with aggregate wagering. The audience is distributed, watching, and putting money on whether she lives.

She holds that last item for one breath — not in disbelief, but to ensure she has correctly priced it as a factor. It is a factor. She files it.
