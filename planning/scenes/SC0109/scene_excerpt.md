INT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — SERVER CORE ANTECHAMBER — CONTINUOUS

The credential lock is a circular reader set into the server core door's frame at chest height — the kind of hardware that is not networked, not remotely addressable, designed to operate independently of the systems it protects. Physical. Dedicated. The one component of Roman's machine built to resist everything except the card.

She has the card.

She places it against the reader. Not inserted — contact first, the way she memorized from Morrow's documentation: present the card to the reader's surface, wait for the orientation indicator, align, insert. She has not run this in a controlled environment. She has run it fourteen times in the last three days in her memory, on the walk through checkpoints she had not yet reached, in the eight seconds between Zone 2 and Zone 3, in the trophy room while she was reading closed event files. The sequence is precise and she has it precisely.

The orientation indicator appears: a small amber light at the reader's top edge.

She aligns.

She inserts.

SERVER CORE CREDENTIAL LOCK:
ADMINISTRATOR AUTHORIZATION — ACTIVE
ORACLE PRIME SHUTDOWN SEQUENCE: INITIALIZED

The door does not open. It was never going to open. The card's function is not entry.

She steps back from the reader.

Through the server core door and through the antechamber walls — everywhere, because this is what proximity to the server core sounds like — the facility-grade cooling at operating pitch, the composite hum of infrastructure at full load. She has been hearing it since she moved through the security station. It has been the room's continuous register. She is hearing it now.

Then something changes.

Not silence — not yet. The cooling continues. The composite continues. But a frequency that was present in the building's operating sound drops out: a load that was running stops carrying. The change is specific enough that she can locate it in the spectrum of what she has been hearing.

She knows what ORACLE PRIME sounds like at full operation. She has been in its proximity for weeks. She knows what this particular frequency means.

ORACLE PRIME BROADCAST ARCHITECTURE
GRACEFUL HALT IN PROGRESS — CONSTITUENT SYSTEMS TERMINATING
ESTIMATED COMPLETION: 00:43

From the broadcast terminal room two levels above: The Herald completes its current sentence. The machine was designed for this — graceful halt preserves current-sentence completion to avoid mid-broadcast interrupt artifacts. The warm register finishes what it started. The calibrated empathy runs to the period. Then it does not begin the next one.

The system sheds load in sequence. Outer-ring thermal management first. Processing units next. Storage arrays. The broadcast synthesis cores last — the components that make The Herald sound like it means what it says. Each one drops off in the order the documentation specified. She timed this sequence from Morrow's notes. Forty-three seconds from authorization to full halt.

She does not count.

She knows when it is done.

The sound stops.

Not a reduction. Not a wind-down toward silence. The specific composite register of ORACLE PRIME's infrastructure — everything that has been underneath Roman's operation since before this compound had a name for what it was building — stops. What remains is the building: climate control, structure, the ordinary ambient of a facility that is large and full of edges she has not yet reached.

Through the secondary door to her left — the door that is not fully closed, the door with the light — the pacing stops.

Roman heard it.

She stands in the antechamber with the card in her hand and the machine finished and the silence where the machine was.

She does not look at the credential reader.

She does not confirm what the display says. She was there. She knows what happened.

Jin is in the building.

She has, she estimates, six minutes before the compound's emergency protocols cycle to a lockdown state that will change the lower-level geometry in ways she has not fully mapped. She made this estimate before she entered the compound. She is making it again now — with the machine gone and Roman in the room to her left who has just heard the machine go and six minutes as the operative constraint.

She turns toward the antechamber's outer door.
