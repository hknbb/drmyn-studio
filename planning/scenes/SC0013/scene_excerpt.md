NADIA sits at the long table.

The room is exactly as it was.

Her hands are folded in front of her.

She does not move.


INT. NADIA'S PRIVATE ROOM — VALE RESIDENCE — LATE AFTERNOON

A window. The light coming through it at a low angle, the kind that will be gone in an hour.

NADIA is standing at the window. She has been here since SC0012. The room is quiet and ordered — her order, not the household's; she keeps her own things in their own arrangement.

From somewhere in the house, below or in the east wing: a sound. Not a gunshot — nothing so clear. A door. Movement. The specific quality of a household absorbing something it has absorbed before.

She does not turn.

---

A knock. Controlled. Professional.

DIMITRI enters.

He stands just inside the door. He does not sit; this is not that kind of visit.

DIMITRI
Mr. Vale asked me to inform you directly.

He pauses.

DIMITRI (CONT'D)
Marcus Havel died this afternoon. The matter has been resolved.

Nadia does not turn from the window.

DIMITRI (CONT'D)
His effects and contacts are being documented as a standard precaution. There was a phone registered to a private address — a residential contact named Vogt, on Brenner Lane. We'll clear that as well.

He waits.

The room is quiet.

DIMITRI (CONT'D)
Is there anything you need?

Nadia says nothing.

Dimitri reads the room. He has been doing this long enough to know what silence means and what it doesn't. He nods — to himself, not to her.

DIMITRI (CONT'D)
I'll let you know if there's anything further.

He leaves. The door closes. Quietly, professionally, the way everything in this house closes.

---

NADIA has not moved.

The light through the window is getting lower.

She is looking at the glass — not through it, not at the view; at the glass itself, the way you look at something when you are not looking at anything.

She breathes in. Slowly. Out.

Her hands, which were at her sides, come together in front of her. One clasp. Still.

She is not crying.

She is not going to cry.

Something in her face makes a very small, very permanent decision, and then her face is still again.

The light falls.
