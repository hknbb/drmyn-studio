EXT. VELTAIN COMPOUND — PRIMARY APPROACH / NORTH GATE — NIGHT

The VELTAIN compound's primary approach is a broad paved lane between ornamental plantings — the kind of landscape design that is also perimeter control, each bed placed at the precise distance from the gate that eliminates cover. The north gate itself is steel, flush with the compound wall, a vehicle barrier embedded beneath the pavement. There are two visible cameras and one that is not visible to anyone who has not read the security infrastructure documentation.

ZARA is at the east end of the approach lane, in the shadow of the ornamental hedge line, watching the gate.

She has been watching for four minutes.

She knows the shift pattern: two guards at the gate, a third on the wall walk above, a response team of four staged at the interior checkpoint whose response time to a perimeter alert is forty seconds. She knows the camera coverage. She knows that the north gate is the approach point that Hilda Krast's architecture is most prepared for, because it is the most direct approach, and because direct approaches are what professional security forces train against.

She has chosen it because she needs Hilda to commit, not split.

She exhales once, through her nose. The sound of accepting a cost. It is the last sound she makes that is not operational.

She moves into the approach lane.

She does not move like a person who is trying to be invisible. A person trying to be invisible moves too carefully, and careful movement at the wrong pace triggers the motion sensors more reliably than ordinary movement. Zara moves like a person who is supposed to be here and has a specific destination and is going to reach it. The motion sensors read her velocity and bearing and flag her to the monitoring station. This is what she needs them to do.

The gate cameras pick her up at forty meters. She knows the moment they have her — the slight adjustment in the wall-walk guard's posture, the radio call she cannot hear, the interior checkpoint response timer that has just started.

Thirty-two seconds.

She keeps moving. She reaches into her jacket and produces the compound access card she has been carrying for six days — a card cloned from the ORACLE PRIME maintenance contractor registry, which will not open the north gate because the north gate uses a separate authentication system, which she already knew. The card is not for opening the gate. The card is for making the security team read her as a sophisticated intrusion attempt rather than a disoriented civilian, which determines whether they call Hilda directly or route through the duty supervisor.

They call Hilda directly.

The north gate opens — not to admit her; the vehicle barrier rises as a tactical measure, because a lowered barrier is a ramming hazard, which is standard protocol for a credentialed intrusion attempt at the primary approach. Zara notes this and adjusts her line, coming at the gate from a slight angle that keeps her out of the vehicle barrier's operational arc.

The four-person response team comes through the inner door at a run. Professional, spread formation, not shouting. Hilda Krast is three steps behind them.

Hilda is fifty-one years old. She moves at the pace of a person who does not need to run because she has already calculated the geometry and found it in her favor. She surveys the approach lane — Zara, the card, the angle of approach, the absence of any secondary actor at the perimeter.

A beat.

HILDA
(to her team, not to Zara)
Hold.

The team holds. Zara stops walking.

They assess each other across twelve meters of ornamental paving.

HILDA (CONT'D)
You drew us here.

Not a question. Not an accusation. A statement of the operational fact, delivered in the register of a professional who has identified the mechanism and is now working out what the mechanism was for. Her eyes move — not to the north gate, but to her left. East. The service corridor.

Zara says nothing.

Hilda's eyes come back to her.

HILDA (CONT'D)
(to team)
East service corridor. Now.

Two of the four break. They are professionals; they move fast. But they will not reach the service corridor before Nadia reaches the basement. The gap is the gap. The gap has always been the gap.

The remaining two approach Zara. She does not move. She does not assist them, but she does not resist in a way that requires force — that would take time, create injury, complicate everything downstream. She lets them take her arms. Professional custody. Hilda watches.

HILDA
(to Zara directly, for the first time)
Was it worth it?

ZARA
(a beat — not performance; she is genuinely considering)
Ask me after.

Hilda looks at her. Then: something in Hilda's expression that is not sympathy and is not contempt. It is the specific expression of a professional who has just encountered someone whose operational logic she does not fully understand and knows she will not understand tonight.

HILDA
(to team)
Take her inside.

Zara is walked through the north gate.

She does not look back at the approach lane. She does not calculate the distance Nadia has covered in the last three minutes. She has stopped calculating. She has made the decision she was always going to make once she understood that the debt was never going to be paid by calculation — that the only move left was the one that had a cost she couldn't first reduce to an acceptable number.

She is inside. The north gate closes behind her.

Somewhere on the east face of the compound, in the service passage that leads to the basement corridor, a clock is running. Thirteen minutes.
