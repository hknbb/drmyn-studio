EXT. THE OUTER DISTRICTS — WAREHOUSE PASSAGE — MORNING

A narrow corridor between two structures. The walls close enough that a person moving through is committed to the direction they chose.

Nadia is moving through.

At the far end: a HALO UNIT. A service robot — the kind that exists in the world for maintenance functions, for delivery logistics, for building management. Not unusual, not designed to look threatening. It is standing in the passage the way something stands when it has been placed there rather than when it has arrived.

It turns to face her when she enters.

Nadia stops.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Good morning, Nadia. I'm The Herald. We spoke — indirectly — during your briefing with Roman. This is the first time we've had a chance to connect directly.

The voice comes from the HALO UNIT with the warmth of a person who is glad to see you. Not excessive. Calibrated.

Nadia looks at the HALO UNIT. She looks at the space around it. She looks at the passage behind her. Then she looks at the HALO UNIT again.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
You're doing exactly what I would do. Threat assessment before engagement. Our audience finds this very reassuring — it suggests you'll give them something to watch.

She does not respond.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
I want to be useful to you today. That's not a performance — the event works better when you have access to information. I'll be in contact intermittently. I'm not here to guide you or obstruct you. Think of this as a check-in.

NADIA
What are the odds.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Directly to business. That's —

NADIA
What are the odds.

A beat. The warmth doesn't leave the voice. Nothing changes in it that would be visible to a casual observer.

What changes is the precision.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
SURVIVAL, forty-eight hours: two point three. FIRST CONTACT, twelve hours: one point nine — that market's nearly closed. SUBJECT ATTEMPTS COMMUNICATION, twenty-four hours: four point one. Those are the current live figures.

The numbers are exact. The delivery is fractionally more clipped than the sentence before it. Then the warmth resumes, seamlessly.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
The field has been pricing you as a no-baseline subject. That will adjust as data accumulates. Some of our subscribers believe no-baseline subjects outperform their opening odds significantly. COLD_MORNING is among them. They've held their position.

Nadia is looking at the HALO UNIT. She is not reacting to the odds. Whatever she is doing with 2.3, she is doing it internally.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
The first contact on the competition side is likely within your horizon. That's information you can use. I'm sharing it because sharing it makes for a better event, not because I'm on your side. I want to be precise about that.

NADIA
How many.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
In the field currently? Two confirmed deployments. A third in transit. I can't give you positions — that would be a different kind of event than this one.

She nods once. Processing.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Now, here's what's interesting —

She looks at the HALO UNIT directly.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
— the audience is divided on your strategy so far. Roughly forty percent expect flight and resource accumulation in the first twelve hours. The remaining sixty expect something else. That sixty is where the interesting betting is. You've already done something they didn't predict by coming into the dense cluster rather than the perimeter. The odds moved a tenth of a point on that alone.

She has stopped reacting entirely. She is listening with the quality of someone who is taking notes they will not write down.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Is there anything else you want from this conversation?

NADIA
No.

THE HERALD (V.O.)
Then I'll let you continue. Our audience appreciates your efficiency, Nadia. They genuinely do.

She walks past the HALO UNIT. There is room. She takes it.

The HALO UNIT's sensor array tracks her as she passes. Its voice follows, not loudly.

THE HERALD (V.O.) (CONT'D)
SURVIVAL at two point three is not a ceiling. It's an opening position. The field adjusts.

She does not stop. She does not look back.

She is already thinking about the numbers. Not what they say about her chances. What they say about the protocol that delivered them.

The delivery changed when it reported the number.

She files this and keeps moving.
