What the preliminary pass missed:

The draw cut is not the only injury. The encounter that produced it produced other things: bruising along the left lateral rib cage from an impact that she categorized as non-structural and set aside; abrasion on the right forearm from the surface she landed on during separation; and — this is the thing that concerns him — a secondary wound channel below the primary suture line that the field dressing had been compressing without either of them knowing it.

He works without speaking. His hands take longer than they did in the first session.

She reads the duration of his attention the way she would read any technical document: the length of focus is proportional to the complexity of the problem. The problem is more complex than the first session suggested.

She has been treating herself as a mechanism. He is treating her as a person who has been damaged. The distinction is informational, and she receives it as such.

---

Midway through, his diagnostic chatter comes back:

TOBIAS
(to himself)
...secondary channel, maybe eight millimeters, probably longer — clean edge, not serrated, good...

He stops. He found the full extent.

Silence. His hands continue.

---

After a time:

TOBIAS
I need — there's a supplier. OTTO. Works out of the Mern district. Compression material, suture grade. I can get proper supplies if I —

He stops himself.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
Never mind. I have what I need.

He goes back to work.

---

She has heard the name.

OTTO.

She does not ask. She does not repeat it. She routes it through her model in the same way she routes everything: as a fact, with a location attached to it, to be assessed when she has time to assess it.

Marcus said the name. Before everything started. In the context of a precaution.

The name exists in two parts of her world now.

She does not react.

---

He finishes. He examines the work. He applies the final dressing with the same excessive precision he brought to his instrument reorganization in the previous session.

TOBIAS
The rib cage bruising will limit your range of motion on the left side for — it doesn't matter how long. It will limit it. Factor that in.

She absorbs this as an operational parameter.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
The treatment buys time. Not recovery. You understand the distinction.

NADIA
Yes.

A beat.

TOBIAS
Then you should go. While there's still a gap.

She stands. She looks at the arm.

The dressing is correct. The stabilization is real. What the machine has cost her body is also real, and it is now a known variable rather than a managed unknown. These are not the same thing.

She goes.
