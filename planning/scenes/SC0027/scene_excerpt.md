```fountain
He works without speaking.

The technique is exact — debriding the wound margins, addressing the maceration, re-suturing with appropriate material at appropriate tension. His hands do not hesitate. The hesitation that inflected everything at the door is not present here.

Midway through the second suture pass, he says — quietly, not to her:

TOBIAS
(to himself)
...proximal to distal. Four bites. Approximate, don't invert...

The note belongs to a different room: a chart on the wall, a logged procedure, a license number on a placard by the door. He has been keeping his own record for four years, without the chart. He does not appear to notice that he has spoken.

She does.

---

He finishes. Examines the suture line. Reaches for a proper dressing, sizes it, applies it with the compression the improvised version could not provide.

TOBIAS
It'll hold. Probably.

NADIA
How long?

TOBIAS
Properly maintained — seventy-two hours. You don't maintain it, forty.

She stands. She is already looking at the door.

TOBIAS (CONT'D)
Don't come back unless it's necessary.

She leaves.

---

He stands at the table.

He picks up the instrument he used last and places it in the correct position in the cabinet. Then the one before that. Then the one before that. The organization, when he finishes, is more precise than it was when he started.

His hands are still.

He resented helping her. He also could not have done otherwise.

Both facts are true and he is not going to resolve the tension between them tonight.


EXT. TOBIAS'S REPAIR UNIT — REAR ALLEY — TWELVE MINUTES LATER

The dressing has soaked through in one corner.

She has been waiting in the cut between this building and the next — a space approximately seventy centimeters wide, not surveilled — for a gap in the HALO UNIT rotation. The gap is not coming at the interval she predicted. She is still here. Her left arm is at her side. She can feel the corner of the dressing against the fabric.

She looks at it.

She looks at the rear entrance.

She goes back.

---

INT. TOBIAS'S REPAIR UNIT — INTERIOR — CONTINUOUS

She knocks once.

The lock turns. He opens the door.

He looks at the dressing. He does not say anything about the dressing. He steps back.

She comes in.

---

This time there is no transaction to establish. He goes directly to the supply cabinet while she sits on the table. He does not motion at the table; she finds it herself.

He removes the dressing. He looks at what is underneath for a moment — not speaking, not reacting. He is cataloguing.

Then he begins.
```
