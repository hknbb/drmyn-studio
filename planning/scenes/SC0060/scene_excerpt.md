INT. 14 HAVEL COURT — SAFE HOUSE UNIT — THIRD FLOOR — NIGHT

Tobias has opened his medical kit on the supply table. She is at the east window.

The alley below is empty. A service road runs left, connecting at the junction sixty meters ahead to the Soral access road. She has verified it from here: the geography is real. Someone positioned this safe house with genuine operational intelligence about the northnorthwest route.

That is the thing about a well-built trap. The geography works.

She says:

NADIA
The NNW exit is real. I've confirmed it from the window.

Tobias does not look up from the kit.

TOBIAS
I know.

NADIA
If we leave tonight, we lose the Soral window. There's a transit restriction on the access road between 0400 and 0600. We'd be moving against it in the dark with no forward cover confirmed.

TOBIAS
And the wound.

NADIA
And the wound.

He holds up the suture kit — the pre-threaded, pre-staged, wound-specific version she found on the supply table.

TOBIAS
This is made for a penetration wound. Someone knew.

NADIA
Everyone knows. That's not a reason to leave it here.

She turns from the window. The rationalization has three parts; she has been building it for the last fifteen minutes.

Part one: the exit route is real and verified and timed-out for night movement — which means staying until before 0400 is the operationally correct use of the route, not a concession to the trap.

Part two: the wound. She cannot suture the entry point herself with the precision needed. She has tried. She knows what the attempt costs in time and in wound quality. Tobias can do it in forty minutes. Forty minutes stationary now versus six hours of increasing infection risk on a night movement route with compromised mobility.

Part three: they have been in the building for twenty-three minutes and the trap has not activated. Either the mechanism is on a longer timer, or it requires a specific trigger — departure attempt, communication hardware activation, something she has not yet done — or the operational response to the channel flag has not been dispatched. Twenty-three minutes without activation is not evidence of safety. It is evidence of patience. Roman is patient.

She holds part three in her mind for a moment.

Then:

NADIA
The trap hasn't activated in twenty-three minutes. That's either patience or a delayed mechanism. If it's patience, we have a window. If it's delayed, we may not. I don't know which.

TOBIAS
You're going to stay anyway.

It is not a question.

NADIA
For the suture. And the 0400 window. Nothing else.

TOBIAS
(muttering, already reaching for the instrument)
Entry wound, anterior aspect, some tissue involvement, going to need two layers probably three depending on—

He stops. Recalibrates. Speaks again — to her this time, not himself.

TOBIAS
You know they may have adjusted for two people. Since I came in.

NADIA
I know.

TOBIAS
And you're staying.

NADIA
The decision is borderline.

A beat.

TOBIAS
Sit down.

She moves from the window to the chair. That is the decision.

Tobias works. His technical commentary resumes under his breath — layer count, tissue state, instrument sequence. The cadence of it is slightly faster than it would be in a neutral environment; his diagnostic chatter running at the rate of a man performing competence while afraid, which is not the same as a man who is losing.

She watches the instruments. She does not watch the door.

The route is partially real. That is the trap's architecture and its leverage simultaneously. A fake rescue made of fake geography and fake resources would have been easy to leave. Roman did not build that rescue. He built one that works.

She is using it anyway.

The suture holds.
