INT. ZORAL CORRIDOR — CHEMICAL TRANSIT DEPOT, DRUM STORAGE BAY — NIGHT (CONTINUOUS)

ZARA OKONKWO comes through the bay entrance at the pace of someone who has already made her decision and is now executing it. She does not run. She does not hesitate in the doorway.

She sees: Piotr Ganz, twelve meters from the target, closing. The target at the wall. The two HALO units on the south wall, camera arrays rotating.

She places herself between Piotr and the HALO sightline — not blocking it, entering it — and speaks at operational volume. The HALO units catch everything.

ZARA
Ganz.

He stops.

ZARA (CONT'D)
This corridor was in my advance filing. You deployed after confirmation.

A beat. Piotr does not turn to look at her immediately. He is assessing the situation: another hunter, a priority claim, a broadcast in progress.

PIOTR GANZ
(without turning)
Your filing covers the transit zone. Not the depot interior.

ZARA
The depot is contiguous with the transit zone on the grid. That's a zone boundary dispute, not a filing conflict. I take zone boundary disputes to the protocol committee.

Now he turns. He looks at her with the patience of someone who has all the time his targets do not.

PIOTR GANZ
You filed at fourteen-hundred. I confirmed deployment at fourteen-twelve.

ZARA
Confirmation sequence doesn't supersede advance zone coverage. That's protocol seven, section four.

PIOTR GANZ
Section four covers exterior perimeter, not interior structure.

ZARA
The depot has no designated interior designation on the current operational grid. Absent designation, the exterior filing holds.

She is not looking at the target. She is not going to look at the target.

NADIA, at the wall, watches two professional killers argue about paperwork. She does not understand what she is looking at. She understands, without reasoning it through, that this is a window. She catalogs what she has: forearm bleeding, right side compromised, the bay entrance is twelve meters to her left, the argument is consuming both of them.

She moves left.

Not fast — controlled, lateral, using the drum cradles as cover. One step. Two. The argument continues at the same volume.

PIOTR GANZ
(to Zara, not turning)
You are disrupting an active capture within my confirmation window. That is a pack-coordination violation.

ZARA
You're making that argument on a disputed zone. I'd document my side of that very carefully before filing it.

Three steps. Nadia is at the edge of the cradle line. The bay entrance is six meters.

PIOTR GANZ
The timestamp will resolve this.

ZARA
Let it resolve it. I'm not moving until it does.

She shifts two steps to her left. It is a positional move in the argument — she is angling toward Piotr to make her claim more physically present. She is not looking at the drainage channel. She is not looking at the bay entrance. She is not looking at Nadia.

The sightline between Piotr and the bay entrance is clear. Nadia takes it.

She does not run — running is noise, running draws the eye. She moves at the pace of someone who is managing a body that has a displaced rib and a bleeding forearm and has decided these are operational parameters, not stopping conditions. She reaches the bay entrance. She goes through it.

She does not look back.

Piotr hears the difference in the space — the depot's acoustic character changes fractionally when someone exits the bay. He registers it.

PIOTR GANZ
(flat)
She's gone.

ZARA
I see that.

A pause that is not comfortable.

PIOTR GANZ
You moved.

ZARA
I moved toward you. To make a point about the filing.

He looks at her for a moment with the patience of someone who has just processed a situation and reached a conclusion he will not state aloud.

PIOTR GANZ
The timestamp will not support your zone-coverage argument.

ZARA
Then I'll file the dispute and find out.

She says it with the professional certainty of someone who has made calculations and is prepared to stand behind them. She is not going to look toward the bay entrance. She is not going to look toward the drainage channel. She is going to hold this space and this argument until it has been argued long enough to be finished.

PIOTR GANZ
Protocol seven, section four, subsection two. Interior structures require a separate designation filing.

ZARA
I'll check my documentation.

A beat.

PIOTR GANZ
Good night.

He turns and walks toward the drainage channel. He does not run either. He moves at the pace of a man who understands that running after a target who has a head start in a depot he knows better than you do is not efficient, and efficiency is the only value he maintains.

Zara watches him go.

The two HALO units on the south wall complete their rotation cycles and begin again.

She stands in the drum storage bay for another twelve seconds. Then she turns and walks to the bay entrance. She goes in the opposite direction from either of them.


She does not know if she made the right decision. She knows what she did.

On the south wall, the HALO units record an empty bay.
