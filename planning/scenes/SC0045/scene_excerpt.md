INT./EXT. ZORAL CORRIDOR — STORM DRAINAGE CULVERT, UNDER TRANSIT ROAD — NIGHT

The culvert is two meters wide and smells of old concrete and standing water. NADIA moves through it at a controlled pace — not running. She is past running. She is in the phase that comes after running, which is the phase where you assess what you still have.

She stops. She needs thirty seconds.

She sits with her back against the culvert wall, forearm pressed to her side. Takes inventory: the forearm laceration has slowed — pressure from movement. The rib is not broken, displaced, possibly cracked, operational. She has been moving on it for forty minutes. She can keep moving on it.

Thirty seconds.

She replays the drum bay. This is what she does when she needs to understand something: she replays it in sequence, clinically, without interpretation first.

A hunter appeared. ZARA OKONKWO — she knows the name from the ORACLE PRIME dossier The Herald had displayed in SC0040. Zara entered the bay. She claimed prior zone-coverage on the depot corridor. She argued it. She did not look at Nadia. She made a positional move that cleared the sightline to the bay entrance. Nadia went through the bay entrance. Zara did not pursue.

That is the event sequence. That is all she can certify.

Now the interpretations.

ONE: genuine dispute. Zara filed an advance claim. Piotr Ganz deployed into her zone. She enforced her filing rights, not because she was protecting Nadia, but because that is how the hunter pool works and Zara enforces her filings. The escape is incidental — a consequence of the dispute, not a goal.

TWO: trap. The intervention was designed to make Nadia feel that she has a potential ally in the field. She is now moving with a lower threat assessment on Zara than she should have, and that miscalibration is the product. Zara is somewhere behind her, waiting for Nadia to act on the false sense of security.

THREE: Roman's design. Roman instructed Zara to create the impression of a defection — a honey positioning, to make Nadia reachable through a false ally. More sophisticated than trap. More expensive to maintain. Not impossible.

FOUR: genuine defection. Zara has reasons of her own — Nadia does not know them — and used the dispute framing as cover because the broadcast was running. She chose Nadia over the capture. She will make contact when it is safe to do so.

FIVE: operational error. Zara misjudged her positional move and accidentally cleared the sightline.

She eliminates five.

Zara's move was two steps, lateral, calculated to the centimeter. It covered the exact angle Piotr had been occupying. She had been in the bay for less than ninety seconds when she made it. You do not make that move by accident. You make that move because you have assessed the geometry of the space and you know what it will do.

Four explanations remain. Including trap. Including Roman's design.

She tries to find the angle at which trap does not fit.

The problem is: every detail that makes the intervention look like a defection also makes it look like a well-executed trap. Zara did not look at her. A genuine defector might not look at her either — to avoid drawing Piotr's attention to the window. Zara moved in a way that created a gap. A trap operative would do exactly the same thing. Zara did not pursue after she was gone. A genuine defector would not pursue. A trap operative, having successfully placed the belief in Nadia's model, would also not pursue — there would be no need to.

She cannot find the angle. Trap fits every observable.

She holds this for a moment in the dark of the culvert. The water at the base of the channel is cold and she can feel it through her boot.

She is not going to resolve this. She does not have enough data to resolve this, and waiting for more data before she moves is not an available option. She has to move.

What she can do is hold both hypotheses — trap/not-trap — and file the anomaly without closing it. Let it stay open. Let it be expensive. Pay the cost of carrying it.

Nadia adds a new entry to her threat model. The entry has no parallel in her existing categories: Hostile. Neutral. Resource. This one does not fit any of those.

She calls it OPEN.

The OPEN entry contains: Zara Okonkwo. One confirmed fact. No confirmed explanation. Trap flag: active. Discard flag: inactive.

She does not know what to do with this entry yet. She knows she is not going to delete it.

Nadia stands up. Forearm still pressed to her side. She looks at the culvert exit — the opening is fifty meters ahead, a circle of less-dark sky. The transit road above her carries no traffic at this hour. She is outside the broadcast grid.

She moves.

The weight in her model is different now. It has more in it. One more thing she is carrying that she cannot put down.

She moves anyway.


EXT. ZORAL CORRIDOR — FREIGHT TRANSFER DEPOT, OUTER GATE — PRE-DAWN

LOTTI is not surprised to see her. That is the thing about LOTTI: she operates in the category of people who are not surprised by logistics clients appearing at irregular hours with injuries they don't explain. She looks at Nadia the way you look at a shipment that arrived slightly damaged — workable, but noted.

LOTTI
You're late.

NADIA
I was delayed.

LOTTI
Mm.

She does not ask about the forearm. She is already moving toward the depot gate, and the expectation is that Nadia will follow. Nadia follows.

LOTTI
I have a pick-up. Twenty minutes. You stay out of the way.

NADIA
Fine.
