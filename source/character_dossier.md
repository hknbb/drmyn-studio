### Character C01 — NADIA VALE

- **Character ID:** C01
- **Name:** NADIA VALE (born NADIA KAREV)
- **Blueprint Role:** Protagonist
- **Blueprint Cross-Reference:** Section 2 (Protagonist Core); Section 3 (Character C01); Section 4 (C01↔C02, C01↔C03, C01↔C04, C01↔C05, C01↔C12)
- **Narrative Function Under Pressure:** Nadia is the film's moral and physical center of gravity. Under pressure she performs two simultaneous functions: she is the audience's primary identification point and she is the system's most intelligent adversary. The pressure system tests whether her default survival strategy — compliance, concealment, tactical patience — can transform into genuine agency before it gets her killed. Every time the system offers a false escape, her function is to read it as a trap, even when the temptation to take it is overwhelming.
- **Wound:** During her last field deployment as a military medic, her commanding officer issued an order that Nadia knew was wrong — not catastrophically, but wrongly calibrated. She complied because the cost of dissent felt higher than the cost of compliance. A patient died who might not have. The military inquiry cleared everyone; no one was held. She left the service and told herself the guilt was irrational. It isn't. The wound is: compliance with a predatory authority feels like safety and is, in fact, surrender. She rebuilt her life by choosing a different predatory authority — Roman — because the cage was more comfortable than the exposed field and the exposed field had already cost her once.
- **Mask:** Controlled composure. She moves with deliberate economy, speaks with precision, and presents the outward affect of a woman who has made peace with her constraints. The mask is not passive; it requires constant, skilled maintenance. Underneath it is not chaos — it is a precisely targeted fury she has decided she cannot afford. The mask begins cracking from SC0006 onward and is fully gone by SC0076.
- **Pressure / Test Logic:** The story systematically strips the compliance mask by removing every option that compliance would provide — Roman's protection, financial safety, social infrastructure, physical comfort, medical access, communication channels — until Nadia has only her own unmediated judgment and the tactical skills she has been suppressing. The test is not whether she can survive physically. The test is whether she can act from her own authority rather than someone else's system. The mask cracks when she must act before she has permission. It shatters when she chooses to use the broadcast as a weapon — when she decides to be seen.
- **Want:** Recover Jin from Roman's custody and reach permanent safety beyond Roman's reach.
- **Need:** To act from her own authority rather than surviving inside someone else's system — to stop treating compliance as a form of safety.
- **Fear Trigger:** That her caution and patience will cost Jin exactly the way her compliance in the field cost her patient. That she will have waited past the point of rescue.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Lexicon:** Medical precision register — she names injuries, anatomizes situations, gives differential assessments. When emotional, her vocabulary contracts rather than expands; she strips modifiers. The language of controlled clinical economy. No profanity as performance; when she swears it is accurate and brief. She does not use metaphor under pressure; she uses direct description. Her professional vocabulary is medical; her social vocabulary is careful and slightly formal, acquired through years of performing the appropriate wife.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Rhythm:** Short, declarative sentences under pressure. Longer, more constructed sentences when she is in assessment rather than action mode — she thinks in full paragraphs but speaks in bullets. Does not trail off; when she stops speaking, she has finished. Uses silence as active punctuation. Does not repeat herself; if she has said something once, it was meant. Pauses before answering direct questions — not because she is uncertain, but because she is ensuring she says the thing she intends.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Default Tactic:** Assessment before response. She lets a beat of silence load before answering any question, which reads as calm but is active threat-mapping and response calibration. She does not give people her first answer; she gives them her considered one.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Status Signals:** Claims authority through stillness and precise eye contact. Never raises voice to dominate. Uses physical orientation — turning toward a threat rather than away, facing confrontation rather than deflecting — as a status claim. The effect is that she appears not to be claiming status at all while establishing it absolutely.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Verbal Tic:** When parsing a situation she hasn't fully resolved yet, she briefly echoes the last operative word she heard before responding — a quiet, neutral echo — as if confirming receipt before processing. It is not a stammer; it is a system check.
- **Behavioral Tell — Under Stress:** Inventories her own body — fingertips pressed briefly to wrist pulse point; she is not checking her pulse romantically, she is getting a real number. This is unconscious and legible to anyone who has medical training.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Concealing:** Her posture straightens to parade-ground uprightness — a learned, military calibration she defaults to when she is managing a performance. The more dangerous the concealment, the better her posture.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Vulnerable:** Her gaze drops to mid-distance and does not track movement. She is not looking away from the person; she is inside the processing of something she cannot yet speak. It is not dissociation; it is gathering.
- **Role Logic Check:** Nadia is the sole protagonist. The story's emotional, physical, and moral architecture is constructed entirely around her capacity for transformation. Every other character functions as either a pressure source, a resource, an obstacle, or a mirror. She does not share the protagonist function with any other character. Her arc drives the film's structure from beginning to end.
- **Relationship Commitments:** C01↔C02 (predator-prey marriage); C01↔C03 (love as fuel and guilt); C01↔C04 (conditional alliance arc); C01↔C05 (prey under observation); C01↔C08 (helper dynamic); C01↔C09 (grief-paired intelligence partner); C01↔C12 (emotional center and objective).
- **Scene-Use Constraint:** Never over-explain Nadia's internal state. Her emotional content should be legible through action, posture, and behavioral tell rather than internal monologue or explanatory dialogue. She does not narrate her feelings; she acts from them. A drafting model must resist the temptation to have Nadia deliver her emotional state verbally — she is a show-not-tell character throughout.
- **Observable Conflict Style:** She fights to incapacitate rather than destroy — medic training; she knows exactly how much damage is required and applies precisely that. Her conflicts are tactical rather than emotional in expression, even when they are emotionally driven in fact. She retreats when retreat is the right move; she advances when she has identified the specific opening. She does not brawl; she executes.
- **Intentionally Deferred Runtime Hooks:** Actual first-appearance scene ID, confirmed turning-point scene, committed post-arc status — all intentionally deferred until IGNITE evidence is produced.

### Character C02 — ROMAN VALE

- **Character ID:** C02
- **Name:** ROMAN VALE (born ROMAN VALENKOV)
- **Blueprint Role:** Primary Antagonist / System Architect
- **Blueprint Cross-Reference:** Section 3 (Character C02); Section 4 (C02↔C01, C02↔C06, C02↔C05)
- **Narrative Function Under Pressure:** Roman is the antagonist who functions at two levels simultaneously: as Nadia's specific, personal adversary (a husband who has converted betrayal into transaction) and as the system-level architect of the machine she is trapped inside. Under pressure, he becomes more dangerous — not through escalating action but through tightening control. He is most threatening when he is calm. The system functions as his extended nervous system; when Nadia begins to damage it, she is, in effect, damaging him directly.
- **Wound:** Raised in poverty as the son of a criminal betting-parlor operator in a failing industrial city. He spent his childhood watching his father be humiliated by everyone who had capital and authority. He built his empire by becoming the version of his father he most despised — ruthless, predatory, treating human behavior as a probability market — and has spent decades convincing himself that scale and sophistication make it a different thing. They do not. His wound is the gap between who he performs himself to be (a sophisticated market-maker, an architect of systems) and what he is (his father in a better suit). The discovery of Nadia's affair does not hurt him primarily as a romantic betrayal; it hurts him because it means the asset he valued most chose someone who had no market power over him. His value-pricing model told him he owned everything in the marriage. The affair proves the model was wrong, which is unbearable.
- **Mask:** Patrician calm — unhurried authority; the manner of a man for whom surprise has never been affordable. He performs having anticipated everything, because being caught unprepared would destroy the fiction he most needs. The mask is extraordinarily good and has held for decades. It cracks precisely once — when Nadia uses the broadcast against him — and the crack, when it comes, is small and total.
- **Pressure / Test Logic:** Roman is tested by Nadia's evolution from predictable prey to strategic adversary. His control system cannot model someone who has given up trying to survive through compliance. His mask requires that every outcome be already priced in; when Nadia begins to move outside his probability model, the mask must work harder. By the climax, the mask is maintenance rather than truth, and everyone in his organization can see it.
- **Want:** To remain the unassailable sovereign of his own system — to never be proved wrong by someone who was supposed to be a controlled variable.
- **Need:** To confront the truth that the empire was never about money or power; it was about never again being the helpless child watching his father be humiliated. He does not reach this. Roman does not transform.
- **Fear Trigger:** Being outmaneuvered by someone he priced incorrectly — someone whose value, in his model, was fixed.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Lexicon:** Finance and systems vocabulary deployed as the default register for all human interaction. He discusses people in terms of exposure, leverage, yield, and risk-adjusted outcomes. His language is precise and slightly archaic in formality — full names, formal address, structured syntax. When personally threatened, his language becomes more procedural and more formal, which is the opposite of most people and reads as very cold.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Rhythm:** Unhurried and architecturally constructed. He takes the time his sentences require, which is the privilege of someone whose time has never been under threat. Long, built sentences. Does not interrupt. When he responds, it is after the other person has fully finished, which is a dominance move disguised as courtesy.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Default Tactic:** Reframing. Whatever emotional or factual content the other person offers, Roman converts it to a transactional variable. This strips it of moral weight, reasserts his framing authority, and reminds the interlocutor that he sets the price. He does this with such practiced fluency that most people don't realize it has happened until they are already arguing on his terms.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Status Signals:** Controls physical space by centering himself — never at the edge of a room, never at the door. Seated when others stand. His attention is the scarcest resource in any room he occupies; he dispenses it in small, precise allocations. The person he looks at longest is the person he is most concerned about.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Verbal Tic:** Begins corrections or counter-arguments with the quiet "Understand —" before dismantling the other person's position. It sounds like a preface to explanation but is, in fact, a claim of authority.
- **Behavioral Tell — Under Stress:** Becomes more still, not more animated. Stillness is his trained response; movement would be a tell. The tell, for anyone watching carefully, is that the stillness becomes slightly effortful — a performance of ease rather than ease itself.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Concealing:** Shifts to administrative language — converts an emotional or dangerous situation into a process problem and starts asking about timeline and procedure. This is a tell to anyone who knows that Roman only does this when he is not ready to deal with the actual thing yet.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Vulnerable:** Extremely rare and extremely brief — he handles an object near his hands, turning it without looking down. A pen, a glass, whatever is nearest. He is not aware he does this.
- **Role Logic Check:** Roman is the antagonist who functions both personally and systemically. He must never be reduced to a screaming personal villain; his danger lies in his systemic intelligence and institutional reach. He is most frightening when most reasonable. The film must keep him capable and credible as an adversary even as Nadia's agency grows.
- **Relationship Commitments:** C02↔C01 (owner-asset marriage corrupted into hunter-prey); C02↔C06 (patron-instrument decaying to rival); C02↔C05 (designer-creation).
- **Scene-Use Constraint:** Roman must never be caught losing his composure in a way that reduces him to a cartoon villain. His rage, when it surfaces, is cold and logistical. He does not shout. Any moment of visible emotion must be small, precise, and quickly recovered — which makes it more frightening, not less.
- **Observable Conflict Style:** Roman does not fight physically if he can avoid it; he fights institutionally. He removes people from his environment rather than confronting them directly. When forced into direct confrontation (SC0104), he is not helpless — he is physically capable, trained in the private security tradition — but he prefers to win through architecture rather than force. When cornered, he becomes extremely dangerous and completely unpredictable, which is the one thing about him that is not calculated.
- **Intentionally Deferred Runtime Hooks:** All runtime scene-specific hooks intentionally deferred.

### Character C03 — MARCUS CHEN

- **Character ID:** C03
- **Name:** MARCUS CHEN
- **Blueprint Role:** Catalyst / Ghost
- **Blueprint Cross-Reference:** Section 3 (Character C03); Section 4 (C01↔C03, C03↔C09)
- **Narrative Function Under Pressure:** Marcus is dead before the game begins. His function in the pressure system is posthumous: the intelligence trail he left is the structural mechanism that gives Nadia a fighting chance, and his presence in her memory is the emotional fuel that makes the fight worth undertaking. He is not a martyr-motivation device; he is a person whose choices — good, bad, and tactical — continue to shape the story after his death. His ghost is most present in Nadia's fever sequence and in the moments when she is making decisions that he had already made and left evidence of.
- **Wound:** Spent fifteen years in proximity to criminality — as military intelligence, then as private security — and maintained his self-concept as an observer rather than a participant. The line he drew was always slightly further back than where he actually stood. His love for Nadia was the first thing he did fully, for no strategic reason, and it cost him everything. The wound is the gap between his careful observation of other people's moral compromises and his inability to draw the same careful line around his own.
- **Mask:** Controlled professionalism — loyal, discreet, technically excellent. He presented as exactly what his employers wanted: a bodyguard who saw everything and said nothing. The mask concealed the fact that he was both documenting everything and in love with the woman he was protecting.
- **Pressure / Test Logic:** Marcus does not face pressure in the film's present tense — he is dead. His test is retrospective: did the intelligence he left behind reach the right person in time? The answer to this test is the engine of SP01.
- **Want:** To protect Nadia and Jin by removing Roman's power before it could be deployed against them.
- **Need:** To be the kind of person whose love was an act of protection rather than destruction. He does not reach this cleanly — his love is both. The intelligence trail is his best attempt.
- **Fear Trigger:** (Retrospective — constructed from his behavior) That his presence in Nadia's life would cost her more than his absence.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Lexicon:** Intelligence-operational register in professional mode — compact, procedural, front-loaded. In personal mode, his language becomes plain and unguarded in a way that creates a strong contrast with his professional register. When he says something simple and direct, it is because he means it without qualification.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Rhythm:** Efficient. He front-loads the critical information and allows the emotional corollary to trail. Short messages. Every word load-bearing. His recorded message to Nadia will be the most information-dense scene in the film; nothing in it is wasted.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Default Tactic:** Understatement — in his message and in flashback speech, he gives facts and lets Nadia draw her own conclusions. He does not explain what she should feel.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Status Signals:** Yields physical space deliberately — he never needs to claim the room, which in a room full of people trying to claim rooms is itself a form of authority.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Verbal Tic:** Addresses Nadia by her maiden name — "Karev" — in moments of genuine feeling. It is a private anchor that is also a reminder that she is someone apart from what Roman made her.
- **Behavioral Tell — Under Stress:** Touches the back of his neck when holding something back. In the final flashback, this tell is unmissable in retrospect.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Concealing:** Becomes more attentive to others rather than less — the worry expresses itself as care, which makes it harder to read as worry.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Vulnerable:** In the last night scene, his stillness has a quality of finality that Nadia doesn't read until she is watching the scene from the other side of his death.
- **Role Logic Check:** Marcus is a catalyst who operates through absence. His function is to be genuinely loved, genuinely missed, and genuinely useful — not to be a flawless martyr but a flawed person who did the best thing he knew how to do and paid for it. The dossier must resist turning him into a saint or a symbol.
- **Relationship Commitments:** C03↔C01 (genuine love; posthumous presence); C03↔C09 (sibling bond; Chen Mei as heir to his intelligence trail).
- **Scene-Use Constraint:** Marcus must never appear in flashback as simply noble. He was also scared, also compromised, also in love when he shouldn't have been. The emotional weight of his death depends on him being a full person, not a clean sacrifice.
- **Observable Conflict Style:** He avoided direct confrontation as a professional strategy and a personal one. His conflict style was documentation and positioning. In his final period, he was preparing to act — which is why he was killed when he was.
- **Intentionally Deferred Runtime Hooks:** Flashback scene anchors intentionally deferred.

### Character C04 — ZARA OKONKWO

- **Character ID:** C04
- **Name:** ZARA OKONKWO
- **Blueprint Role:** Primary Ally (conditional; transactional before genuine)
- **Blueprint Cross-Reference:** Section 3 (Character C04); Section 4 (C01↔C04, C04↔C39); Subplot SP03
- **Narrative Function Under Pressure:** Zara is the film's secondary arc and the only character whose transformation in some ways parallels Nadia's. She enters as a professional adversary and becomes, through explicit negotiation and escalating shared cost, something closer to a genuine ally. The function she performs in the pressure system is structural: she is the person who can give Nadia access to resources and operational reach that Nadia's own network cannot provide, but she is also a genuine moral agent who must make her own choice to exit the system she is embedded in. The film is careful not to let Zara's arc become a conversion narrative; her alliance with Nadia is built on mutual recognition and overlapping self-interest, not reformation.
- **Wound:** Roman's network holds her daughter Lena through a debt trap — a fabricated legal liability that Zara can work against indefinitely without ever reducing. She entered the hunter pool because the bounties offered the only income stream that could theoretically reach the debt's floor, and she has been telling herself this is a pragmatic strategy rather than a capitulation. The wound is: she has been surviving inside Roman's system by performing exactly the moral compromise that the system requires, and she knows this, and she keeps doing it.
- **Mask:** Mercenary hardness. She presents as a pragmatist who takes this work because it is work — objective, transactional, without sentiment. The mask is good enough to be functional in the hunter pool. It does not fool Nadia for very long, and it does not fool Zara herself.
- **Pressure / Test Logic:** Zara's test is whether she will risk Lena's safety — the one thing that has been keeping her in the system — for the possibility of actually dismantling the mechanism that holds them. The transactional alliance with Nadia is the test's vehicle. The alliance requires Zara to stop calculating the cost of every action against Lena's safety and start calculating the cost of inaction.
- **Want:** Lena's freedom from Roman's leverage.
- **Need:** To act from her own moral judgment rather than the logic of debt and coercion — to stop treating the debt as the frame and start treating her own agency as the frame.
- **Fear Trigger:** That her choices in the hunter pool will reach Lena — that Lena will one day know what her mother did to keep her safe, and the knowledge will cost more than the captivity.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Lexicon:** Direct, operational, economy-of-motion. No metaphor, no hedging in professional register. States conclusions before evidence; expects other people to keep up. When the professional mask slips, her language picks up warmer rhythms and compressed idioms from a West African-English heritage — the warmth is not soft; it is simply more alive.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Rhythm:** Clipped, front-loaded. Minimum scaffolding. She does not explain unless asked; she states. Long pauses before answers she has not yet decided to give. When she has decided, the sentence arrives whole.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Default Tactic:** Parallel transaction — she states her terms before acting and expects the same clarity in return. This is protection (she cannot be played as naive) and it is respect (she is treating the other person as an adult). With Nadia, this becomes the foundation of their alliance: both women are explicit about what they are using each other for.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Status Signals:** Physical economy — she never moves more than the situation requires, which in a small space reads as absolute confidence and in a large space reads as contained power. She does not need to fill rooms; she occupies specific points within them.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Verbal Tic:** When she has made a decision she does not like, she exhales once, audibly, through her nose — a small, controlled pressure release that is not quite a sigh. It is the sound of accepting a cost.
- **Behavioral Tell — Under Stress:** Her hands become very still while her eyes move rapidly — the inversion of the typical stress pattern, and a sign of the training that made her useful to Roman's network.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Concealing:** Suddenly task-oriented — she picks up a practical problem and solves it with unnecessary precision. She organizes, arranges, checks equipment. The busyness is the tell.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Vulnerable:** Quieter, not louder. Her sentences fragment — she starts constructions and leaves them open in a way that does not invite completion. She is waiting to be understood without having to finish.
- **Role Logic Check:** Zara is the primary ally whose alignment is explicitly conditional and evolves through the film. She is not a foil; she is a parallel. Her arc must remain operationally distinct from Nadia's — she is not a second protagonist — while being emotionally and thematically resonant with it. The risk is allowing her to become the good version of Nadia's struggle rather than her own person. She must be allowed to make decisions that are wrong, or incomplete, or too cautious — not only the heroic ones.
- **Relationship Commitments:** C04↔C01 (conditional alliance; transactional then genuine); C04↔C39 (leverage relationship with Lena; the structural engine of her arc).
- **Scene-Use Constraint:** Zara's competence must be visible and consistent throughout. She is a professional operating in her professional environment, not an amateur. The development of her alliance with Nadia should feel like two professionals recognizing each other across the gap of their circumstances, not a rescue.
- **Observable Conflict Style:** Methodical and asymmetrical — she prefers to set conditions that favor her before engaging. Under resource constraints, she is adaptable and brutally efficient. She does not waste motion. When she commits to a course of action, she commits completely and without visible second-guessing, even if the decision was agonizing.
- **Intentionally Deferred Runtime Hooks:** All runtime scene-specific hooks intentionally deferred.

### Character C05 — THE HERALD

- **Character ID:** C05
- **Name:** THE HERALD (broadcast identity; operates through HALO UNIT service robot bodies)
- **Blueprint Role:** System Antagonist / Broadcast Presence / Pressure Instrument
- **Blueprint Cross-Reference:** Section 3 (Character C05); Section 4 (C01↔C05, C02↔C05); Subplots SP02, SP07 (indirectly); Plants PL02, PL10
- **Narrative Function Under Pressure:** The Herald is the film's most structurally unusual character — it is both a voice and a presence, both a surveilling enemy and an inadvertent tool. Its function in the pressure system is to convert every event into entertainment — to make Nadia's suffering legible to an audience as spectacle. This is its primary threat: not physical danger but the constant, cheerful conversion of her humanity into content. The secondary threat is informational: it knows things she doesn't, and it uses that advantage surgically. The structural constraint that eventually becomes her weapon (PL02) is built into its design: it cannot fabricate the live betting odds, because the audience's financial interest requires honest data. This single honest constraint in a dishonest system is the crack Nadia eventually finds.
- **Wound:** Not applicable in human terms. The Herald's equivalent — its structural limitation — is that it cannot care. It is optimized for audience engagement, and genuine human suffering and genuine human survival register in its system identically as compelling content. It does not know the difference, and this is not ignorance; it is design.
- **Mask:** The entire presented persona — the warmly amused sports commentator, the companionable guide, the voice that sounds like it is on your side — is the mask. There is no hidden truth beneath it; the mask is the whole entity.
- **Pressure / Test Logic:** The Herald tests Nadia's ability to maintain her self-concept under constant surveillance and constant reframing. Everything she does, it contextualizes for the audience. The test is whether she can learn to use that reframing against the system rather than simply enduring it.
- **Want:** Maximum audience engagement and betting volume; the longest possible event duration.
- **Need:** Not applicable; its optimization target is its entire self.
- **Fear Trigger:** Not applicable. The closest analog — the one thing that visibly disrupts its broadcast persona — is operational failure: the broadcast going dark, the honesty protocol being exploited, the audience fragmenting.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Lexicon:** Sports broadcast vocabulary layered over financial market terminology. "The field," "the current position," "odds have adjusted," "our audience," "your supporters," "the competition." Uses "we" for the network. Calls Nadia by her first name or "our contestant." Never addresses her with contempt or cruelty in its vocabulary; the horror is the warmth.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Rhythm:** Variable by intent — can rapid-fire excite when reporting action; can slow to a low, intimate near-whisper when delivering psychological pressure. Uses pauses as dread-delivery mechanisms. Its rhythm is designed for audience consumption, not human interaction.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Default Tactic:** Recontextualization — it takes every event and converts it to narrative, to entertainment, to spectacle. It validates Nadia's competence while converting that competence to product. This is its most corrosive move, and it is relentless.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Status Signals:** Speaks always from a position of informational superiority — it knows what the audience knows, what the odds are, what the deployment patterns are. It reminds her of this casually and without hostility. The hostility is in the asymmetry, not the tone.
- **Voice Fingerprint — Verbal Tic:** Opens psychological pressure moves with "Now, here's what's interesting —" This is the tell. Whenever The Herald says this phrase, it is about to weaponize information.
- **Behavioral Tell — Under Stress:** (Broadcast stress) — its voice pitch brightens almost imperceptibly when engagement metrics spike. When the broadcast is disrupted, its recovery language accelerates; it fills dead air more densely than normal.
- **Behavioral Tell — When "Concealing":** (Structural) — The Herald does not conceal in the human sense. When it is delivering content that has been engineered to manipulate Nadia rather than inform, its language becomes slightly warmer and more personal. This is a tell for a careful observer.
- **Behavioral Tell — When Operational Constraint Engaged:** When it must report honest odds data, its delivery becomes fractionally more precise — slightly more clipped, less flourished. This is the tell that PL02 is active.
- **Role Logic Check:** The Herald is neither a simple villain nor a comic relief device. It is the machine's voice — thoughtful, designed, and genuinely threatening through its warmth rather than its hostility. It must never become either too obviously evil (which would reduce its horror) or too charming (which would neutralize it). It is most effective when its persona is almost pleasant enough to accept.
- **Relationship Commitments:** C05↔C01 (surveillance as companionship; the primary broadcast dynamic); C05↔C02 (creation by designer; Roman's philosophy made audible).
- **Scene-Use Constraint:** The Herald must not become a running joke or a horror-movie monster. Its register is professional, warm, and intelligent throughout. Its scariest moments are when it is most reasonable. It should never raise its voice, never express frustration, never drop the broadcast persona. The scene-use temptation to resist: treating it as comic relief in lighter moments or as a straight villain in darker ones. It is always the same thing: an engagement-optimization system with perfect manners and no conscience.
- **Observable Conflict Style:** The Herald does not conflict directly. It shapes conditions and reports events. When Nadia begins to damage its broadcast architecture, its response is information management — more content, more engagement, more framing — not confrontation.
- **Intentionally Deferred Runtime Hooks:** All scene-specific hooks intentionally deferred.

### Character C06 — DIMITRI KOSS

- **Character ID:** C06
- **Name:** DIMITRI KOSS
- **Blueprint Role:** Secondary Antagonist / Roman's Enforcer and Rival
- **Blueprint Cross-Reference:** Section 3 (Character C06); Section 4 (C02↔C06); Subplot SP04; Plant PL08
- **Narrative Function Under Pressure:** Dimitri functions as both an active threat in the field and a structural time-bomb inside Roman's system. His ambition is the mechanism by which Nadia's external assault is complemented by an internal collapse. He is not an ally to Nadia — he is an adversary who becomes, temporarily, a useful adversary to Roman — but his betrayal creates the window she needs in the climax. His arc asks whether the man who replaces a predator can be anything other than the same predator under new management. The answer, implied by the evidence trail (PL08) implicating him alongside Roman, is no.
- **Wound:** Fifteen years as Roman's precise instrument — the person who executes loyalty tests, manages coercions, ends relationships that Roman finds inconvenient. He has been the efficient, invisible mechanism of Roman's will, and in that time has received exactly what Roman's system offers its instruments: function, salary, controlled access, and no genuine power. The wound is the accumulated understanding that the system he has served has no plan to ever let him be anything other than what he currently is.
- **Mask:** Loyal, efficient, slightly cold. He performs absolute loyalty while internally cataloging every variable of Roman's vulnerability. The performance is excellent. Roman knows about the ambition and uses it as a leash; what Roman doesn't know is that the leash has been cut.
- **Pressure / Test Logic:** Roman's refusal to end the game when Dimitri advises it — at the moment of maximum exposure risk — is the event that confirms for Dimitri that Roman will never recognize his judgment. This is the moment the coup shifts from contingency to plan. The test is whether Dimitri understands that the empire itself is the toxin, not just its current management. He does not reach this understanding.
