## Title and scope note

This document defines the recurring location system of `CLOSING PRICE` using screenplay-grounded evidence. It is a human-readable source bible for later planning, scene-card refinement, prompt writing, and visual canonization. It does not create new `LOC###` IDs. Downstream planning records may map these locations later, but this document remains source-authoritative at the naming and identity level.

## Location-system overview

The project's location system divides into four broad families:
- Controlled domestic space: Roman's wealth-world, where curation and surveillance overlap.
- Institutional/operational space: offices, control rooms, newsrooms, safe houses, and legal/investigative environments.
- Industrial/transit space: docks, terminals, depots, freight corridors, drainage paths, staff rooms, approach roads.
- Broadcast non-place: ORACLE PRIME's machine-facing visual layer and subscriber feed environments.

Across all families, space is action-relevant. Doors, corridors, gates, loading areas, windows, and service routes carry story function.

## Naming policy for locations and aliases

- Preserve the dominant label used by the screenplay slugline when one is clear.
- If several sluglines clearly describe subspaces of one larger environment, treat the larger environment as the dominant location and record subspaces as aliases or component zones.
- Do not invent source-level location IDs.
- Keep city names attached when the location identity depends on city context.
- If a scene opens on an inferred/dash boundary and only later reveals a specific slugline, record that alias as a subordinate evidence note rather than a hard source-origin label.

## Domestic vs institutional vs industrial location categories

- **Domestic:** Vale Residence; Keston's residence; Jin's care spaces inside the compound
- **Institutional:** Roman's offices; ORACLE PRIME ops rooms; newsroom/legal offices; VELTAIN technical/control rooms
- **Industrial / transit:** Outer Districts, ZORAL depots, MERIN waterfront, KASPAR TERMINAL, freight corridors, loading bays, service roads

Operational distinction:
- Domestic spaces reveal what a person protects or controls.
- Institutional spaces reveal hierarchy, process, and information ownership.
- Industrial/transit spaces reveal exposure, labor, improvisation, and route logic.
