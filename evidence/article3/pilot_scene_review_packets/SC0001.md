## Excerpt

```fountain
INT. VALE RESIDENCE — VARDOVA — KITCHEN PASSAGE — EARLY MORNING

The kitchen is expensive and underused. Pale stone surfaces. A single unwashed cup beside the sink — Nadia's.

NADIA moves through the passage with the economy of someone who has mapped every room for exits. Not rushed. Not nervous. Just precise.

She fills a glass of water, drinks half, sets it exactly where she found it.

Her left wrist turns as she sets the glass down. A thin pale scar crosses the underside — surgical-clean, old, not decorative. She doesn't look at it.

She opens a cabinet. Inventories its contents the way a person inventories a kit.

INT. VALE RESIDENCE — EAST-WING CORRIDOR — CONTINUOUS

The corridor is wide. Corporate art on the walls — purchased, not chosen. Nadia walks it like a route she has timed.

BIRTA, seventies, compact, moves toward her from the far end — a tray of folded linens balanced with the authority of a woman who has carried things her whole life.

BIRTA
You didn't sleep again.

NADIA
I slept.

BIRTA
You slept the way you fold laundry. Very neat. Not restful.

Nadia almost smiles. Not quite.

NADIA
Jin's formula. There's a new tin in the second shelf, behind the vitamin drops.

BIRTA
I found it. He's still down. Your husband —

She stops herself. Adjusts the linens.

BIRTA (CONT'D)
Mr. Vale called from the car. He said not to wait on breakfast.

NADIA
He won't be back before eight.

She says it without looking at Birta. She already knew.

BIRTA
I'll leave something covered.

Nadia nods. They pass each other. Birta continues down the corridor. Nadia stops at the third framed photograph — a landscape, VARDOVA skyline at dusk.

She notices the angle.

Off. By three or four degrees. She would not have been able to say why she knew the angle, but she knows.

Her fingertips touch the frame. She straightens it.

The wall-mount's dust-shadow is slightly larger than the frame now sitting over it. Someone moved this. Recently. And replaced it not quite right.

Her fingers stay on the frame for one beat longer than the task requires.

Then she lets go and walks on.

Her pace doesn't change. Her posture is impeccable. She touches her fingertips briefly to the inside of her own wrist as she rounds the corner — pulse-check, two seconds, automatic — and continues toward Jin's room.

INT. VALE RESIDENCE — JIN'S ROOM — CONTINUOUS

The room is warm and dim. A white-noise machine hums.

Nadia stands in the doorway. Doesn't enter. JIN sleeps in his crib, eight months, face turned sideways, one hand open.

She watches him the way she watched the cabinet. Taking inventory of what is present. What is safe. What is still where it should be.

She stays there thirty seconds.

Then she pulls the door almost closed and goes to get dressed.

END SCENE
```
